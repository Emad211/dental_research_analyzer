### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

**Study Characteristics:**
*   **Study Design:** Prospective, single-blind (patients blinded), randomized clinical trial (RCT).
*   **Objectives:** 
    1. Assess pain perception, pain medication use, and filling material extrusion in single-visit root canal treatments with or without Foraminal Enlargement (FE).
    2. Investigate the correlation between sealer extrusion and postoperative pain.
*   **Study Groups:** Two independent groups:
    1. Foraminal Patency (FP) group (Working length 1 mm short of foramen).
    2. Foraminal Enlargement (FE) group (Working length at the foramen level).
*   **Sample Size:** 140 patients total (70 per group, accounting for 1 potential dropout per group based on a priori power calculation).
*   **Inclusion Criteria:** 19–55 years old; asymptomatic necrotic teeth; single, fully formed, straight root canal; max probing depth 3 mm; ASA I health status.
*   **Exclusion Criteria:** Chronic/acute systemic disease; antibiotics/painkillers in the last 10 days; swelling, sinus tract, root resorption, or previous endodontic treatment.

**Variables and Measurements:**
*   **Independent Variable:** Instrumentation technique (Categorical: FE vs. FP).
*   **Dependent Variable 1 (Pain):** Measured via modified Visual Analog Scale (VAS) from 0-10. Categorized into: no pain (0), mild (1–3), moderate (4–7), and severe (8–10). Measured at 4 time points: 24, 48, 72 hours, and 1 week. (Ordinal data).
*   **Dependent Variable 2 (Medication):** Number of 400mg ibuprofen tablets taken. (Discrete/Count data).
*   **Dependent Variable 3 (Sealer Extrusion):** Assessed via postoperative periapical radiograph. (Categorical/Dichotomous data: Yes/No).
*   **Data Collection Method:** Phone interviews by a blinded dental assistant at the specified time points; radiographic assessment by endodontists.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY (Core tests required to answer primary objectives)

**1. Mann-Whitney U Test**
*   **Purpose:** To compare postoperative pain scores (VAS) and the number of pain relief tablets taken between the FE and FP groups at each specific time point.
*   **Why appropriate:** 1) Pain scores (especially when categorized) and pill counts are non-normally distributed ordinal/discrete data. 2) The study compares two independent groups.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It does not account for the repeated-measures nature of the data across the four time points simultaneously.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When comparing paired or matched data (e.g., the same patient before and after treatment).
*   **Alternative:** Independent Samples t-test (only if the data surprisingly meets the assumption of strict normality, which is highly unlikely for VAS pain scores).

**2. Chi-Square Test of Independence**
*   **Purpose:** To compare the incidence rates of sealer extrusion (Yes/No) between the FE and FP groups.
*   **Why appropriate:** 1) Both variables (Group and Extrusion) are categorical. 2) It effectively determines if the proportion of extrusion differs significantly between the two independent groups.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** If the overall incidence of extrusion is very low, resulting in expected cell frequencies of less than 5.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When analyzing continuous or ordinal dependent variables.
*   **Alternative:** Fisher’s Exact Test (if expected cell counts in the contingency table are < 5).

**3. Spearman’s Rank-Order Correlation**
*   **Purpose:** To evaluate the correlation between sealer extrusion (binary) and postoperative pain intensity (ordinal).
*   **Why appropriate:** 1) It does not assume a linear relationship or normal distribution. 2) It is perfectly suited for correlating a dichotomous variable with an ordinal variable (categorized pain scores).
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It treats the binary variable as ranks, which can sometimes limit statistical power compared to specialized tests.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When both variables are strictly continuous, normally distributed, and share a linear relationship.
*   **Alternative:** Point-biserial correlation (if the raw 0-10 VAS scores are treated as continuous rather than ordinal categories).

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED (Enhances quality and rigor)

**1. Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) or Mixed-Effects Ordinal Logistic Regression**
*   **Purpose:** To analyze the longitudinal effect of the instrumentation technique on pain over the four time points (24h, 48h, 72h, 1 week).
*   **Why appropriate:** 1) It accounts for the within-subject correlation (the same patient is measured 4 times). 2) It handles ordinal dependent variables (the 4 pain categories) and missing data (dropouts) robustly.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It requires a relatively large sample size to converge properly and can be complex to interpret.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** For simple cross-sectional data where subjects are only measured once.
*   **Alternative:** Friedman Test (though this only looks at changes within a single group over time and cannot compare the FE and FP groups simultaneously).

**2. Independent t-test and Chi-Square (Baseline Demographics)**
*   **Purpose:** To compare baseline characteristics (e.g., age, sex, tooth type) between the FE and FP groups.
*   **Why appropriate:** 1) Ensures that the randomization process was successful. 2) Confirms that no confounding demographic variables are skewing the pain results.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** If demographic data violates normality assumptions (for age).
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** To analyze the primary outcome variables of the study.
*   **Alternative:** Mann-Whitney U test (for age, if non-normally distributed).

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL (Supplementary insights)

**1. Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis**
*   **Purpose:** To evaluate the "time to pain resolution" (time until a patient reports a pain score of 0) between the two groups.
*   **Why appropriate:** 1) It handles time-to-event data perfectly. 2) It accounts for right-censored data (patients who still have pain at 1 week).
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** The time intervals are wide and discrete (24h, 48h, 72h, 1 week), which reduces the precision of the "survival" curve.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When the outcome is a continuous measure rather than a defined "event" (like pain cessation).
*   **Alternative:** Cox Proportional-Hazards Model (if the researchers want to adjust for covariates like age or sealer extrusion).

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS

*   **Repeated Measures ANOVA:** Should **NOT** be used to analyze the pain scores over time. Pain is measured on a VAS and explicitly grouped into ordinal categories (0, 1-3, 4-7, 8-10). ANOVA assumes continuous, normally distributed interval data. Using it on skewed, ordinal pain data will yield invalid results. *(Note: The authors mention using ANOVA for sample size calculation, which is mathematically unusual for a 2-group comparison, where a t-test formula is standard).*
*   **Pearson Correlation:** Should **NOT** be used to correlate sealer extrusion and pain. Pearson requires two continuous, normally distributed variables. Extrusion is binary (yes/no) and pain is ordinal.
*   **Paired Samples t-test / Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test (for between-group analysis):** Should **NOT** be used to compare the FE group to the FP group. These tests are for paired/matched data, whereas this study utilizes two independent groups of different patients.

### TASK 4: SUMMARY

The overall analytical approach must be driven by the non-parametric nature of the primary outcomes. Because pain is subjective, measured on a bounded scale (0-10), and explicitly categorized into ordinal ranks, parametric tests (like ANOVA or t-tests) are invalid for the primary analysis. The core strategy relies on **Mann-Whitney U tests** for point-by-point group comparisons and **Chi-Square/Fisher's Exact tests** for binary outcomes like sealer extrusion. To elevate the statistical rigor, a longitudinal model such as **GEE** is highly recommended to evaluate the trajectory of pain over the 7-day period while accounting for the repeated measures inherent in the study design. Finally, non-parametric correlation (Spearman's) will safely and accurately assess the relationship between the categorical extrusion data and ordinal pain scores.
