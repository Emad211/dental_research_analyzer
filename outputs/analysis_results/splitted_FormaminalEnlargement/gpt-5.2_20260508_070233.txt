## TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION (study characteristics)

### Study identification
- **Title/topic:** Effect of foraminal enlargement (FE) vs foraminal patency (FP) on **postoperative pain**, **analgesic intake**, and **sealer extrusion** after single-visit root canal treatment.
- **Design:** **Prospective, parallel-group randomized clinical trial** (CONSORT stated).
- **Setting:** Private practice clinical setting (Brazil), Feb 2019–Jan 2020.
- **Ethics:** Approved (protocol #2.913.225). Written informed consent.

### Objectives / hypotheses
- **Primary aim (implied):** Compare **postoperative pain perception** between FE vs FP after single-visit endodontic treatment.
- **Secondary aims:** Compare **use of pain relief medication** and **sealer extrusion** between groups.
- **Exploratory aim:** Assess **correlation between sealer extrusion and postoperative pain**.
- **Null hypothesis:** No differences between FE and FP for assessed variables.

### Participants
- **Age:** 19–55 years.
- **Inclusion:** Asymptomatic necrotic **single-rooted**, straight canal; with/without apical radiolucency; periodontal probing depth ≤3 mm; ASA I.
- **Exclusion:** Systemic disease; antibiotics/analgesics in last 10 days; swelling/sinus tract; internal/external resorption; previous endodontic treatment; intra-operative bleeding (would exclude; none occurred).

### Randomization / masking
- **Randomization method:** Patient-selected **sealed envelope** allocation to FP or FE.
- **Blinding:** **Single-blind** (likely patient blinded; operator not).
- **Groups:**
  - **FP group:** Working length (WL) **1 mm short of foramen**.
  - **FE group:** WL **at foramen** (0.0 reading on apex locator).

### Interventions (standardized protocol)
- **Anesthesia:** 3.6 mL 2% lidocaine + epinephrine 1:100,000.
- **Instrumentation system:** WaveOne Gold reciprocating; X-Smart Plus motor.
- **Irrigation:** NaOCl 2.5% throughout; final irrigation with ultrasonic activation; EDTA 17%; total **25 mL NaOCl** per tooth.
- **Apical size:** Prepared to **25/.07** (“Primary”) or **45/.05** (“Large”) depending on initial apical diameter.
- **Obturation:** Single-cone gutta-percha + **AH Plus** sealer; temporary seal Coltosol + glass ionomer.
- **Radiograph:** Postoperative periapical radiograph for **sealer extrusion** assessment.
- **Operators:** Two experienced endodontists; checklist; microscope 10×.

### Outcomes and measurements
1. **Postoperative pain intensity** (repeated over time)
   - Instrument: **modified VAS 0–10** with faces.
   - Time points: **24h, 48h, 72h, 1 week**.
   - Recorded by patient; collected via phone by assistant; entered into spreadsheet.
   - Pain also categorized: none (0), mild (1–3), moderate (4–7), severe (8–10).
2. **Analgesic use**
   - Rescue medication: **ibuprofen 400 mg q6h PRN**.
   - At each follow-up, if any pain reported: asked **whether taken** and **number of tablets**.
3. **Sealer extrusion**
   - Determined from **postoperative periapical radiograph** (binary yes/no is implied; degree not specified).

### Sample size
- **Planned:** 69 per group based on ANOVA assumptions (difference in means 0.03, SD 0.05, power 0.80, alpha 0.05).
- **Enrolled:** **70 per group** (total **N=140**), allowing ~1 loss to follow-up/group (actual attrition not provided here).

### Key variables/covariates available from text (potential confounders/moderators)
- Age, sex (mentioned in intro as pain-related factors but not stated as collected; likely available).
- Tooth location (maxillary/mandibular via anesthesia type; may be recorded).
- Presence of apical radiolucency (included).
- Final apical preparation size (Primary vs Large).
- Baseline pain: all **asymptomatic** (baseline pain essentially 0 by design).

# CATEGORY A – MANDATORY (core analyses)

## A1) Linear mixed-effects model (LMM) for repeated pain scores
- **Purpose:** Test whether **pain trajectories over time differ** between FE and FP.
- **Why appropriate (≥2):**
  1. Pain is measured **repeatedly** within the same patient (correlated outcomes); LMM accounts for within-subject correlation.
  2. Handles **unequal/missing follow-ups** without discarding all data (under MAR).
- **Why it may not be appropriate (≥1):**
  - Pain VAS 0–10 is **bounded and often skewed/zero-inflated**; normality may be poor.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If residual diagnostics show severe non-normality/heteroscedasticity that cannot be remedied (and outcome clearly ordinal/zero-inflated).
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - **Generalized linear mixed model (GLMM)** with appropriate distribution (e.g., ordinal cumulative logit mixed model; or Poisson/negative binomial if treated as counts), or **nonparametric longitudinal methods** (e.g., rank-based mixed models).
- **Implementation notes (recommended structure):**
  - Fixed effects: **Group (FE/FP), Time (24/48/72/1w), Group×Time**.
  - Random effects: subject-specific intercept (and possibly random slope for time).
  - Primary estimand: difference in mean pain between groups at each time (model-based contrasts) and/or overall group×time test.

## A2) Chi-square test (or Fisher’s exact) for sealer extrusion (FE vs FP)
- **Purpose:** Compare **proportion with extrusion** between groups.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Outcome is **categorical (binary)** and groups are independent by randomization.
  2. Provides a direct test of **association of treatment with extrusion**.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If expected cell counts are small (rare extrusion), chi-square approximation may fail.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - When any expected cell count is too low (common rule: <5).
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - **Fisher’s exact test**; additionally report **risk ratio** (or odds ratio) with CI.

## A3) Compare analgesic use between groups (binary “any use” and/or count “# tablets”)
Two complementary mandatory outcomes are typical; choose based on how authors define the endpoint.

### A3a) Logistic regression (or chi-square) for “any ibuprofen use” (yes/no)
- **Purpose:** Test if FE increases probability of taking analgesics.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Outcome is **binary**.
  2. Regression allows **adjustment** for prognostic factors (radiolucency, tooth location, apical size).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If very low event rate, model may be unstable.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Quasi-separation or extremely sparse outcomes.
- **Alternative:**
  - **Firth penalized logistic regression** or Fisher’s exact test (unadjusted).

### A3b) Negative binomial regression (or Poisson with robust SE) for “number of tablets”
- **Purpose:** Compare **analgesic consumption intensity** between groups.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Tablets are **count data** with expected right-skew.
  2. Negative binomial handles **over-dispersion** common in medication counts.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If most participants have **0 tablets** (zero inflation), standard NB may fit poorly.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Clear excess zeros that dominate distribution without modeling that mechanism.
- **Alternative:**
  - **Zero-inflated negative binomial** or **hurdle model**; or analyze as binary any-use.

## A4) Association between extrusion and pain (correlation/relationship)
- **Test name:** Regression model linking extrusion to pain (preferred) OR Spearman correlation (simpler)
- **Purpose:** Evaluate whether **extrusion is associated with higher pain**.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. The question concerns a relationship between a **binary predictor (extrusion)** and pain outcome.
  2. Regression permits **adjustment for randomized group and time** (important because FE may increase extrusion).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If extrusion is rare, estimates will be imprecise; correlation may be misleading for mixed types.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Do not use simple Pearson correlation (binary vs bounded non-normal pain) or unadjusted comparisons that ignore time structure.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - For pain at specific time point: **Wilcoxon rank-sum** (extrusion yes/no) or **quantile regression**.
  - For longitudinal pain: **mixed model** with extrusion as covariate and interactions (extrusion×time), robust SEs if needed.

# CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED (improves rigor)

## B1) Baseline comparability checks (descriptive + standardized differences)
- **Test name:** Standardized mean difference (SMD); avoid p-value driven “baseline testing”
- **Purpose:** Assess balance of baseline covariates after randomization.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. RCTs should show **covariate balance**; SMD is not sample-size driven like p-values.
  2. Helps decide if **adjusted analyses** are warranted for precision.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If key covariates were not collected/available.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Do not interpret imbalance testing as evidence randomization “failed.”
- **Alternative:**
  - None needed; present stratified descriptives if limited variables.

## B2) Ordinal mixed model for categorized pain (none/mild/moderate/severe)
- **Purpose:** Use the prespecified **pain categories** as an ordinal outcome over time.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Categories are **ordinal**; cumulative logit respects ordering.
  2. Robust to non-normal VAS and communicates clinically interpretable shifts in severity.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - **Proportional odds assumption** may be violated.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If categories are too sparse in upper levels (few severe cases), model unstable.
- **Alternative:**
  - **Partial proportional odds** model; or collapse categories (eg, moderate/severe vs none/mild) and use logistic mixed model.

## B3) Adjusted primary analysis (precision gains)
- **Test name:** ANCOVA-type adjustment within mixed model (include covariates)
- **Purpose:** Increase precision and account for prognostic factors: apical radiolucency, tooth arch (max/mand), apical size (Primary/Large), age/sex if available.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. In RCTs, covariate adjustment can **improve efficiency** without introducing bias if prespecified.
  2. Addresses residual imbalance and improves interpretability.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Overfitting if too many covariates relative to events (especially for extrusion/analgesic models).
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Small sample within strata or sparse outcomes.
- **Alternative:**
  - Use a **parsimonious** covariate set; penalized regression for sparse binary outcomes.

## B4) Multiple-comparisons control for time-point contrasts
- **Test name:** Holm correction (or Tukey for model-based pairwise comparisons)
- **Purpose:** Control family-wise error when comparing groups at multiple time points.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. There are **4 planned time-point comparisons**.
  2. Reduces false positives while preserving power better than Bonferroni (Holm).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If a single primary contrast is prespecified (e.g., 24h only), adjustment may be unnecessary.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - When only the global group×time interaction is the primary inference and time-point contrasts are exploratory.
- **Alternative:**
  - False discovery rate (Benjamini–Hochberg) for exploratory contrasts.

## B5) Effect size reporting
- **Measures:** Mean difference in pain (model-based), odds ratio/risk ratio for extrusion and any-analgesic use, incidence rate ratio for tablet counts.
- **Purpose:** Quantify clinical relevance beyond p-values.
- **Why appropriate:** Standard for RCT interpretation; allows meta-analysis.
- **Why may not be appropriate:** If outcome distributions prevent stable estimates (rare events).
- **Alternative:** Exact CIs, penalized methods.

# CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL (supplementary insights)

## C1) Area-under-the-curve (AUC) summary of pain over 1 week + group comparison
- **Test name:** Compare AUC via t-test/ANCOVA (or Mann–Whitney)
- **Purpose:** Provide a single summary of cumulative pain burden.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Collapses longitudinal pain into **one clinically intuitive metric**.
  2. Avoids multiple time-point testing.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Requires reasonably complete repeated measures; imputation assumptions affect AUC.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - High missingness or irregular assessment timing.
- **Alternative:**
  - Model-based marginal mean pain over time from mixed model.

## C2) Time-to-first analgesic intake
- **Test name:** Kaplan–Meier + log-rank; Cox regression
- **Purpose:** Evaluate **timing** of rescue medication use.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Accounts for **censoring** (no analgesic by 1 week).
  2. Uses information on **when** medication was first taken (if collected).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - The study collected tablets at discrete calls; exact timing may be unknown.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If only total tablets or yes/no per interval is known.
- **Alternative:**
  - Discrete-time survival model or interval-censored methods.

## C3) Interaction / subgroup analyses (exploratory only)
- **Examples:** group×radiolucency, group×apical size (Primary vs Large), group×arch.
- **Purpose:** Explore effect modification.
- **Why appropriate:** Clinically plausible heterogeneity.
- **Why not appropriate:** Underpowered; inflation of type I error.
- **When NOT to use:** If not prespecified; present as hypothesis-generating.
- **Alternative:** Report stratified descriptives without formal claims.

## TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS (should NOT be used)

1. **Independent-samples t-test at each time point without accounting for repeated measures** (and no multiplicity control)  
   - Ignores within-subject correlation; inflates type I error; inefficient.

2. **One-way ANOVA treating the 4 pain measurements as independent observations**  
   - Violates independence assumption; incorrect standard errors.

3. **Pearson correlation between sealer extrusion (binary) and pain**  
   - Pearson assumes continuous, approximately normal variables; binary predictor violates this; regression/point-biserial/Spearman or group comparison is preferable.

4. **Simple linear regression on VAS without checking distribution / strong zero-inflation**  
   - Can yield biased inference if residual assumptions are badly violated.

5. **Using only pain categories and applying multinomial logistic regression ignoring ordinality and repeated measures**  
   - Loses ordering information and ignores within-subject correlation.

## TASK 4: SUMMARY (overall analytical approach)

Because pain was measured repeatedly (24h, 48h, 72h, 1 week), the primary analysis should be a **mixed-effects longitudinal model** estimating **group differences over time** (including group×time). Binary outcomes (extrusion; any analgesic use) should be analyzed with **2-group proportion tests and/or logistic regression**, and tablet counts with **count regression** (negative binomial/zero-inflated as needed). The extrusion–pain relationship should be assessed using **regression frameworks** that can adjust for treatment group and (ideally) time, providing effect sizes with confidence intervals and appropriate handling of missing repeated measures.
