### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Prospective, randomized clinical trial (RCT).  
   - Single-blind design (patients blinded to group allocation).  

2. **Study Objectives**:  
   - Primary: To assess postoperative pain, use of pain relief medication, and extrusion of filling material in single-visit root canal treatments with or without foraminal enlargement (FE).  
   - Secondary: To investigate the correlation between sealer extrusion and postoperative pain.  

3. **Study Groups**:  
   - **Group 1 (FE)**: Teeth treated with foraminal enlargement.  
   - **Group 2 (FP)**: Teeth treated with foraminal patency only.  

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - 70 patients per group (n = 140 total).  
   - Sample size calculation based on ANOVA with parameters: mean difference = 0.03, SD = 0.05, power = 80%, alpha = 0.05.  

5. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Asymptomatic, necrotic single-rooted teeth with fully formed and straight root canals.  
   - Patients aged 19–55 years, ASA class I, with a maximum periodontal probing depth of 3 mm.  

6. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
   - Systemic diseases, recent antibiotic or painkiller use, teeth with swelling, sinus tract, root resorption, or prior endodontic treatment.  

7. **Randomization**:  
   - Patients randomly selected sealed envelopes indicating group allocation.  

8. **Blinding**:  
   - Single-blind (patients blinded).  

9. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variable**: Treatment type (FE vs. FP).  
   - **Dependent Variables**:  
     - Postoperative pain (measured using a modified visual analog scale, 0–10).  
     - Pain relief medication use (number of ibuprofen tablets).  
     - Sealer extrusion (radiographic assessment).  

10. **Measurements**:  
    - Pain scores recorded at 24, 48, 72 hours, and 1 week postoperatively.  
    - Pain categories: No pain (0), mild pain (1–3), moderate pain (4–7), severe pain (8–10).  
    - Sealer extrusion evaluated radiographically.  

11. **Data Collection Methods**:  
    - Pain scores recorded by patients using a visual analog scale.  
    - Follow-up phone calls by a dental assistant to confirm pain scores and medication use.  
    - Radiographic evaluation of sealer extrusion.  

12. **Treatment Protocol**:  
    - Standardized root canal treatment performed by two experienced endodontists.  
    - Instrumentation with WaveOne Gold files and irrigation with sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) and EDTA.  
    - Final obturation with gutta-percha and AH Plus sealer.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test (or Mann-Whitney U Test if non-normal).  
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean pain scores between the FE and FP groups at each time point.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The study aims to compare two independent groups.  
     2. Pain scores are continuous variables suitable for parametric or non-parametric testing.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normal distribution of pain scores, which may not hold.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If pain scores are heavily skewed or violate homogeneity of variance.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test if assumptions are violated.  

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test).  
   - **Purpose**: Compare proportions of patients with sealer extrusion and pain relief medication use between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Both variables are categorical (presence/absence of extrusion, medication use).  
     2. Suitable for comparing proportions between independent groups.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Small cell counts may invalidate the Chi-Square test.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected frequencies in any cell are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test for small sample sizes.  

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model).  
   - **Purpose**: Assess changes in pain scores over time and between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Pain scores are measured longitudinally at multiple time points.  
     2. Accounts for within-subject correlations.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes sphericity, which may not hold.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sphericity assumption is violated and corrections (e.g., Greenhouse-Geisser) are insufficient.  
   - **Alternative**: Mixed-Effects Model for more flexibility in handling missing data and unequal variances.  

2. **Test Name**: Pearson or Spearman Correlation.  
   - **Purpose**: Assess the correlation between sealer extrusion and pain scores.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Both variables are continuous or ordinal.  
     2. Directly addresses a secondary objective of the study.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linearity and normality for Pearson correlation.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are non-linear or heavily skewed.  
   - **Alternative**: Spearman correlation for non-parametric data.  

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.  
   - **Purpose**: Model the likelihood of sealer extrusion as a function of treatment group and other covariates (e.g., age, sex).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Sealer extrusion is a binary outcome.  
     2. Allows adjustment for potential confounders.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires a larger sample size for reliable estimates.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If the number of events (extrusions) is too low.  
   - **Alternative**: Exact logistic regression for small sample sizes.  

2. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.  
   - **Purpose**: Analyze time to pain resolution between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Time-to-event data (pain resolution) is of interest.  
     2. Accounts for censored data (e.g., patients lost to follow-up).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes proportional hazards, which may not hold.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If follow-up times are inconsistent or incomplete.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.
