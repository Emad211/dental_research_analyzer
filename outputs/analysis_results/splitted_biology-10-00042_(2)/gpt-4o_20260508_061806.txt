### **TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION**

#### **Study Characteristics**
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Triple-armed, single-blinded, randomized controlled clinical trial.

2. **Objective**: 
   - To compare the instrumentation time and obturation quality of three root canal instrumentation techniques in primary molars:
     - Adaptive XP-endo Shaper rotary files.
     - Kedo-S pediatric rotary files.
     - Manual hand K-files.

3. **Groups**:
   - Group 1: XP-endo Shaper rotary files (n=25).
   - Group 2: Kedo-S pediatric rotary files (n=25).
   - Group 3: Hand K-files (n=25).

4. **Sample Size**:
   - Total: 75 patients (25 per group).
   - Inclusion criteria: Patients aged 4–9 years with necrotic primary molars containing three canals.

5. **Variables**:
   - **Primary Outcomes**:
     - Instrumentation time (measured in minutes using a digital stopwatch).
     - Quality of obturation (radiographically assessed as optimal, underfilled, or overfilled).
   - **Independent Variable**:
     - Type of instrumentation technique (XP-endo Shaper, Kedo-S, or Hand K-files).
   - **Covariates**:
     - Patient age, tooth type, and canal morphology.

6. **Measurements**:
   - **Instrumentation Time**:
     - Measured from the introduction of the first file into the canal to the final saline irrigation.
   - **Quality of Obturation**:
     - Radiographic evaluation using O’Riordan and Coll’s classification:
       - Optimal: 1 mm short of the apex.
       - Underfilled: 2 mm short of the apex.
       - Overfilled: Beyond the apex.

7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Randomization: Computer-generated randomization.
   - Blinding: Single-blinded (evaluating observer and analyst were blinded; operator was not blinded due to the recognizable characteristics of the files).
   - Ethical Approval: Approved by the Institutional Review Board (CDSH/IEC/2018-19/004).
   - Informed Consent: Obtained from parents of all participants.

8. **Instrumentation Protocols**:
   - **XP-endo Shaper**:
     - Speed: 800 rpm; Torque: 1 Ncm.
     - Used with light vertical strokes and irrigation with 2.5% NaOCl and EDTA.
   - **Kedo-S Rotary Files**:
     - Speed: 250 rpm; Torque: 2.2 Ncm.
     - Used with in-and-out motion and irrigation with 2.5% NaOCl and EDTA.
   - **Hand K-files**:
     - Quarter-turn-and-pull motion with sequential sizes (#15 to #30).
     - Irrigation with 2.5% NaOCl and EDTA.

### **TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN**

#### **CATEGORY A – MANDATORY**
1. **Test Name**: One-Way ANOVA.
   - **Purpose**: To compare the mean instrumentation times across the three groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves comparing means of a continuous variable (instrumentation time) across three independent groups.
     2. The design assumes normal distribution and homogeneity of variances, which can be tested.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the assumption of homogeneity of variances is violated.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the data are not normally distributed and cannot be transformed.
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test (non-parametric alternative).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test of Independence.
   - **Purpose**: To compare the distribution of obturation quality (categorical variable: optimal, underfilled, overfilled) across the three groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The outcome variable (obturation quality) is categorical.
     2. The groups are independent, and the sample size is sufficient for chi-square analysis.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If expected cell frequencies are too low (<5 in >20% of cells).
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small for chi-square assumptions.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test (for small sample sizes).

#### **CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED**
1. **Test Name**: Post-Hoc Pairwise Comparisons (e.g., Tukey’s HSD or Bonferroni correction).
   - **Purpose**: To identify which specific groups differ in instrumentation time if the ANOVA test is significant.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Necessary to pinpoint group differences after a significant ANOVA result.
     2. Controls for Type I error rate in multiple comparisons.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If ANOVA assumptions are violated.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the overall ANOVA is not significant.
   - **Alternative**: Pairwise Mann-Whitney U tests with Bonferroni correction.

2. **Test Name**: Cohen’s d or Eta-Squared.
   - **Purpose**: To calculate effect size for the differences in instrumentation time and obturation quality.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides a measure of the magnitude of the observed differences.
     2. Complements hypothesis testing by quantifying practical significance.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If data are not normally distributed.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the primary analysis (e.g., ANOVA) is invalid.
   - **Alternative**: Rank-biserial correlation for non-parametric data.

#### **CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL**
1. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.
   - **Purpose**: To model the probability of achieving optimal obturation quality based on instrumentation technique and other covariates (e.g., age, tooth type).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Allows for adjustment of covariates.
     2. Provides odds ratios for the likelihood of optimal obturation.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the sample size is insufficient for multivariable modeling.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the outcome variable has sparse categories.
   - **Alternative**: Stratified analysis or subgroup analysis.

2. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.
   - **Purpose**: To assess the time to complete instrumentation across the three groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Useful for time-to-event data.
     2. Provides a visual comparison of instrumentation time distributions.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If there is no censoring in the data (e.g., all instrumentation times are recorded without interruption).
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the data do not represent time-to-event outcomes.
   - **Alternative**: Descriptive statistics or ANOVA.
