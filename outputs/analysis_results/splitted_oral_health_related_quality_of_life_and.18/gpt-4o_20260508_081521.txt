### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Longitudinal prospective study.

2. **Objective**: 
   - To investigate the factors affecting oral health-related quality of life (OH-QOL) and satisfaction of patients before and after root canal treatment (RCT) performed by operators with varying levels of expertise (students, postgraduates, and specialists).

3. **Groups**:
   - Patients were grouped based on the expertise level of the operator performing the RCT:
     - Undergraduate students.
     - Postgraduate students.
     - Specialists.

4. **Sample Size**:
   - 250 patients.

5. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Patients aged 18–60 years.
   - Medically and mentally fit to provide informed consent.

6. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Patients with uncontrolled diabetes, hypertension, blood disorders, pregnancy, orthodontic treatment, or failed RCTs requiring retreatment.

7. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variables**:
     - Operator expertise level (students, postgraduates, specialists).
   - **Dependent Variables**:
     - Oral health-related quality of life (OH-QOL) measured using the OHIP-17 questionnaire.
     - Patient satisfaction measured using a semantic differential scale.
   - **Covariates**:
     - Sociodemographic factors (e.g., age, gender, education level).

8. **Measurements**:
   - **OHIP-17 Questionnaire**:
     - Used to assess OH-QOL before and 2 weeks after RCT.
     - Validated Arabic version with a kappa statistic of 0.95.
   - **Semantic Differential Scale**:
     - Used to assess patient satisfaction 2 weeks after RCT.
   - **Radiographic Assessment**:
     - Used to evaluate clinical outcomes.

9. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - OHIP-17 questionnaire and semantic differential scale administered by an independent examiner.
   - Baseline data collected before treatment and follow-up data collected 2 weeks after treatment.

10. **Ethics**:
    - Ethical clearance obtained (approval no. 9-16-8/39).
    - Informed consent obtained from all participants.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon signed-rank test if non-normal data).
   - **Purpose**: To compare OH-QOL scores before and after RCT within the same group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves pre- and post-treatment comparisons for the same patients.
     2. OHIP-17 scores are continuous and paired data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality of the difference scores, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the data are not normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: One-way ANOVA (or Kruskal-Wallis test if non-normal data).
   - **Purpose**: To compare OH-QOL improvement across the three operator expertise groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study compares more than two independent groups.
     2. OHIP-17 improvement scores are continuous.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes homogeneity of variance and normality.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If assumptions of ANOVA are violated.
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test (non-parametric).

3. **Test Name**: Chi-square test (or Fisher’s exact test for small sample sizes).
   - **Purpose**: To compare categorical variables (e.g., satisfaction levels) across operator expertise groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Satisfaction levels are categorical.
     2. Operator groups are independent.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires sufficient sample size in each category.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If expected frequencies in contingency tables are too low.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s exact test.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Multiple linear regression.
   - **Purpose**: To assess the impact of operator expertise and other covariates (e.g., age, gender) on OH-QOL improvement.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Allows adjustment for potential confounders.
     2. Quantifies the independent effect of operator expertise.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes linear relationships between predictors and outcome.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If multicollinearity or non-linearity is present.
   - **Alternative**: Generalized additive models (GAM).

2. **Test Name**: Repeated measures ANOVA (or mixed-effects model).
   - **Purpose**: To analyze changes in OH-QOL over time and across operator expertise groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Accounts for within-subject correlations.
     2. Suitable for longitudinal data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes sphericity, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If sphericity assumption is violated.
   - **Alternative**: Mixed-effects model.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Mediation analysis.
   - **Purpose**: To explore whether satisfaction mediates the relationship between operator expertise and OH-QOL improvement.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides insights into indirect effects.
     2. Enhances understanding of causal pathways.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires large sample size for reliable estimates.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If sample size is insufficient.
   - **Alternative**: Path analysis.

2. **Test Name**: Subgroup analysis.
   - **Purpose**: To explore whether the effect of operator expertise differs by sociodemographic factors (e.g., age, gender).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Identifies effect modifiers.
     2. Provides targeted insights for specific subgroups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Risk of multiple testing and Type I error.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If subgroup sizes are too small.
   - **Alternative**: Interaction terms in regression models.

   - **Reason**: Not suitable for continuous outcomes like OH-QOL scores.
