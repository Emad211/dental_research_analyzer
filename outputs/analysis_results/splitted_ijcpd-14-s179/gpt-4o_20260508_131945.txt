### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**: Single-blinded randomized controlled trial (RCT).
2. **Objective**: To compare and evaluate the child's behavior, anxiety level, and pain perception during pulpectomy using manual (K files) and rotary (ProTaper NiTi files) instrumentation techniques.
3. **Groups**:
   - **Group I**: Manual instrumentation using K files.
   - **Group II**: Rotary instrumentation using ProTaper NiTi files.
4. **Sample Size**: 
   - Total: 88 participants (44 per group).
   - Determined from a pilot study with 95% power and 5% alpha error.
5. **Target Population**: Children aged 4–7 years attending Saveetha Dental College for routine dental treatment.
6. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Cooperative behavior during the first dental visit.
   - Vital primary teeth requiring pulpectomy.
7. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Non-cooperative children.
   - Children with physical/psychological disabilities or systemic diseases.
   - Abscessed/nonvital teeth, internal/pathological resorption, or inadequate coronal tooth structure.
   - Parents unwilling to provide informed consent.
8. **Randomization and Allocation Concealment**: Computer-generated randomization sequence with sealed envelope concealment.
9. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variable**: Type of instrumentation (manual vs. rotary).
   - **Dependent Variables**:
     - Child's behavior (Frankl Behavior Rating Scale).
     - Anxiety level (Venham’s Interval Rating Scale).
     - Pain perception (Wong-Baker FACES Pain Scale).
10. **Measurements**:
    - **Behavior**: Frankl Behavior Rating Scale (4 categories: definitely positive, positive, negative, definitely negative).
    - **Anxiety**: Venham’s Interval Rating Scale (scores 0–5).
    - **Pain**: Wong-Baker FACES Pain Scale (self-reported).
11. **Data Collection Methods**:
    - Behavior and anxiety scores recorded by the operator during canal preparation.
    - Pain scores self-reported by children after instrumentation.
12. **Intervention Details**:
    - **Manual Group**: K files used in quarter pull turn method with saline irrigation.
    - **Rotary Group**: ProTaper files used sequentially (SX and S2) with an X-Smart motor at 300 rpm and low torque.
13. **Ethical Approval**: Approved by the institutional ethical committee (SRB/SDMDS15PED12).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test (or Mann-Whitney U Test if assumptions are violated).
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean scores of behavior, anxiety, and pain between manual and rotary groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves comparing two independent groups.
     2. The dependent variables (behavior, anxiety, pain) are measured on ordinal or continuous scales.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normal distribution of data, which may not hold for ordinal scales (e.g., Frankl and Venham scales).
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are heavily skewed or non-normal.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test for non-parametric data.

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test of Independence.
   - **Purpose**: Assess the association between type of instrumentation and categorical behavior outcomes (Frankl scale categories).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Behavior outcomes are categorical (4 levels).
     2. Suitable for comparing proportions between two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires sufficient sample size in each category; sparse data may lead to unreliable results.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected frequencies in any cell are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test for small sample sizes.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Wilcoxon Rank-Sum Test (alternative to Mann-Whitney U Test).
   - **Purpose**: Compare anxiety and pain scores between groups when data are ordinal and non-normal.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Non-parametric test suitable for ordinal data.
     2. Does not assume normality.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Less powerful than parametric tests for large sample sizes.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are truly continuous and normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Independent Samples t-Test.

2. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Friedman Test for non-parametric data).
   - **Purpose**: Analyze changes in anxiety and pain scores across different time points (if applicable).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Suitable for longitudinal or repeated measures data.
     2. Accounts for within-subject variability.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes sphericity; violations require corrections (e.g., Greenhouse-Geisser).
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are ordinal or non-normal.
   - **Alternative**: Friedman Test.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.
   - **Purpose**: Predict categorical behavior outcomes based on instrumentation type and other covariates (e.g., age, gender).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Allows adjustment for confounding variables.
     2. Provides odds ratios for categorical outcomes.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires large sample size for reliable estimates.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is insufficient or data are sparse.
   - **Alternative**: Chi-Square Test.

2. **Test Name**: Spearman’s Rank Correlation.
   - **Purpose**: Assess relationships between anxiety, pain, and behavior scores.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Suitable for ordinal data.
     2. Does not assume linearity or normality.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Cannot infer causation.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are nominal or binary.
   - **Alternative**: Pearson’s Correlation (for continuous data).
