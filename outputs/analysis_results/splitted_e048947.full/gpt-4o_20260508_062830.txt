### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### **Study Characteristics**
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Two-arm parallel randomized controlled trial (RCT).

2. **Study Objectives**:  
   - To compare the antimicrobial efficacy of Calcipex (CH alone) and Metapex (CH with iodoform) in endodontic treatment of teeth presenting with chronic apical periodontitis.

3. **Groups**:  
   - **Group A (Control)**: Calcipex (CH alone).  
   - **Group B (Intervention)**: Metapex (CH with iodoform).

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Total: 60 teeth (30 in each group).  
   - Based on population mean and SD (50.3 ± 13 CFU/mL) from prior studies, with 95% confidence level and 0.05 precision.

5. **Sampling Technique**:  
   - Block randomization with a block size of 6.

6. **Blinding**:  
   - Triple-blinded: Patient, analyzer, and statistician are blinded.  
   - Investigator placing the medicament is not blinded due to color differences between the two medicaments.

7. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Single-rooted permanent teeth with single root canal.  
   - Diagnosed with chronic apical periodontitis.  
   - No antibiotics use in the last 3 months.

8. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
   - Periodontally compromised teeth (Grade III mobility).  
   - Endodontically non-salvageable teeth.  
   - Immunocompromised patients or those with systemic illness.  
   - Excessive root resorption.  
   - History of allergy to CH or iodoform.  
   - Endodontic retreatment cases.

9. **Interventions**:  
   - **Calcipex**: Water-based CH paste, applied for 7 days.  
   - **Metapex**: Oil-based CH paste with iodoform, applied for 7 days.  
   - Both medicaments placed using lentulo spiral fillers and sealed with temporary resin-based filling material.

10. **Outcome**:  
    - **Primary Outcome**: Reduction in bacterial load (CFU/mL) measured via microbial culture.  
    - Samples collected at baseline (S1) and after 7 days (S2).

11. **Data Collection Methods**:  
    - Paper points used to collect samples from the root canal.  
    - Samples processed in microbiology lab using Brain Heart Infusion Broth (BHIB) and blood agar for CFU quantification.

12. **Follow-Up**:  
    - 7 ± 3 days after initial intervention.

13. **Ethical Considerations**:  
    - Approved by the Ethical Review Committee (ERC) of Aga Khan University Hospital.  
    - Informed consent obtained from participants.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### **CATEGORY A – MANDATORY**
1. **Test Name**: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon signed-rank test if non-parametric).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare pre- and post-intervention CFU/mL values within each group (S1 vs. S2).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Continuous outcome variable (CFU/mL).  
     2. Same participants measured at two time points (paired data).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normal distribution of differences.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are not normally distributed.  
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Independent t-test (or Mann-Whitney U test if non-parametric).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare the mean CFU/mL reduction between the two groups (Calcipex vs. Metapex).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Two independent groups.  
     2. Continuous outcome variable (CFU/mL).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and equal variances.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are not normally distributed or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test (non-parametric).

3. **Test Name**: Chi-square test (or Fisher’s exact test).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare categorical adverse event rates between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Categorical data (presence/absence of adverse events).  
     2. Two independent groups.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Small sample size in one or more cells.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If expected cell counts are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s exact test.

#### **CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED**
1. **Test Name**: Repeated measures ANOVA (or Friedman test if non-parametric).  
   - **Purpose**: To evaluate within-group changes in CFU/mL over time (S1 vs. S2) and interaction with group (Calcipex vs. Metapex).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for repeated measurements within participants.  
     2. Tests for interaction effects between time and group.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and sphericity.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Friedman test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Effect size calculation (e.g., Cohen’s d).  
   - **Purpose**: To quantify the magnitude of the difference in CFU/mL reduction between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides practical significance of results.  
     2. Complements p-values for better interpretation.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not a hypothesis test, only descriptive.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are not interpretable or sample size is too small.  
   - **Alternative**: Non-parametric effect size measures.

#### **CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL**
1. **Test Name**: Subgroup analysis (e.g., stratified by baseline CFU levels).  
   - **Purpose**: To explore whether baseline CFU levels influence the efficacy of the medicaments.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Identifies potential effect modifiers.  
     2. Provides additional insights into treatment efficacy.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Risk of overfitting with small sample size.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If sample size is insufficient for stratification.  
   - **Alternative**: Regression models with interaction terms.

2. **Test Name**: Regression analysis (e.g., linear regression).  
   - **Purpose**: To adjust for potential confounders (e.g., age, baseline CFU).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Adjusts for covariates.  
     2. Evaluates independent effect of the intervention.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linearity and independence of errors.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Generalized linear models.
