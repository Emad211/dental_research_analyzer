### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Prospective cohort study.

2. **Study Objectives**:  
   - To elicit patients’ subjective evaluations of root canal treatment (RCT) outcomes one year after initiation of treatment in general dental practice.  
   - To assess the reliability of responses to the questionnaire.

3. **Study Population**:  
   - **Baseline Population**: 243 patients who started RCT during an 8-week period at 20 public dental clinics in Västra Götaland, Sweden.  
   - **Respondents**: 159 patients (67.4%) responded to the first questionnaire, and 44 patients (88%) responded to the second questionnaire for reliability testing.  
   - **Demographics**: 52.7% women, 47.3% men; mean age = 48.3 years (SD = 16.4).  
   - **Tooth Characteristics**: 64.9% symptomatic teeth, main diagnoses: pulpal necrosis with apical periodontitis (38.1%) and pulpitis (37.7%), molars predominated (47.7%).

4. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variables**: Gender, age, jaw (maxilla/mandible), tooth group (incisor/premolar/molar), presence of symptoms (asymptomatic/pain), diagnosis at initiation of RCT.  
   - **Dependent Variables**:  
     - Tooth retention (yes/no).  
     - Completion of RCT with root filling (yes/no).  
     - Pain intensity (VAS scale, 0–10).  
     - Recollection of pain during RCT (VAS scale, 0–10).  
     - Post-operative aesthetics (VAS scale, 0–10).  
     - Tooth function during chewing (VAS scale, 0–10).  
     - Perceived cost-effectiveness of RCT (VAS scale, 0–10).  
     - Retrospective decision to choose RCT again (yes/no/uncertain).  

5. **Questionnaire and Data Collection**:  
   - Questionnaire adapted from Dugas et al. (2002).  
   - Visual Analogue Scale (VAS, 10 cm) used for subjective assessments.  
   - First questionnaire sent 1–3 years after RCT initiation, with reminders and follow-up calls to improve response rates.  
   - Second questionnaire sent to the first 50 respondents to assess test-retest reliability.  

6. **Data Management**:  
   - Data manually entered into Excel.  
   - Missing data were left empty and designated as missing in the analysis.

7. **Reliability Analysis**:  
   - Test-retest reliability assessed using intraclass correlation coefficients (ICCs) for responses to the second questionnaire.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Descriptive Statistics**:  
   - **Purpose**: Summarize demographic and baseline characteristics of the study population.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides an overview of the sample distribution (e.g., age, gender, tooth characteristics).  
     2. Helps identify missing data patterns.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not inferential; does not provide statistical comparisons.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: N/A (always necessary).  
   - **Alternative**: None.  

2. **Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Compare categorical variables (e.g., tooth retention vs. gender).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Tests associations between independent and dependent categorical variables.  
     2. Suitable for binary outcomes (e.g., tooth retention).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires sufficient sample size in each cell; not suitable for small expected frequencies.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If >20% of expected counts are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test for small sample sizes.  

3. **Paired t-Test (or Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Assess test-retest reliability of continuous variables (e.g., VAS scores).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Evaluates differences between paired responses.  
     2. Suitable for continuous data with normal distribution.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality of differences.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are not normally distributed.  
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test for non-normal data.  

4. **Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Assess test-retest reliability of questionnaire responses.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Measures consistency or agreement between repeated measures.  
     2. Recommended for reliability studies.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Sensitive to sample size and variability.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is too small or data are highly inconsistent.  
   - **Alternative**: Kappa statistic for categorical responses.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Independent t-Test (or Mann-Whitney U Test)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean VAS scores between two independent groups (e.g., gender).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Tests differences in continuous outcomes between groups.  
     2. Suitable for normally distributed data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and equal variances.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are skewed or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test for non-normal data.  

2. **ANOVA (or Kruskal-Wallis Test)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean VAS scores across multiple groups (e.g., age categories).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Tests differences in continuous outcomes across >2 groups.  
     2. Suitable for normally distributed data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and homogeneity of variances.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis Test for non-normal data.  

3. **Logistic Regression**:  
   - **Purpose**: Identify predictors of binary outcomes (e.g., tooth retention).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Models relationships between predictors and binary outcomes.  
     2. Adjusts for confounders.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Sensitive to small sample sizes and multicollinearity.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is insufficient for the number of predictors.  
   - **Alternative**: Penalized logistic regression for small samples.  

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Identify underlying dimensions of patient satisfaction from questionnaire items.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Reduces dimensionality of data.  
     2. Explores latent constructs in satisfaction measures.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires large sample size and continuous data.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is too small or data are categorical.  
   - **Alternative**: Principal Component Analysis (PCA).  

2. **Correlation Analysis**:  
   - **Purpose**: Assess relationships between continuous variables (e.g., pain intensity and aesthetics).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Identifies linear relationships.  
     2. Useful for exploratory analysis.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linearity and normality.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are non-linear or non-normal.  
   - **Alternative**: Spearman’s rank correlation for non-normal data.
