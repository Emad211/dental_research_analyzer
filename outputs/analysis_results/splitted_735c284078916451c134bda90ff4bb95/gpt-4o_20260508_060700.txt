### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:
   - Randomized, parallel-group, sham-controlled clinical trial with two arms.

2. **Objective**:
   - To compare the effect of low-level laser therapy (LLLT) on postoperative pain after single-visit endodontic retreatment (ER) of mandibular molars.

3. **Groups**:
   - **Intervention Group**: LLLT after single-visit retreatment.
   - **Control Group**: Mock laser (sham) after single-visit retreatment.

4. **Sample Size**:
   - Total randomized: 36 participants.
   - Intervention group: 18 participants.
   - Control group: 18 participants.

5. **Eligibility Criteria**:
   - **Inclusion**:
     - Patients in good health based on medical history and verbal questioning.
     - Scheduled for root canal retreatment on symptomatic first or second mandibular molars.
     - Mandibular molars with previous root canal treatment and a PAI (Periapical Index) score of 2 or 3.
   - **Exclusion**:
     - Patients who consumed analgesics during the study period were excluded and replaced.

6. **Randomization**:
   - Achieved using a web-based algorithm (www.random.org).

7. **Intervention Details**:
   - **LLLT Group**:
     - 980-nm diode laser.
     - Energy density: 6.89 W/cm².
     - Power: 0.5 W.
     - Tip diameter: 10 mm.
     - Duration: 15 seconds.
     - Laser applied to soft tissues covering mesial and distal apices of the tooth from the buccal side, with the tip held 10 mm from the mucosa.
   - **Sham Group**:
     - Laser handpiece positioned similarly but not activated.

8. **Outcome Measures**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Postoperative pain levels measured using a Visual Analog Scale (VAS).
     - Time points: 4, 8, 12, and 24 hours; 2, 3, and 7 days post-treatment.
   - **Secondary Outcome**: Analgesic consumption (dosage and time).

9. **Data Collection**:
   - Pain levels recorded by patients on VAS scales.
   - Patients contacted by phone to remind them to record pain levels.
   - Analgesic consumption recorded in a home questionnaire.

10. **Blinding**:
    - Patients were blinded to the intervention (sham-controlled).
    - Clinician administering the laser was different from the clinician recording the outcomes.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-test (or Mann-Whitney U test if assumptions are violated).
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean postoperative pain scores between the LLLT and sham groups at each time point.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves two independent groups.
     2. The primary outcome (VAS scores) is continuous and measured at multiple time points.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality of data distribution, which may not hold for VAS scores.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the data are non-normally distributed or contain significant outliers.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model if assumptions are violated).
   - **Purpose**: Assess changes in pain scores over time within and between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Pain scores are measured repeatedly over time.
     2. Allows evaluation of both time and group effects, as well as their interaction.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes sphericity, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If sphericity is violated and cannot be corrected using adjustments like Greenhouse-Geisser.
   - **Alternative**: Mixed-effects model (handles missing data and does not require sphericity).

3. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test if sample size is small).
   - **Purpose**: Compare proportions of participants consuming analgesics between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The variable (analgesic consumption) is categorical.
     2. Suitable for comparing proportions between two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires a sufficiently large sample size for valid results.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If expected cell counts are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Effect Size Analysis (e.g., Cohen’s d).
   - **Purpose**: Quantify the magnitude of the difference in pain scores between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides a standardized measure of the intervention effect.
     2. Useful for interpreting clinical significance.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Does not account for repeated measures or time effects.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the primary analysis shows no significant group difference.
   - **Alternative**: None; effect size is a descriptive measure.

2. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.
   - **Purpose**: Analyze time to analgesic consumption between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Accounts for censored data (e.g., participants who never consumed analgesics).
     2. Suitable for time-to-event data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes non-informative censoring, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the event (analgesic consumption) does not occur frequently.
   - **Alternative**: Log-rank test.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (e.g., stratified by PAI score).
   - **Purpose**: Explore whether the effect of LLLT differs by baseline PAI score.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides insights into potential effect modifiers.
     2. May guide future research on patient selection.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Reduces statistical power due to smaller subgroup sizes.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If subgroup sizes are too small to yield reliable results.
   - **Alternative**: Interaction term in a regression model.

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **Paired t-test**:
   - **Why Not**: The groups are independent, not paired.
2. **ANOVA without repeated measures**:
   - **Why Not**: Does not account for the repeated measures nature of the data.
3. **Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test**:
   - **Why Not**: Designed for paired or matched data, not independent groups.
