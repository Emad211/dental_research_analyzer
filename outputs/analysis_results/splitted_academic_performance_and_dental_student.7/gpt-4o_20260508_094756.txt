### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**:  
   Retrospective cohort study.

2. **Objectives**:  
   - Evaluate the consequences of emergency remote teaching on dental students' learning satisfaction.  
   - Compare academic performance in summative examinations between students taught via emergency remote teaching and those taught face-to-face in the prepandemic year.

3. **Groups**:  
   - **Group 1**: Students taught via emergency remote teaching during the COVID-19 pandemic (2019–2020 academic year).  
   - **Group 2**: Students taught face-to-face in the prepandemic year (2018–2019 academic year).

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Group 1: 39 students.  
   - Group 2: 37 students.

5. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variables**:  
     - Mode of teaching (emergency remote teaching vs. face-to-face).  
     - Lecture delivery method (face-to-face, online-synchronized, online-asynchronized).  
   - **Dependent Variables**:  
     - Student satisfaction (survey responses).  
     - Academic performance (summative examination scores).

6. **Measurements**:  
   - **Student Satisfaction**:  
     - Survey with 18 questions rated on satisfaction levels (satisfied, neutral, unsatisfied).  
     - Categories assessed: comprehension, retention, communication, stress-free learning, time convenience, technical support, transportation.  
   - **Academic Performance**:  
     - Summative examination scores (multiple-choice questions based on a fixed blueprint).  
     - Item analysis performed using ExamSoft platform.

7. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Satisfaction survey distributed via email before summative assessments.  
   - Examination scores collected via ExamSoft platform for both cohorts.  
   - Lectures and lecturers were identical across both cohorts.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test (or Mann-Whitney U Test if assumptions are violated).  
   - **Purpose**: Compare summative examination scores between the two cohorts.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Continuous dependent variable (exam scores).  
     - Two independent groups (remote teaching vs. face-to-face teaching).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes normality and homogeneity of variance.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If data are not normally distributed or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test (nonparametric).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test of Independence (or Fisher’s Exact Test for small sample sizes).  
   - **Purpose**: Analyze the association between lecture delivery method and student satisfaction levels.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Categorical variables (lecture delivery method and satisfaction levels).  
     - Large enough sample size for expected frequencies in contingency tables.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Small sample size may lead to low expected frequencies.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If more than 20% of cells have expected frequencies <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: One-Way ANOVA (or Kruskal-Wallis Test if assumptions are violated).  
   - **Purpose**: Compare satisfaction scores across the three lecture delivery methods (face-to-face, online-synchronized, online-asynchronized).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Continuous dependent variable (satisfaction scores).  
     - Three independent groups (lecture delivery methods).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes normality and homogeneity of variance.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If data are not normally distributed or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis Test (nonparametric).

2. **Test Name**: Paired Samples t-Test (or Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test if assumptions are violated).  
   - **Purpose**: Compare satisfaction scores within the same cohort across different lecture delivery methods.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Continuous dependent variable (satisfaction scores).  
     - Paired data (same students rating multiple methods).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes normality of differences.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If differences are not normally distributed.  
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Correlation Analysis (Pearson or Spearman).  
   - **Purpose**: Investigate the relationship between satisfaction scores and academic performance.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Continuous variables (satisfaction scores and exam scores).  
     - Useful for exploring potential associations.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes linearity and normality for Pearson correlation.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If data are not linear or normally distributed.  
   - **Alternative**: Spearman correlation (nonparametric).

2. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.  
   - **Purpose**: Predict likelihood of satisfaction (binary outcome: satisfied vs. unsatisfied) based on lecture delivery method.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Binary dependent variable (satisfaction).  
     - Multiple independent variables can be included.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires large sample size for reliable estimates.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If sample size is insufficient.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test for categorical analysis.
