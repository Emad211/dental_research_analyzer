### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Prospective, randomized, two-arm, parallel clinical study with a 1:1 allocation ratio.

2. **Study Objective**:
   - To evaluate and compare the treatment outcomes of Advanced Platelet-Rich Fibrin (A-PRF) and Platelet-Rich Fibrin (PRF) as scaffolds in regenerative endodontic treatment (RET) of traumatized immature non-vital teeth.

3. **Study Groups**:
   - Group 1: A-PRF scaffold (n = 15).
   - Group 2: PRF scaffold (n = 15).

4. **Sample Size**:
   - Total: 30 patients (15 in each group).
   - 9 patients were excluded during the 12-month follow-up period.

5. **Study Population**:
   - Inclusion Criteria:
     - Patients aged 8–30 years.
     - Cooperative patients with an immature single-rooted non-vital tooth with apical width >1 mm.
     - Teeth with or without periapical lesions, trauma as an etiology, and no systemic diseases.
   - Exclusion Criteria:
     - Teeth with mature apex, internal/external resorption, mobility, ankylosis, root fracture, periodontal pocket, or unrestorable structure.
     - Patients with known allergies to antibiotics or local anesthetics.

6. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variable**: Type of scaffold (A-PRF vs. PRF).
   - **Dependent Variables**:
     - Clinical outcomes (healed, healing, diseased).
     - Radiographic outcomes:
       - Periapical healing (Periapical Index [PAI] scores: 1–2 = healed, 3–4 = healing, >4 = diseased).
       - Root length changes (preoperative vs. postoperative).
       - Root dentin thickness changes (preoperative vs. postoperative).

7. **Measurements**:
   - **Clinical evaluation**: Signs and symptoms of infection.
   - **Radiographic evaluation**:
     - Periapical healing assessed using PAI scores.
     - Root length and dentin thickness measured preoperatively and postoperatively.

8. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Clinical and radiographic evaluations were conducted at baseline, 6 months (±2 weeks), and 12 months (±2 weeks).
   - Radiographs were taken using the same X-ray device and paralleling technique by the same operator.
   - PAI scores were assigned by a single investigator.

9. **Randomization**:
   - Patients were randomly allocated to the A-PRF or PRF groups.

10. **Ethical Considerations**:
    - Conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki.
    - Approved by the institutional ethics committee (Protocol ID: JSS/DCH/IEC/MD-17/2017-2018).
    - Informed consent/assent obtained from participants or parents.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon signed-rank test if non-parametric).
   - **Purpose**: To compare preoperative and postoperative changes in root length and dentin thickness within each group (A-PRF and PRF).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The data involves paired measurements (pre- and post-treatment) for continuous variables (root length and dentin thickness).
     2. The study aims to assess changes over time within the same group.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normal distribution of differences; if violated, a non-parametric test (Wilcoxon signed-rank test) should be used.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small to reliably assess normality.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Wilcoxon signed-rank test.

2. **Test Name**: Independent t-test (or Mann-Whitney U test if non-parametric).
   - **Purpose**: To compare changes in root length and dentin thickness between the A-PRF and PRF groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves comparing two independent groups.
     2. The dependent variables (root length and dentin thickness) are continuous.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality and homogeneity of variances; if violated, a non-parametric test (Mann-Whitney U test) should be used.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample sizes are highly unequal or the data is heavily skewed.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Mann-Whitney U test.

3. **Test Name**: Chi-square test (or Fisher’s exact test if expected cell counts are <5).
   - **Purpose**: To compare categorical treatment outcomes (healed, healing, diseased) between the A-PRF and PRF groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The dependent variable (treatment outcome) is categorical.
     2. The study involves comparing proportions between two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the sample size is too small, leading to low expected frequencies in contingency table cells.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If more than 20% of cells have expected counts <5.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Fisher’s exact test.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Repeated measures ANOVA (or Friedman test if non-parametric).
   - **Purpose**: To assess changes in PAI scores across time points (baseline, 6 months, 12 months) within each group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The data involves repeated measurements of the same variable (PAI scores) over time.
     2. Allows for assessment of trends over time within each group.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes sphericity; if violated, adjustments (e.g., Greenhouse-Geisser correction) are needed.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small for reliable estimation of variance components.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Friedman test.

2. **Test Name**: Logistic regression.
   - **Purpose**: To assess the association between scaffold type (A-PRF vs. PRF) and treatment outcomes (healed, healing, diseased) while adjusting for potential confounders (e.g., age, baseline PAI score).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The outcome variable is categorical.
     2. Allows adjustment for covariates.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires a sufficient number of events per variable to avoid overfitting.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small for reliable model estimation.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Exact logistic regression.
