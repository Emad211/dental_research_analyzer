Test name:

Purpose:
- Compare post-12-month root length (or thickness) between groups adjusting for baseline values.

Why appropriate (≥2 reasons):
1) ANCOVA is more efficient and less biased than using change scores when there is baseline variability; it adjusts for baseline differences.
2) Particularly helpful with small sample sizes to reduce residual variance and increase power.

Why it may not be appropriate:
- Assumes linear relationship between baseline and follow-up, and equal slopes (no baseline × group interaction).
- Requires approximate normality and homoscedasticity of residuals.

When NOT to use:
- If there is strong evidence of violation of linearity or heterogeneity of regression slopes (significant interaction baseline×group) and sample is too small to model it adequately.

Alternative if assumptions are violated:
- Rank ANCOVA (nonparametric ANCOVA) or perform analysis on change scores using robust methods.
- Generalized estimating equations (GEE) with identity link and robust SEs, clustering by tooth/patient.
