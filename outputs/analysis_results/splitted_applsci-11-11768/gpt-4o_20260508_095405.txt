### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Prospective longitudinal clinical study.
2. **Objective**: To evaluate preoperative factors affecting the outcome of surgical endodontic treatment.
3. **Study Setting**: Conducted at the Department of Operative Dentistry, Dow University of Health Sciences, Karachi, from July 2017 to December 2018.
4. **Ethical Approval**: Approved by the Institutional Review Board (IRB-862/DUHS/Approval/2017/50).
5. **Sample Size**:
   - Initially enrolled: 52 patients.
   - Excluded: 12 patients (7 healed after conventional re-root canal treatment; 5 lost to follow-up).
   - Final sample size: 40 patients underwent periapical surgery.
6. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Patients aged 15–57 years.
   - Chronic apical periodontitis of single-rooted anterior teeth.
   - Radiolucency >4 mm around the root apex.
   - Failed root canal treatment or open root apex due to trauma.
7. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Medically compromised patients (ASA III).
   - Pre-existing periodontal disease.
   - Lesions <4 mm.
   - Multirooted teeth, cracked teeth, or root fractures.
   - Pregnant or lactating women.
   - Patients lost to follow-up.
8. **Groups**:
   - No explicit grouping; all patients underwent periapical surgery.
9. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variables**: Age, smoking status, size of preoperative lesion, presence of preoperative signs and symptoms.
   - **Dependent Variable**: Periapical healing (clinical and radiographic outcomes).
10. **Measurements**:
    - **Clinical Parameters**: Pain, swelling, sinus tract, tenderness to palpation/percussion, mobility, discoloration.
    - **Radiographic Parameters**: Periapical Index (PAI) for healing assessment.
11. **Data Collection Methods**:
    - Preoperative digital radiographs using the parallel technique.
    - Follow-up evaluations at 6 and 12 months post-surgery.
12. **Intervention**:
    - Full-thickness mucoperiosteal flap reflection.
    - Retrograde cavity preparation using zirconia-coated ultrasonic tips.
    - Retrograde filling with mineral trioxide aggregate (MTA).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test or Fisher’s Exact Test.
   - **Purpose**: To assess associations between categorical variables (e.g., smoking status, presence of preoperative symptoms) and healing outcomes.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - The study involves categorical data (e.g., healed vs. not healed).
     - It evaluates relationships between independent variables and binary outcomes.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Small sample sizes may require Fisher’s Exact Test instead of Chi-Square.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts in contingency tables are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

2. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test or Mann-Whitney U Test.
   - **Purpose**: To compare continuous variables (e.g., lesion size) between healed and non-healed groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Lesion size is a continuous variable.
     - It evaluates differences between two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality for t-Test; Mann-Whitney U Test is non-parametric.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data distribution is non-normal for t-Test.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test.

3. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.
   - **Purpose**: To identify predictors of healing outcomes (e.g., smoking, lesion size, age).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Binary dependent variable (healed vs. not healed).
     - Allows adjustment for confounders.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires a sufficiently large sample size for reliable estimates.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is too small or multicollinearity exists.
   - **Alternative**: Penalized logistic regression.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.
   - **Purpose**: To evaluate time-dependent healing outcomes.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Longitudinal follow-up data (6 and 12 months).
     - Accounts for censored data (e.g., loss to follow-up).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires precise time-to-event data.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If healing is not recorded as a time-dependent event.
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.

2. **Test Name**: ANOVA or Kruskal-Wallis Test.
   - **Purpose**: To compare lesion size across multiple subgroups (e.g., age groups, smoking status).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Useful for comparing continuous variables across >2 groups.
     - Kruskal-Wallis is non-parametric.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality and homogeneity of variances for ANOVA.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If assumptions are violated.
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis Test.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Pearson or Spearman Correlation.
   - **Purpose**: To assess relationships between continuous variables (e.g., lesion size and age).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Evaluates linear or monotonic relationships.
     - Useful for exploratory analysis.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Does not account for confounding variables.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data is non-linear or heavily skewed.
   - **Alternative**: Partial correlation.

2. **Test Name**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).
   - **Purpose**: To analyze repeated measures data (e.g., clinical and radiographic outcomes at 6 and 12 months).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Accounts for within-subject correlations.
     - Suitable for longitudinal data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires advanced statistical expertise.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data is not longitudinal or clustered.
   - **Alternative**: Mixed-effects models.
