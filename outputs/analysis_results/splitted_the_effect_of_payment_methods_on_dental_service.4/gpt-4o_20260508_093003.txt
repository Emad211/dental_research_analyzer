### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**: Retrospective cohort study.
2. **Objective**: To describe the service mix of private general practitioners and investigate the association of service mix with the method of payment (salary vs. fee-for-service [FFS]) in one dental clinic.
3. **Groups**:
   - Dentists who worked in both payment methods (n = 36).
   - Dentists who worked only in the FFS method (n = 9).
   - Dentists who worked only in salaried employment (n = 9).
4. **Sample Size**:
   - Patient records: 85 records per dentist per payment period.
   - Total records: 3060.
5. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variable**: Payment method (salary vs. FFS).
   - **Dependent Variable**: Dental service mix categorized into four groups:
     1. Practices without treatments (visits, diagnostic radiography, referrals).
     2. Preventive treatments (simple restorations, scaling).
     3. Aggressive treatments (extraction, crown).
     4. Highly aggressive treatments (surgery).
6. **Measurements**:
   - Number of services provided in each category.
   - Frequency of each type of service.
7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Retrospective review of patient records from two time periods:
     - Salary period: October 1–30, 2018.
     - FFS period: March 1–30, 2019.
   - Records excluded if incomplete, ambiguous, or related to specialized services, patients under 18 years old, or charity services.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Chi-Square Test**
   - **Purpose**: To compare the distribution of dental service categories across payment methods.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The dependent variable (service mix) is categorical.
     2. The study aims to assess associations between payment methods and service mix.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes independence of observations, which may be violated if dentists are clustered within the clinic.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If expected frequencies in any cell of the contingency table are too low (<5).
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test for small sample sizes.

2. **Independent Samples t-Test or Mann-Whitney U Test**
   - **Purpose**: To compare the mean number of services provided per dentist between salary and FFS payment methods.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves comparing two independent groups (payment methods).
     2. The number of services is a continuous variable.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality of data distribution (t-test).
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If data are heavily skewed or have outliers.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test for non-parametric data.

3. **Paired Samples t-Test or Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test**
   - **Purpose**: To compare the service mix within dentists who worked in both payment methods.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves paired observations (same dentists under two payment methods).
     2. The number of services is a continuous variable.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality of differences (t-test).
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If differences are non-normally distributed or contain outliers.
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test for non-parametric data.
