### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

*   **Study Design:** Educational action research utilizing a mixed approach: a cross-sectional survey of students and a retrospective analysis of clinical database records.
*   **Objectives:** To assess the effectiveness of blended learning in conservative dentistry with endodontics during the COVID-19 pandemic.
*   **Participants / Groups:**
    *   *Survey Groups:* 4th-year dental students divided into two cohorts: (1) Students who had clinical exercises in their 3rd year, and (2) Students who took 3rd-year classes online only.
    *   *Clinical Data Groups:* Pre-pandemic period (October 2019–January 2020) vs. Pandemic period (October 2020–January 2021).
*   **Sample Size:** Not explicitly stated in the provided text (survey distributed to all 4th-year students; pre-validation done on 5 volunteers).
*   **Variables:**
    *   *Independent Variables:* Prior clinical experience (Yes/No), Time period (Pre-pandemic vs. Pandemic).
    *   *Dependent Variables:* Student perceptions/evaluations (ordinal and nominal), Standardized number of dental procedures performed.
*   **Measurements:**
    *   *Survey:* A 39-item author-designed questionnaire divided into 5 domains (self-evaluation, theoretical e-learning, practical clinical, safety, blended learning evaluation). Measured using a 5-point Likert scale and dichotomous (yes/no) scales.
    *   *Clinical Data:* Number of selected conservative dentistry and endodontic procedures.
*   **Data Collection Methods:**
    *   *Survey:* Online via Google Forms (closed and open questions).
    *   *Clinical Data:* Retrospective extraction from the university's patient database, standardized against the number of practicing student pairs per day in a given 4-week block.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY (Tests without which the analysis would be incomplete)

**1. Mann-Whitney U Test (Wilcoxon Rank-Sum Test)**
*   **Purpose:** To compare the 5-point Likert scale survey responses between the two student groups, and to compare the standardized number of clinical procedures between the pre-pandemic and pandemic periods.
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) Likert scale data is ordinal, making non-parametric testing ideal. (2) The groups being compared (Group 1 vs. Group 2; Pre-pandemic vs. Pandemic) are independent of each other.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It tests for differences in mean ranks, not medians or means directly; if the distributions of the two groups have entirely different shapes, interpretation becomes difficult.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When comparing paired or matched data (e.g., the same students tested before and after a course).
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Independent Samples t-test (if the data is transformed or aggregated into a continuous score that meets normality assumptions).

**2. Chi-Square Test of Independence (or Fisher’s Exact Test)**
*   **Purpose:** To compare the dichotomous (e.g., Yes/No) survey responses between the two student groups.
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) The dependent variable is categorical (nominal). (2) The observations are independent.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** Chi-square loses validity if the sample size is very small and expected cell frequencies fall below 5.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When the categorical data is paired (e.g., asking the exact same Yes/No question to the same students at two different times).
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Fisher’s Exact Test (mandatory if expected cell counts are < 5).

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED (Tests that enhance the quality and rigor of the analysis)

**1. Cronbach’s Alpha**
*   **Purpose:** To assess the internal consistency and reliability of the newly developed 39-item questionnaire.
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) The survey is an "author's questionnaire" and requires validation to prove the items reliably measure the intended constructs. (2) It is the standard metric for multi-item Likert scale domains.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It assumes unidimensionality; if a domain measures multiple underlying traits, alpha will be artificially deflated.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** For evaluating the dichotomous (Yes/No) questions on the survey.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** McDonald’s Omega (more robust if the assumption of tau-equivalence is violated) or Kuder-Richardson 20 (KR-20) for dichotomous items.

**2. Multidimensional Correspondence Analysis (MCA)** *(Note: Mentioned by authors)*
*   **Purpose:** To assess the complex, multivariate relationships between the categorical potential advantages/disadvantages of blended learning and the students' overall evaluations.
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) It is specifically designed to detect structural relationships among multiple categorical variables. (2) It provides a visual map (biplot) making complex survey data easier to interpret.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It can become highly cluttered and uninterpretable if too many variables or categories are included simultaneously.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When the primary goal is to predict an outcome (regression) rather than explore associations, or when dealing with continuous data.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Categorical Principal Component Analysis (CATPCA).

**3. Shapiro-Wilk Test**
*   **Purpose:** To formally test the normality of the standardized clinical procedure counts.
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) It justifies the authors' choice to use the non-parametric Mann-Whitney test for clinical data. (2) It is highly accurate for small to medium sample sizes.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** In very large sample sizes, it becomes overly sensitive and will flag trivial deviations from normality as significant.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** On the nominal or ordinal (Likert) survey data, as these are inherently non-normal.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Visual inspection using Q-Q plots and histograms.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL (Tests that provide supplementary insights)

**1. Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test**
*   **Purpose:** To compare responses between two different Likert-scale questions *within* the same group of students (e.g., comparing a student's rating of theoretical classes vs. their rating of practical classes).
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) Handles ordinal data effectively. (2) Accounts for the paired nature of within-subject responses.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It discards data if there are many "ties" (students giving the exact same rating to both questions).
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When comparing responses between the two different, independent student groups.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Sign Test (less powerful but does not assume symmetric distribution of differences).

**2. Spearman’s Rank-Order Correlation**
*   **Purpose:** To evaluate the strength and direction of association between different ordinal survey domains (e.g., does higher self-evaluation correlate with higher perceived safety?).
*   **Why appropriate:** (1) Does not assume linear relationships, only monotonic ones. (2) Perfectly suited for ordinal Likert scale data.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It only measures monotonic relationships; it will miss complex, non-monotonic associations.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When attempting to correlate a nominal variable (like gender) with an ordinal variable.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Kendall’s Tau (better for small data sets with many tied ranks).

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS

*   **Independent Samples t-test:** Should NOT be used for the individual 5-point Likert scale items. Likert items are ordinal, meaning the distance between "Agree" and "Strongly Agree" is not mathematically equal to the distance between "Neutral" and "Agree." A t-test assumes continuous, interval-level data.
*   **Pearson Correlation:** Should NOT be used to correlate survey items. Pearson assumes that both variables are continuous and normally distributed, which is violated by ordinal survey data.
*   **Analysis of Variance (ANOVA):** Should NOT be used. The study only compares two independent groups (Group 1 vs. Group 2; Pre-pandemic vs. Pandemic). ANOVA is designed for comparing three or more groups and assumes continuous, normally distributed data.

### TASK 4: SUMMARY

The overall analytical approach is highly appropriate for an educational action research study relying heavily on survey data and real-world clinical counts. Because the primary data sources are 5-point Likert scales (ordinal data) and standardized clinical procedure counts (which are typically skewed and non-normally distributed), a **non-parametric statistical framework** is the most rigorous choice. 

Using the Mann-Whitney U test allows for robust comparison between the two distinct student cohorts without violating the assumptions of parametric tests. Furthermore, the inclusion of Multidimensional Correspondence Analysis (MCA) is an excellent methodological choice by the authors, as it elevates the analysis from simple univariate comparisons to a sophisticated exploration of how multiple qualitative perceptions (advantages/disadvantages) interact in the context of blended learning. To ensure maximum rigor, validating the custom questionnaire with Cronbach's Alpha and utilizing Fisher's Exact test for dichotomous variables will solidify the reliability of the findings.
