### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Retrospective cohort study with a 10-year follow-up.

2. **Study Objectives**:  
   - **Primary Aim**: Evaluate the 10-year survival rate of root-filled teeth treated by general dental practitioners (GDPs) in Norrbotten, Sweden.  
   - **Secondary Aim**: Identify prognostic factors affecting tooth survival.

3. **Population and Sample**:  
   - **Initial Cohort**: 3,676 individuals who received root canal treatment in 2006.  
   - **Final Sample**: 280 individuals (randomly selected) treated by 102 GDPs across 28 clinics.  
   - **Exclusions**: 331 deceased individuals, 19 declined participation, and others excluded for reasons such as specialist involvement or missing data.  
   - **Demographics**: Mean age at baseline = 45 years (range: 10–80); 50% female.

4. **Groups**:  
   - No explicit intervention/control groups; survival analysis was conducted on a single cohort with stratification by independent variables.

5. **Outcome Variable**:  
   - **Primary Outcome**: Tooth survival (presence or absence of the root-filled tooth).  
   - **Time-to-Event Data**: Time (in days) from root canal treatment completion to tooth extraction or censoring.

6. **Independent Variables**:  
   - **Preoperative Factors**: Tooth type, apical status, pulp necrosis, marginal bone level, etc.  
   - **Intraoperative Factors**: Number of sessions, use of rubber dam, procedural accidents, etc.  
   - **Postoperative Factors**: Coronal restoration type, use of posts, abutment status, etc.

7. **Data Collection**:  
   - Data were extracted from dental records (analogue and digital) and supplemented by patient follow-ups (phone, mail, or clinical examination).  
   - Randomization was used to select patients and teeth for inclusion.  
   - Missing data were addressed by contacting patients or estimating extraction dates based on available records.

8. **Measurements**:  
   - Survival was assessed via radiographs, dental records, or patient reports.  
   - Independent variables were categorized and recorded (e.g., apical status, restoration type).  
   - Inter- and intra-examiner reliability was assessed during data collection.

9. **Statistical Methods Mentioned**:  
   - Kaplan-Meier survival analysis (Figure 2).  
   - Hazard ratios (HRs) for independent variables (Table 1).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis**  
   - **Purpose**: Estimate the survival probability of root-filled teeth over 10 years.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Time-to-event data is the primary outcome.  
     2. Censoring is present (e.g., teeth still retained at the end of follow-up).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes non-informative censoring, which may not hold if censored individuals differ systematically from those with observed events.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If time-to-event data is incomplete or unreliable.  
   - **Alternative**: Life table analysis if survival times are grouped into intervals.

2. **Cox Proportional Hazards Regression**  
   - **Purpose**: Identify prognostic factors affecting tooth survival and estimate hazard ratios.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Allows for adjustment of multiple covariates.  
     2. Handles censored data effectively.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes proportional hazards, which may not hold for all variables.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If proportional hazards assumption is violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Stratified Cox regression or time-varying covariates.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
3. **Log-Rank Test**  
   - **Purpose**: Compare survival curves between groups (e.g., based on tooth type or restoration type).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Non-parametric test suitable for comparing survival distributions.  
     2. Does not require proportional hazards assumption.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Sensitive to censoring patterns.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample sizes are very small in one or more groups.  
   - **Alternative**: Breslow or Tarone-Ware tests for weighted comparisons.

4. **Chi-Square Test or Fisher’s Exact Test** (for categorical variables)  
   - **Purpose**: Assess associations between categorical independent variables and tooth survival.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Simple and intuitive for binary outcomes.  
     2. Useful for exploratory analysis.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Does not account for time-to-event data.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are very low.  
   - **Alternative**: Logistic regression for binary outcomes.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
5. **Multivariable Logistic Regression**  
   - **Purpose**: Explore associations between independent variables and the binary outcome (tooth survival vs. extraction).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Adjusts for confounders.  
     2. Provides odds ratios for interpretation.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Ignores time-to-event data.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If time-to-event data is critical.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox regression.

6. **Sensitivity Analysis**  
   - **Purpose**: Assess robustness of findings (e.g., excluding individuals with missing data).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Addresses potential bias from missing data.  
     2. Enhances credibility of results.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires additional assumptions about missing data mechanisms.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If missing data is minimal.  
   - **Alternative**: Multiple imputation.

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **T-Test or ANOVA**  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: These tests are designed for continuous outcomes and do not account for time-to-event data or censoring.  
   - **Alternative**: Kaplan-Meier analysis or Cox regression.

2. **Pearson Correlation**  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: Correlation does not account for survival data or the binary nature of the outcome.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox regression or logistic regression.

3. **Linear Regression**  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: Assumes a continuous outcome and does not handle censoring.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox regression or logistic regression.
