### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Clinical trial.
2. **Objective**: To measure the impact of root canal treatment on oral health-related quality of life (OHRQoL) in single-visit versus multiple-visit treatments.
3. **Study Setting**: Dental teaching hospital, Makkah, Saudi Arabia.
4. **Study Period**: September 2019 to January 2020.
5. **Ethical Approval**: Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval (IRB number: 132-19).
6. **Participants**:
   - **Inclusion Criteria**: Patients aged 16–60 years diagnosed with symptomatic irreversible pulpitis and normal periapical tissue.
   - **Exclusion Criteria**: Cases with calcified canals, systemic diseases complicating root canal treatment (e.g., endocarditis), or any kind of resorption.
7. **Sample Size**:
   - Calculated to detect a mean difference of 8.0 in OHIP scores with SD = 6.0, power = 82%, and alpha = 0.05.
   - 16 participants per group (total = 32 participants).
8. **Randomization**: Patients were randomly assigned into two groups:
   - **Group 1**: Single-visit root canal treatment.
   - **Group 2**: Multiple-visit root canal treatment.
9. **Outcome Measure**: OHRQoL assessed using the OHIP-14 questionnaire (Arabic version).
   - **Scoring**: Each question scored 0–4, with a total score range of 0–56 (higher scores indicate worse OHRQoL).
10. **Data Collection**:
    - **Single-Visit Group**: OHIP-14 completed at baseline and at the second follow-up visit after obturation.
    - **Multiple-Visit Group**: OHIP-14 completed at baseline, after the first visit (access cavity and pulp extirpation), and after obturation.
    - Data collected via face-to-face interviews.
11. **Intervention**:
    - Root canal treatment performed by two undergraduate dental students under supervision.
    - Standardized protocol for anesthesia, isolation, working length determination, irrigation, preparation, and obturation.
    - Multiple-visit group received intracanal medication and temporary fillings between visits.
12. **Bias Reduction**:
    - Randomization to reduce selection bias.
    - Mixed-effect models used for analysis.
    - Standardized operator selection to reduce operator bias.
    - Interview forms used to reduce information bias.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon signed-rank test if non-normal).
   - **Purpose**: To compare pre- and post-treatment OHIP-14 scores within each group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The outcome variable (OHIP-14 score) is continuous.
     2. The study aims to assess within-group changes over time.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality of the difference scores, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the OHIP-14 score differences are non-normally distributed.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test.

2. **Test Name**: Independent t-test (or Mann-Whitney U test if non-normal).
   - **Purpose**: To compare the mean change in OHIP-14 scores between the single-visit and multiple-visit groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study compares two independent groups.
     2. The outcome variable is continuous (change in OHIP-14 scores).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes equal variances and normality, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the data are non-normal or variances are unequal.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Mann-Whitney U test or Welch’s t-test.

3. **Test Name**: Mixed-effects model.
   - **Purpose**: To account for repeated measures and assess the interaction between time and group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Accounts for within-subject correlations due to repeated measures.
     2. Can model the effect of group (single vs. multiple visits) and time simultaneously.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires advanced statistical expertise and software.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small to fit a reliable model.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Generalized estimating equations (GEE).
