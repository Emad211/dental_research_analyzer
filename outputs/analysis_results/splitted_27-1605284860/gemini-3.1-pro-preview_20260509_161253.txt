### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

As a biostatistician, I must address the authors' decision to dichotomize the VAS score (0-4 vs 5-10). Dichotomizing continuous/ordinal data leads to a significant loss of statistical power and variance. Therefore, the plan below addresses their stated methodology (binary outcome) while strongly recommending the analysis of the raw VAS scores.

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
*(Tests without which the analysis would be incomplete based on the authors' stated methodology).*

**1. Pearson Chi-Square Test of Independence**
*   **Purpose:** To compare the proportion of patients experiencing "Pain" vs. "No Pain" at 48 hours between Group 1 and Group 2.
*   **Why appropriate:**
    1. The authors defined their final outcome as a categorical binary variable (Pain vs. No Pain).
    2. The two groups (Regular vs. On-demand) are independent of each other.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** It does not account for baseline pain levels, which might heavily influence the 48-hour outcome.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When the data is paired/matched, or when expected cell frequencies are too low.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Fisher’s Exact Test (used if more than 20% of expected cell counts are less than 5).

**2. Independent Samples t-test (or Mann-Whitney U Test)**
*   **Purpose:** To compare baseline VAS scores and Age between the two groups to ensure randomization was successful (Baseline Equivalence).
*   **Why appropriate:**
    1. It is critical to prove that one group did not start with significantly more pain than the other.
    2. It validates the randomization process.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** If the baseline VAS scores are highly skewed, the mean (used in t-tests) is not a good measure of central tendency.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When comparing categorical baseline variables (e.g., Gender, Tooth type).
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Mann-Whitney U test (for non-normal continuous/ordinal data like VAS).

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
*(Tests that enhance the quality and rigor of the analysis, correcting for methodological weaknesses).*

**1. Mann-Whitney U Test (on RAW 48-hour VAS scores)**
*   **Purpose:** To compare the actual, un-dichotomized VAS scores (0-10) between the two groups at 48 hours.
*   **Why appropriate:**
    1. VAS is an ordinal/non-normally distributed continuous scale; non-parametric testing preserves the full spectrum of the data.
    2. It avoids the massive loss of statistical power caused by the authors' arbitrary dichotomization (e.g., treating a VAS of 4 and a VAS of 0 as the exact same outcome).
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** If the distributions of the two groups have completely different shapes, comparing medians becomes difficult to interpret clinically.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** If the data is normally distributed and meets all parametric assumptions (though rare for VAS).
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Independent Samples t-test (if raw VAS data is perfectly normally distributed).

**2. Binary Logistic Regression**
*   **Purpose:** To evaluate the effect of the analgesic group on the binary outcome (Pain vs. No Pain) while controlling for baseline VAS scores.
*   **Why appropriate:**
    1. Allows adjustment for baseline pain, which is the strongest predictor of post-operative pain.
    2. Provides an Odds Ratio (OR), which is highly interpretable for clinical decision-making.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** The sample size (N=60) is relatively small. If the number of patients experiencing "Pain" is very low, the model may overfit (violating the "10 events per variable" rule).
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When the dependent variable is continuous (e.g., raw VAS scores).
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test (to stratify by baseline pain categories instead of regression).

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
*(Tests that provide supplementary clinical insights).*

**1. Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test**
*   **Purpose:** To compare baseline VAS to 48-hour raw VAS *within* each group separately.
*   **Why appropriate:**
    1. Demonstrates whether the endodontic treatment itself successfully reduced pain from baseline in both groups.
    2. Accounts for the paired nature of pre- and post-treatment scores in the same individuals.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** The primary research question is between-group differences, not within-group changes.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** For comparing Group 1 against Group 2.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Paired Samples t-test (if the differences between paired scores are normally distributed).

**2. Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR) and Number Needed to Treat/Harm (NNT/NNH)**
*   **Purpose:** To translate the binary outcome findings into practical clinical metrics.
*   **Why appropriate:**
    1. Provides clinicians with a tangible metric (e.g., "How many patients do I need to prescribe regular acetaminophen to prevent one case of pain?").
    2. Highly recommended by CONSORT guidelines for clinical trials.
*   **Why it may not be appropriate:** If the Chi-Square test shows absolutely no statistically significant difference, calculating NNT can be misleading or yield a confidence interval that crosses infinity.
*   **When this test should NOT be used:** When analyzing continuous outcomes.
*   **Alternative if assumptions are violated:** Standard reporting of relative risk (RR) or Odds Ratios (OR).

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS

The following tests should **NOT** be used for the primary analysis of this study, and doing so would constitute a statistical error:

1.  **Independent Samples t-test or ANOVA for the Primary Outcome:** The authors explicitly defined their final outcome as categorical (0-4 = No Pain, 5-10 = Pain). You cannot run parametric tests comparing means on a binary categorical variable.
2.  **Paired t-test for Group Comparisons:** Group 1 and Group 2 consist of different individuals. Paired tests are only for matched samples or repeated measures on the same individuals.
3.  **Pearson Correlation:** The independent variable (Group) is nominal, and the dependent variable (Pain/No Pain) is nominal. Pearson correlation requires two continuous variables.
4.  **Repeated Measures ANOVA:** While VAS was measured twice (baseline and 48 hours), VAS data rarely meets the strict normality and sphericity assumptions required for RM-ANOVA, and the authors' dichotomization of the final score makes this impossible.

### TASK 4: SUMMARY

The overall analytical approach must first establish **baseline equivalence** to ensure the randomization was successful, utilizing t-tests/Mann-Whitney U for continuous demographics (Age, Baseline VAS) and Chi-Square for categorical demographics (Gender, Tooth type). 

For the **primary analysis**, because the authors explicitly chose to dichotomize their outcome into "Pain" vs. "No Pain", a **Chi-Square test of independence** is mandatory to answer their specific research question. However, from a biostatistical standpoint, dichotomizing a 10-point scale at an arbitrary cut-off of 4/5 destroys valuable data variance and reduces the power of the study. Therefore, it is highly recommended to "rescue" the data by analyzing the raw 48-hour VAS scores using a **Mann-Whitney U test**. Finally, utilizing **Binary Logistic Regression** will allow the researchers to control for baseline pain, providing a much more rigorous and clinically accurate evaluation of whether regular acetaminophen prescription is truly superior to on-demand usage.
