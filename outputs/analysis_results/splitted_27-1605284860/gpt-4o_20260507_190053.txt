### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Clinical trial (randomized, non-probability consecutive sampling).
2. **Objective**: To compare post-endodontic pain outcomes between two groups: acetaminophen prescribed regularly (6-hourly) versus acetaminophen taken on demand (SOS).
3. **Groups**:
   - **Group 1**: Regular acetaminophen prescription (6-hourly).
   - **Group 2**: On-demand acetaminophen (SOS).
4. **Sample Size**: 60 participants (determined using WHO sample size calculator with population proportions of 0.2 and 0.14, 95% confidence level, and 90% power).
5. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Patients aged 18–60 years.
   - Maxillary or mandibular first or second premolar with irreversible pulpitis.
   - Normal radiographic appearance of peri-apical tissues.
   - Negative response to pulp sensibility tests (cold, heat, electrical pulp testing).
6. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Patients <18 or >60 years.
   - Systemic disorders, sensitivity to acetaminophen, gastric or liver conditions, pregnancy.
   - Teeth unsuitable for restoration, severe periodontal disease, periapical radiolucency, swelling, or draining sinuses.
   - Patients taking analgesics within the previous 12 hours.
7. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variable**: Type of acetaminophen administration (regular prescription vs on-demand).
   - **Dependent Variable**: Pain score at 48 hours post-treatment (measured using Visual Analogue Scale [VAS]).
8. **Measurements**:
   - **Baseline**: VAS recorded before treatment.
   - **Outcome**: VAS recorded 48 hours post-treatment (0–4 = No Pain, 5–10 = Pain).
9. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - VAS scores collected via phone follow-up.
   - Radiographs used to verify canal working length.
10. **Intervention**:
    - Endodontic treatment performed by the principal investigator.
    - Canal irrigants: Sodium hypochlorite (5.25%), normal saline, EDTA.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test or Fisher’s Exact Test
   - **Purpose**: To compare the proportion of patients experiencing pain (VAS ≥5) between Group 1 and Group 2.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - The dependent variable (pain/no pain) is categorical.
     - The study involves two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes independence of observations; may not account for potential confounding variables.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected frequencies in any cell are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test (if sample size is small).

2. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test or Mann-Whitney U Test
   - **Purpose**: To compare mean VAS scores between Group 1 and Group 2.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - VAS scores are continuous and measured on an interval scale.
     - Two independent groups are being compared.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normal distribution of VAS scores; may not hold true.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If VAS scores are not normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test (non-parametric).

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Paired Samples t-Test or Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test
   - **Purpose**: To compare baseline VAS scores with 48-hour post-treatment VAS scores within each group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Evaluates the change in pain scores within each group.
     - Accounts for paired data (pre- and post-treatment scores).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normal distribution of differences; may not hold true.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If differences are not normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression
   - **Purpose**: To assess the effect of acetaminophen administration type on the likelihood of experiencing pain (VAS ≥5), adjusting for potential confounders (e.g., age, gender).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Dependent variable is binary (pain/no pain).
     - Allows adjustment for covariates.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires a large sample size for stable estimates.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is insufficient or multicollinearity exists.
   - **Alternative**: Stratified analysis or subgroup comparisons.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Analysis of Covariance (ANCOVA)
   - **Purpose**: To compare post-treatment VAS scores between groups while controlling for baseline VAS scores.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Adjusts for baseline differences in pain scores.
     - Provides more precise estimates of group differences.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes homogeneity of regression slopes; may not hold true.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If assumptions of linearity or homogeneity are violated.
   - **Alternative**: Non-parametric ANCOVA or stratified analysis.

2. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA
   - **Purpose**: To analyze changes in VAS scores over time (baseline vs 48 hours) across both groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Accounts for within-subject correlations over time.
     - Tests for interaction effects between time and group.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires normality and sphericity assumptions.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are not normally distributed or sphericity is violated.
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).
