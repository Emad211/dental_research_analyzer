### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### **Study Characteristics**
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Prospective, double-blind, randomized clinical trial (RCT).

2. **Objective**: 
   - To evaluate the combined effect of preoperative oral ibuprofen (800 mg) and dexamethasone (0.5 mg) on the success rate of inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB) in mandibular molars with symptomatic irreversible pulpitis (SIP).

3. **Study Groups**: 
   - Two groups:
     - **Intervention group**: Patients receiving a combination of ibuprofen and dexamethasone as premedication.
     - **Control group**: Likely receiving a placebo or standard treatment (not explicitly mentioned in the provided text but inferred from the study design).

4. **Sample Size**: 
   - 94 adult patients.

5. **Inclusion Criteria**: 
   - Adults (≥18 years old) with:
     - Moderate to severe pain (55–170 mm on the Heft-Parker Visual Analog Scale [VAS]).
     - Mandibular molars diagnosed with SIP (confirmed by electric pulp testing and cold testing).
     - Normal periapical radiographic appearance.
     - Good health (ASA class I or II).
     - No analgesics taken in the last 12 hours.

6. **Exclusion Criteria**: 
   - Patients under 18 years old.
   - Allergies/hypersensitivity to study medications or local anesthetics.
   - Pregnant or nursing women.
   - Immunocompromised patients.
   - Active pathosis in the injection area.
   - Patients unable to provide informed consent.

7. **Variables**:
   - **Primary outcome**: Success rate of IANB (likely measured by pain reduction on the VAS scale).
   - **Independent variables**: Intervention (ibuprofen + dexamethasone) vs. control.
   - **Covariates**: Baseline pain level, patient demographics (age, sex), and ASA classification.

8. **Measurements**:
   - Pain intensity measured using the Heft-Parker VAS (55–170 mm range for inclusion).
   - Clinical diagnosis of SIP confirmed by:
     - Electric pulp testing (Digitest; Parkell Inc).
     - Cold testing (Endo Ice 1,1,1,2 tetrafluoroethane; Coltene).

9. **Randomization**:
   - Patients were randomly assigned to groups (details of randomization process not provided in the text).

10. **Blinding**:
    - Double-blind design (both patients and evaluators were blinded to group allocation).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### **CATEGORY A – MANDATORY**
1. **Test Name**: Chi-square test or Fisher’s exact test.
   - **Purpose**: To compare the success rates of IANB (categorical outcome: success vs. failure) between the intervention and control groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The primary outcome (success rate) is categorical.
     2. The study involves two independent groups (intervention vs. control).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the sample size is small or if expected frequencies in any cell are less than 5, the Chi-square test may not be valid.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - When assumptions of the Chi-square test (e.g., minimum expected frequency) are violated.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Fisher’s exact test.

2. **Test Name**: Independent t-test or Mann-Whitney U test.
   - **Purpose**: To compare the mean pain reduction (continuous variable) between the two groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Pain reduction is a continuous variable (measured on the VAS scale).
     2. The study involves two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the data are not normally distributed (for t-test).
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - When normality assumptions for the t-test are violated.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Mann-Whitney U test (non-parametric alternative).

3. **Test Name**: Paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test.
   - **Purpose**: To assess within-group changes in pain scores (pre- vs. post-treatment).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Pain scores are continuous and measured before and after treatment.
     2. The test accounts for paired data (within-group comparison).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the data are not normally distributed (for paired t-test).
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - When normality assumptions for the paired t-test are violated.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**:
     - Wilcoxon signed-rank test.

   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
