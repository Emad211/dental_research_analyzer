### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Randomized clinical trial (RCT).  
   - Double-blinded (observer and statistician blinded).  

2. **Objective**:  
   - To evaluate the effect of intracanal cryotherapy (cold irrigants) on postendodontic pain and interleukin-6 (IL-6) expression using different irrigation protocols.

3. **Participants**:  
   - Total sample size: 48 patients.  
   - Inclusion criteria:  
     - Age: 20–50 years.  
     - Symptomatic apical periodontitis (AP) with preoperative pain score >7 on the visual analog scale (VAS).  
   - Exclusion criteria:  
     - Periapical abscess.  
     - Use of immunosuppressive chemotherapy, anti-inflammatory drugs, or antibiotics in the past 2 months.  
     - Medically compromised patients.  
     - Pregnant or lactating females.  

4. **Randomization**:  
   - Patients were randomly assigned to one of four groups (12 patients per group) using opaque, sealed envelopes.  

5. **Groups**:  
   - **Group I (Control)**: Irrigation with 5% sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) at room temperature (RT) throughout the procedure.  
   - **Group II**: Irrigation with 5% cold NaOCl (2°C–5°C) throughout the procedure.  
   - **Group III**: Irrigation with 5% NaOCl at RT throughout the procedure, followed by a final rinse with 20 mL of 5% cold NaOCl (2°C–5°C) for 5 minutes.  
   - **Group IV**: Irrigation with 5% NaOCl at RT throughout the procedure, followed by a final rinse with 20 mL of cold saline (2°C–5°C) for 5 minutes.  

6. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variable**: Irrigation protocol (temperature and type of irrigant).  
   - **Dependent Variables**:  
     - Postendodontic pain (measured using VAS).  
     - IL-6 expression (biological marker for inflammation).  

7. **Measurements**:  
   - **Postendodontic Pain**: Assessed using the VAS, which measures pain intensity on a scale of 0–10.  
   - **IL-6 Expression**: Not explicitly described in the provided text, but likely measured using a laboratory assay (e.g., ELISA or qPCR).  

8. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Pain scores were recorded preoperatively and postoperatively (specific time points not provided in the text).  
   - IL-6 levels were measured as a marker of inflammation (details on sample collection and analysis not provided).  

9. **Ethical Considerations**:  
   - Approved by the Ethics Committee of the Faculty of Dentistry, Suez Canal University (56/2017).  
   - Registered on ClinicalTrials.gov (ID: NCT04324398).  
   - Informed consent obtained from all participants.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare postendodontic pain and IL-6 levels across the four groups over time.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The study involves repeated measurements (e.g., preoperative and postoperative pain/IL-6 levels).  
     2. It accounts for within-subject correlations and between-group differences.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality of residuals and sphericity, which may not hold.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are non-normal or if sphericity is violated.  
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Non-parametric equivalent (e.g., Friedman test for within-group comparisons, Kruskal-Wallis test for between-group comparisons).  

2. **Test Name**: One-Way ANOVA (or Kruskal-Wallis Test).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare mean pain scores or IL-6 levels between the four groups at a single time point.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The independent variable (irrigation protocol) has four levels.  
     2. The dependent variables (pain, IL-6) are continuous.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and homogeneity of variances.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are non-normal or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Kruskal-Wallis test.  

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Post-Hoc Pairwise Comparisons (e.g., Tukey’s HSD or Bonferroni).  
   - **Purpose**: To identify specific group differences if the overall ANOVA is significant.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides detailed insights into which irrigation protocols differ.  
     2. Adjusts for multiple comparisons to control Type I error.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Increases complexity and may reduce power if sample size is small.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If overall ANOVA is not significant.  
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Pairwise Mann-Whitney U tests with Bonferroni correction.  

2. **Test Name**: Effect Size Calculation (e.g., Partial Eta-Squared).  
   - **Purpose**: To quantify the magnitude of differences between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides a measure of practical significance.  
     2. Complements p-values, which only indicate statistical significance.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: May not be meaningful if the sample size is very small.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are highly skewed or non-normal.  
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Non-parametric effect size measures (e.g., rank-biserial correlation).  

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Correlation Analysis (e.g., Pearson or Spearman).  
   - **Purpose**: To explore the relationship between IL-6 levels and pain scores.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Both variables are continuous.  
     2. May reveal insights into the biological mechanism of cryotherapy.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linearity (for Pearson correlation).  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are non-linear or if there are outliers.  
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated**: Spearman’s rank correlation.
