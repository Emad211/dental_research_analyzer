### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

**Study Characteristics:**

1. **Study Design:**
   - Randomized Controlled Clinical Trial (RCT).

2. **Objective:**
   - To evaluate the disinfectant effect of a 445-nm diode laser as an adjunctive therapy for reducing bacterial load in root canals after conventional disinfection.

3. **Study Groups:**
   - Group A: Additional rinsing with sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl).
   - Group B: Laser irradiation with a 445-nm diode laser.
   - Group C: Combination of NaOCl rinsing and laser irradiation.

4. **Sample Size:**
   - Total: 57 patients.
   - Group size: 19 patients per group.

5. **Inclusion Criteria:**
   - Patients aged ≥18 years.
   - Teeth with irreversible pulp disease but endodontically preservable root structure.
   - Written informed consent.

6. **Exclusion Criteria:**
   - Acute pain after pulp removal.
   - Antibiotic use in the past 6 months.
   - Periodontal-endodontic lesions.
   - Previous endodontic treatment of the affected tooth.
   - Pregnancy.

7. **Variables:**
   - **Primary Outcome Variable:** Total bacterial load (TBL) in genome-equivalent CFUs/mL.
   - **Secondary Variables:** Presence of specific bacterial species (e.g., *Enterococcus faecalis*, *Porphyromonas gingivalis*, etc.).

8. **Measurements:**
   - Microbiological samples collected using sterile paper points at three time points:
     1. Baseline (after removal of temporary filling).
     2. Post-chemomechanical preparation.
     3. Post-adjuvant disinfection.
   - Quantitative real-time PCR (qPCR) used for bacterial analysis.

9. **Data Collection Methods:**
   - Random assignment of patients to groups.
   - Standardized treatment protocol for all groups.
   - Microbiological samples sent to an external laboratory for analysis.

10. **Interventions:**
    - Chemomechanical preparation with NaOCl and EDTA.
    - Group-specific disinfection protocols:
      - NaOCl rinsing (Group A).
      - Laser irradiation (Group B).
      - Combination of NaOCl and laser (Group C).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name:** Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model)
   - **Purpose:** To compare bacterial load reduction across the three groups over three time points.
   - **Why Appropriate:**
     1. The study involves repeated measurements (baseline, post-chemomechanical, post-adjuvant disinfection).
     2. The design includes multiple groups and time-dependent changes.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Assumes normality and sphericity, which may not hold for bacterial load data.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If data are non-normal or sphericity is violated.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated:** Non-parametric Friedman test or Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).

2. **Test Name:** Post-hoc Pairwise Comparisons (e.g., Tukey’s HSD or Bonferroni)
   - **Purpose:** To identify which groups differ significantly in bacterial load reduction.
   - **Why Appropriate:**
     1. Necessary to interpret significant group effects.
     2. Controls for Type I error in multiple comparisons.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** May lack power if sample size is small.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If the primary test (e.g., ANOVA) is not significant.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated:** Pairwise Wilcoxon rank-sum tests with Bonferroni correction.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name:** Multivariate Analysis of Variance (MANOVA)
   - **Purpose:** To assess the combined effect of group and time on multiple bacterial species.
   - **Why Appropriate:**
     1. Accounts for correlations among bacterial species.
     2. Provides a more holistic view of treatment effects.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Requires multivariate normality, which may not hold.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If bacterial species data are highly skewed or sparse.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated:** Canonical correlation analysis or GEE.

2. **Test Name:** Effect Size Analysis (e.g., Partial Eta Squared or Cohen’s d)
   - **Purpose:** To quantify the magnitude of treatment effects.
   - **Why Appropriate:**
     1. Complements p-values by providing practical significance.
     2. Helps compare the efficacy of interventions.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Does not account for variability in small sample sizes.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If primary tests are inconclusive.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated:** Bootstrap confidence intervals for effect sizes.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name:** Logistic Regression
   - **Purpose:** To assess the likelihood of detecting specific bacterial species post-treatment.
   - **Why Appropriate:**
     1. Suitable for binary outcomes (presence/absence of bacteria).
     2. Adjusts for potential confounders (e.g., age, tooth type).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Requires large sample sizes for stable estimates.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If bacterial species are rare or data are sparse.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated:** Exact logistic regression.

2. **Test Name:** Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis
   - **Purpose:** To evaluate symptom-free survival time post-treatment.
   - **Why Appropriate:**
     1. Useful for time-to-event data (e.g., symptom recurrence).
     2. Accounts for censored observations.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Not directly related to bacterial load outcomes.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If no time-to-event data are available.
   - **Alternative if Assumptions Are Violated:** Cox proportional hazards model.
