### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Retrospective observational study.

2. **Objective**:  
   - To assess the quality of root canal fillings and the frequency of procedural errors in endodontic treatment performed by undergraduate female dental students at Riyadh Elm University.

3. **Study Population**:  
   - Radiographs of 306 anterior and posterior teeth treated by female undergraduate dental students (4th, 5th, and 6th year) at Al Namuthajya Campus, Riyadh Elm University, between 2013 and 2019.

4. **Sampling Method**:  
   - Random selection of cases from patient charts.

5. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Postendodontically treated teeth with complete treatment records and radiographs (preoperative, working length determination, and postoperative radiographs).

6. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
   - Teeth with incomplete treatment, missing or poor-quality radiographs, or radiographs with anatomical superimposition preventing proper evaluation.

7. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variables**:  
     - Year of study (4th, 5th, 6th year).  
     - Tooth type (anterior or posterior).  
   - **Dependent Variables**:  
     - Quality of root canal filling (acceptable or unacceptable).  
     - Types of procedural errors (overfilling, underfilling, inadequate density).  

8. **Outcome Measures**:  
   - **Acceptable RCT**: Uniform density, no voids, 0–2 mm from apex, tapered filling.  
   - **Unacceptable RCT**: Overfilling, underfilling (≥2 mm short of apex), or inadequate density (voids in filling mass or between filling and canal walls).

9. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Radiographic evaluation using SIDEXIS XG and Dentalplus software.  
   - Evaluation criteria based on European Society of Endodontology guidelines.  
   - Independent assessment by two senior endodontists with ≥5 years of experience.  
   - Inter- and intra-examiner reliability assessed using kappa statistics (kappa = 0.89).

10. **Sample Size**:  
    - 306 teeth.

   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not inferential; does not test hypotheses.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: N/A (always needed for summarizing data).  
   - **Alternative**: None.

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test of Independence  
   - **Purpose**: Assess the association between categorical variables (e.g., year of study, tooth type, and quality of RCT).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Suitable for testing relationships between categorical variables.  
     2. Non-parametric, does not assume normality.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Small expected frequencies in contingency tables may violate assumptions.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When >20% of expected counts are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test (for small sample sizes).

3. **Test Name**: Kappa Statistic  
   - **Purpose**: Assess inter- and intra-examiner reliability.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Measures agreement beyond chance.  
     2. Already reported in the study (kappa = 0.89).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not applicable for continuous data or when only one rater is involved.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When there is no variability in ratings.  
   - **Alternative**: Weighted kappa or intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC) for ordinal/continuous data.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression  
   - **Purpose**: Model the probability of acceptable vs. unacceptable RCT as a function of predictors (e.g., year of study, tooth type).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Handles binary outcome variables.  
     2. Allows adjustment for multiple predictors simultaneously.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires a sufficient sample size for stable estimates.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When sample size is too small for the number of predictors.  
   - **Alternative**: Exact logistic regression.

2. **Test Name**: Mann-Whitney U Test  
   - **Purpose**: Compare continuous variables (e.g., patient age) between two groups (e.g., acceptable vs. unacceptable RCT).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Non-parametric, does not assume normality.  
     2. Suitable for small sample sizes.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Less powerful than parametric tests if data are normally distributed.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: For more than two groups.  
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test for >2 groups.

1. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (Stratified Chi-Square Tests)  
   - **Purpose**: Explore differences in RCT quality within subgroups (e.g., anterior vs. posterior teeth).

   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Increases the risk of Type I error due to multiple comparisons.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When subgroup sizes are too small.  
   - **Alternative**: Combine subgroups or use a multivariate model.

2. **Test Name**: Multinomial Logistic Regression  
   - **Purpose**: Model the probability of different types of errors (e.g., overfilling, underfilling, inadequate density) as a function of predictors.

   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires a larger sample size for stable estimates.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When sample size is insufficient.
