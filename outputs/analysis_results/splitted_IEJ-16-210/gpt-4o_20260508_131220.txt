### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Double-blind randomized clinical trial.
2. **Objective**: To evaluate the effect of adding clonidine to articaine/epinephrine on post-operative pain relief in patients with irreversible pulpitis.
3. **Groups**:
   - **Case Group**: Received 0.2 mL clonidine (30 µg) + 1.8 mL 4% articaine with 1:100,000 epinephrine.
   - **Control Group**: Received 0.2 mL distilled water + 1.8 mL 4% articaine with 1:100,000 epinephrine.
4. **Sample Size**:
   - Total: 100 patients (50 per group).
   - Excluded: 5 patients during eligibility screening.
   - Final analysis: 98 patients (49 per group after exclusions due to failed blocks or analgesic consumption).
5. **Variables**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Post-operative pain intensity measured using the Heft-Parker Visual Analog Scale (VAS) at 6, 12, 24, 48, and 72 hours.
   - **Secondary Outcomes**: Adverse effects (nausea, vomiting, dizziness, drowsiness, headache), analgesic consumption, and blood pressure/heart rate changes.
6. **Measurements**:
   - Pain intensity: VAS categorized into no pain (0 mm), mild pain (>0 mm to ≤54 mm), moderate pain (>54 mm to <114 mm), and severe pain (≥114 mm).
   - Blood pressure and heart rate: Measured before and 5 minutes after anesthetic administration.
   - Adverse effects: Self-reported via phone/text follow-ups.
7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Pain assessment: VAS at multiple time points (6, 12, 24, 48, and 72 hours).
   - Adverse effects and analgesic consumption: Follow-up phone calls/text messages.
   - Randomization: Dice roll (odd/even) to allocate patients to groups.
   - Double-blinding: Anesthetic solutions prepared and encoded by a third party; both patients and investigators were blinded.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-test (or Mann-Whitney U test if non-normal distribution).
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean post-operative pain scores between the case and control groups at each time point.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - The study involves two independent groups (case and control).
     - Pain scores are continuous variables suitable for parametric or non-parametric analysis.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality and homogeneity of variance; may be violated.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If pain scores are heavily skewed or sample sizes are unequal.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test for non-parametric data.

2. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Friedman test if non-normal distribution).
   - **Purpose**: Assess changes in pain scores over time within each group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Pain scores are measured at multiple time points (6, 12, 24, 48, and 72 hours).
     - Suitable for within-group comparisons of continuous data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes sphericity; may be violated.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are non-normal or sphericity assumption fails.
   - **Alternative**: Friedman test for non-parametric data.

3. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test for small sample sizes).
   - **Purpose**: Compare categorical variables (e.g., adverse effects) between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Adverse effects are categorical (presence/absence).
     - Suitable for comparing proportions between independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Small sample sizes may lead to inaccurate results.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Mixed-Effects Model.
   - **Purpose**: Analyze pain scores over time while accounting for repeated measures and group effects.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Accounts for both within-subject (time) and between-subject (group) variability.
     - Handles missing data effectively.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires advanced statistical software and expertise.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data collection is incomplete or assumptions of linearity fail.
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).

2. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.
   - **Purpose**: Assess predictors of adverse effects (binary outcome).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Suitable for binary outcomes (presence/absence of adverse effects).
     - Can include covariates (e.g., age, gender).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires large sample size for reliable estimates.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If adverse effects are rare (<10% prevalence).
   - **Alternative**: Exact Logistic Regression.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.
   - **Purpose**: Evaluate time to pain resolution (VAS = 0 mm) in each group.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Suitable for time-to-event data.
     - Provides insights into pain resolution trends.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Pain resolution may not be consistently reported.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If censoring is excessive or data are incomplete.
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.

2. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (e.g., stratified by age or gender).
   - **Purpose**: Explore differences in outcomes within demographic subgroups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Provides deeper insights into potential effect modifiers.
     - Useful for hypothesis generation.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Reduces statistical power due to smaller sample sizes.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If subgroup sizes are too small (<10 per group).
   - **Alternative**: Pooling subgroups or meta-regression.
