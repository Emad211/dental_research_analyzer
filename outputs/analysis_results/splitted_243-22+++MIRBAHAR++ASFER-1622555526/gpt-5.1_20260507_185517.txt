1.2 Baseline comparability tests  
- Name:

- Purpose: Evaluate whether patients/teeth treated in the two units differ systematically in baseline factors that could bias outcome comparisons.  
- Why appropriate:
  1) Groups are independent (different units) and mostly categorical variables; chi-square is the standard for such comparisons.  
  2) For continuous variables, t-test or Mann–Whitney allows assessment of distribution differences between two independent groups.  
- Why it may not be appropriate:
  - If sample sizes are small and expected cell counts <5 for many categories, chi-square assumptions are violated.  
  - If continuous variables are highly skewed or contain outliers, t-test assumptions fail.  
- When this should NOT be used:
  - Avoid chi-square if >20–25% of expected counts are <5; avoid t-test if sample size is very small and normality clearly violated.  
- Alternatives:
  - Fisher’s exact test instead of chi-square.  
  - Mann–Whitney U test instead of t-test for skewed continuous data.
