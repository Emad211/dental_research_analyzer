### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Single-blind, randomized controlled clinical trial.
2. **Objective**: To compare the effects of Endoseal MTA (bioceramic sealer) and AH Plus (resin-based sealer) on the incidence and intensity of postoperative pain after nonsurgical root canal treatment.
3. **Groups**:
   - Group 1 (Anterior teeth/premolars): Subgroups 1En (Endoseal MTA) and 1AH (AH Plus).
   - Group 2 (Molars): Subgroups 2En (Endoseal MTA) and 2AH (AH Plus).
4. **Sample Size**:
   - Total enrolled: 108 patients.
   - Group 1: 41 randomized (22 to Endoseal MTA, 19 to AH Plus); analyzed: 17 (Endoseal MTA) and 15 (AH Plus).
   - Group 2: 47 randomized (24 to Endoseal MTA, 23 to AH Plus); analyzed: 18 (Endoseal MTA) and 17 (AH Plus).
5. **Variables**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Postoperative pain intensity (VAS scores) at rest and while biting over 7 days.
   - **Secondary Outcome**: Incidence of postoperative pain.
   - **Covariates**: Preoperative pain intensity, tooth type (anterior/premolar vs molar), obturation technique (single-cone vs continuous-wave), and patient demographics (age, gender).
6. **Measurements**:
   - Pain intensity: Visual Analog Scale (VAS) scores (0–10 scale).
   - Pain assessment: Preoperative (baseline) and postoperative (daily for 7 days).
7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Randomization: Block randomization.
   - Pain assessment: Self-reported VAS scores recorded daily and at the 7-day recall visit.
   - Treatment standardization: Single practitioner performed all procedures.
   - Follow-up: Recall visit on day 7.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test (or Mann-Whitney U Test if assumptions are violated)
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean VAS pain scores between Endoseal MTA and AH Plus groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - The study aims to compare pain intensity between two independent groups.
     - VAS scores are continuous variables suitable for parametric or non-parametric comparison.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normality and homogeneity of variance, which may not hold for VAS scores.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If VAS scores are highly skewed or sample sizes are small.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test)
   - **Purpose**: Compare the incidence of postoperative pain between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Incidence is a categorical variable (presence/absence of pain).
     - Suitable for analyzing proportions in independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires sufficient sample size in each cell; may be inappropriate for small subgroups.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If expected frequencies in any cell are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

3. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Friedman Test if assumptions are violated)
   - **Purpose**: Analyze changes in VAS scores over time (days 1–7) within and between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Accounts for repeated measurements of pain intensity over time.
     - Suitable for comparing trends between groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes sphericity and normality of residuals.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If data violates sphericity or normality assumptions.
   - **Alternative**: Friedman Test (non-parametric).

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Mixed-Effects Model
   - **Purpose**: Account for both fixed effects (group, time) and random effects (patient variability).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Handles unbalanced data due to dropouts.
     - Accounts for repeated measures and inter-patient variability.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires advanced statistical software and expertise.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If sample size is insufficient for model convergence.
   - **Alternative**: Repeated Measures ANOVA.

2. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression
   - **Purpose**: Assess predictors of postoperative pain incidence (categorical outcome).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Allows adjustment for covariates (e.g., preoperative pain, tooth type).
     - Suitable for binary outcomes.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires sufficient events per predictor variable.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If sample size is insufficient for reliable estimates.
   - **Alternative**: Chi-Square Test with stratification.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (e.g., stratified t-tests or ANOVA)
   - **Purpose**: Explore differences within subgroups (e.g., anterior/premolar vs molar).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Provides insights into subgroup-specific effects.
     - May reveal differential effects of sealers by tooth type.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Reduces statistical power due to smaller subgroup sample sizes.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If subgroup sample sizes are too small.
   - **Alternative**: Pooled analysis with interaction terms.

2. **Test Name**: Correlation Analysis (e.g., Spearman’s Rank Correlation)
   - **Purpose**: Assess relationship between preoperative pain and postoperative pain intensity.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Explores potential predictors of postoperative pain.
     - Suitable for ordinal or non-normally distributed data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Does not account for confounding variables.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If data is dichotomized (presence/absence of pain).
   - **Alternative**: Logistic Regression.

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Paired t-Test
   - **Why Inappropriate**: Groups are independent, not paired.
2. **Test Name**: One-Way ANOVA
   - **Why Inappropriate**: Does not account for repeated measures or time effects.
3. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis
   - **Why Inappropriate**: Not relevant for pain intensity or incidence outcomes.
