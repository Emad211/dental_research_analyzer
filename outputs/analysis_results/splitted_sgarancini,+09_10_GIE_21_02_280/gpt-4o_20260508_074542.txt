### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Prospective, three-arm, parallel-group, single-blind, single-center, randomized clinical trial.

2. **Objective**:  
   - To compare the effects of depotphoresis and diode laser (DL) on microbial count, postoperative pain, and healing of periapical lesions after 6 months using CBCT.

3. **Study Groups**:  
   - **Depotphoresis group**: Cupral paste with electrical activation.  
   - **Diode Laser (DL) group**: 940 nm diode laser irradiation.  
   - **Control group**: Standard root canal treatment with calcium hydroxide as medicament.

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - 90 patients (30 per group).  
   - Final analysis included 26 (depotphoresis), 25 (DL), and 16 (control) due to dropouts.

5. **Population**:  
   - Adults aged 18–60 years with pulp necrosis and chronic apical periodontitis.  
   - Exclusion criteria: diabetic, immunocompromised, pregnant, recent analgesic/antibiotic use, periodontal disease, or inability to isolate the tooth with a rubber dam.

6. **Randomization and Blinding**:  
   - Simple randomization using an online system.  
   - Patient blinding was implemented.  
   - Independent, blinded examiners evaluated outcomes.

7. **Variables**:  
   - **Primary outcomes**:  
     - Microbial load (CFU/ml) at three time points: pre-treatment (S1), post-treatment (S2), and after 6 months (S3).  
     - Postoperative pain (VAS scores at 6, 12, 24, 48, and 72 hours).  
     - Healing of periapical lesions (CBCT measurements of lesion diameter in apicocoronal, mesiodistal, and buccolingual directions).  
   - **Secondary outcomes**: None explicitly stated.

8. **Measurements and Data Collection**:  
   - **Microbial load**: Colony-forming unit (CFU) counting from samples collected at S1, S2, and S3.  
   - **Postoperative pain**: Visual Analog Scale (VAS) scores.  
   - **Healing**: CBCT scans analyzed for lesion diameter at baseline and 6 months.  

9. **Interventions**:  
   - **Depotphoresis group**: Cupral paste with electrical activation (0.8–1.5 mA for 5 minutes).  
   - **DL group**: 940 nm diode laser irradiation in pulse mode (2 W power, 20 s irradiation, repeated 3 times).  
   - **Control group**: Calcium hydroxide medicament without adjunctive techniques.

10. **Statistical Considerations**:  
    - Sample size calculation based on an effect size of 0.82, power of 80%, and significance level of 0.05.  
    - Dropout rates were accounted for in the initial sample size.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model)**  
   - **Purpose**: To compare microbial load (CFU/ml) across the three groups over three time points (S1, S2, S3).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The outcome (CFU/ml) is continuous and measured repeatedly over time.  
     2. It accounts for within-subject correlations.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and sphericity of data.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are non-normal or sphericity is violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Friedman test (non-parametric) or Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).

2. **Two-Way ANOVA**  
   - **Purpose**: To compare lesion diameter changes (apicocoronal, mesiodistal, buccolingual) across groups and time points.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Continuous outcome (lesion diameter).  
     2. Allows interaction analysis between group and time.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires normality and homogeneity of variance.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test for group comparisons; Wilcoxon signed-rank test for paired data.

3. **Repeated Measures ANOVA for VAS Scores**  
   - **Purpose**: To compare postoperative pain (VAS scores) across groups and time points.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Continuous outcome (VAS scores).  
     2. Accounts for repeated measures over time.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and sphericity.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are non-normal or sphericity is violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Friedman test or GEE.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test)**  
   - **Purpose**: To compare dropout rates across groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Categorical outcome (dropout: yes/no).  
     2. Tests for group differences in attrition.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Small sample sizes in some groups.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

2. **Effect Size Calculation**  
   - **Purpose**: To quantify the magnitude of differences between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides clinically meaningful interpretation.  
     2. Complements p-values.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not a statistical test; requires post hoc calculations.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are unreliable.  
   - **Alternative**: None.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Survival Analysis (Kaplan-Meier)**  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze time to healing of periapical lesions.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for time-dependent outcomes.  
     2. Handles censored data (e.g., patients lost to follow-up).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires precise time-to-event data.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If healing time is not recorded.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox proportional hazards model.

2. **Correlation Analysis**  
   - **Purpose**: To assess relationships between microbial load reduction and lesion healing.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Explores potential associations.  
     2. Adds depth to findings.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linear relationships.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are non-linear or non-normal.  
   - **Alternative**: Spearman’s rank correlation.
