### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Retrospective observational study.
2. **Objective**: To assess the quality of root canal (RC) filling before and after RC re-treatment and evaluate changes in periapical status.
3. **Groups**: 
   - Baseline radiographs (before re-treatment).
   - Follow-up radiographs (6 months after re-treatment).
4. **Sample Size**: 238 radiographs of failed endodontic treatments.
5. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variables**: Quality of RC filling (density, length, homogeneity).
   - **Dependent Variables**: Periapical status (presence or absence of lesion).
6. **Measurements**:
   - **Quality of RC Filling**: Scored 1–6 based on density, length, and homogeneity (Unal et al.).
   - **Periapical Status**: Evaluated using Periapical Index (PAI) scoring system (1–5 scale).
     - PAI <3: Absence of periapical lesion.
     - PAI >3: Presence of periapical lesion.
7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Digital intraoral periapical radiographs (IOPARs) taken with Schick sensor using Gnatus intraoral radiographic unit (72 kVp, 8 mA, exposure time 0.30–0.50 sec).
   - Rinn XCP holder used for sensor positioning.
   - Baseline and follow-up radiographs compared.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon signed-rank test for non-normal data).
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean scores of RC filling quality and PAI scores before and after re-treatment.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - The study involves paired data (baseline vs. follow-up radiographs).
     - Continuous variables (quality scores and PAI scores) are being analyzed.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes normal distribution of differences; if violated, non-parametric alternative is needed.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are ordinal or non-normal.
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon signed-rank test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-square test (or Fisher’s exact test for small sample sizes).
   - **Purpose**: Assess association between RC filling quality categories and periapical lesion presence/absence.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Both variables are categorical (quality categories and lesion presence).
     - Tests for independence between two categorical variables.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires sufficient sample size for expected cell counts.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s exact test.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Logistic regression.
   - **Purpose**: Model the probability of periapical lesion presence (PAI >3) based on RC filling quality scores.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Dependent variable is binary (presence/absence of lesion).
     - Allows adjustment for potential confounders (e.g., age, gender).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes linear relationship between predictors and log odds.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If predictors are highly collinear.
   - **Alternative**: Ridge regression or penalized logistic regression.

2. **Test Name**: Intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC).
   - **Purpose**: Assess reliability of PAI scoring across radiographs.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Evaluates consistency of scoring between raters or across time.
     - Suitable for continuous or ordinal data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires multiple raters or repeated measurements.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If only one rater is involved.
   - **Alternative**: Cohen’s kappa (for categorical data).

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve analysis.
   - **Purpose**: Evaluate diagnostic accuracy of RC filling quality scores in predicting periapical lesion presence.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Provides insight into sensitivity and specificity of quality scores.
     - Useful for threshold determination.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires binary classification of outcomes.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If outcome is not binary.
   - **Alternative**: Precision-recall curve (for imbalanced datasets).

2. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier survival analysis.
   - **Purpose**: Analyze time-to-event data (e.g., time to lesion resolution).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Suitable for longitudinal follow-up data.
     - Accounts for censored observations.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires time-to-event data, which may not be available.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If time-to-event data are not collected.
   - **Alternative**: Cox proportional hazards model.
