### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Blinded, parallel, randomized controlled trial (RCT) following CONSORT 2010 guidelines.
   - Allocation ratio: 4:1.

2. **Study Objectives**:  
   - **Primary Objective**: To evaluate and compare postendodontic pain in patients undergoing single-visit endodontics (SVE) after aqueous ozone application (no agitation, manual, sonic, and ultrasonic activation) versus no ozone treatment at baseline; 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours; and 7 days.  
   - **Secondary Objective**: To analyze the effect of other variables like sex, age group, and arch (maxilla/mandible) on postendodontic pain.

3. **Groups**:  
   - Group 1: Ozone treatment with no activation (NA).  
   - Group 2: Ozone treatment with manual dynamic activation (MDA).  
   - Group 3: Ozone treatment with passive ultrasonic activation (PUA).  
   - Group 4: Ozone treatment with sonic activation (SA).  
   - Group 5: No ozone treatment (Control group).

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Total: 120 patients randomized into 5 groups (24 patients per group).  
   - Adjusted for dropouts: Final analyzed sample sizes were Group 1 (n=21), Group 2 (n=22), Group 3 (n=21), Group 4 (n=23), and Group 5 (n=23).

5. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variables**:  
     - Treatment groups (NA, MDA, PUA, SA, Control).  
     - Demographic factors (sex, age group, arch).  
   - **Dependent Variable**:  
     - Postendodontic pain measured using the Visual Analog Scale (VAS) at baseline; 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours; and 7 days.

6. **Measurements**:  
   - Pain intensity was recorded using a 10-cm VAS scale, where 0 = "no pain" and 10 = "pain as bad as it could be."  
   - Time points: Baseline, 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours, and 7 days.

7. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Pain diaries were maintained by participants and collected during follow-up visits.  
   - Blinded assessors recorded VAS scores into a master chart.

8. **Randomization and Blinding**:  
   - Randomization: Computer-generated random sequence with sealed opaque envelopes.  
   - Blinding: Patients and the statistician analyzing pain intensity were blinded. The operator could not be blinded due to the nature of the intervention.

9. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Age: 18–65 years.  
   - Single-rooted teeth with necrotic pulp and apical periodontitis.  
   - Periapical index score <3.  
   - Sound periodontal apparatus and sufficient coronal tooth structure.

10. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
    - Systemic diseases, immunocompromised conditions, pregnancy, allergies to drugs/irrigants, sinus tract, acute periapical abscess, calcified canals, immature apex, or mobility.  
    - Contraindications to ozone therapy (e.g., uncontrolled hyperthyroidism, thrombocytopenia, glucose-6-phosphatase deficiency).

11. **Intervention Details**:  
    - Aqueous ozone (4 mg/mL) was prepared using an ozone generator and applied for 3–5 minutes.  
    - Activation techniques: MDA, PUA, SA.  
    - Control group received saline irrigation as a placebo.  
    - Pain management: No postoperative analgesics were advised, but ibuprofen was provided as a reserve medication.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare VAS pain scores across time points (baseline, 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours, and 7 days) and between treatment groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The study involves repeated measurements of pain scores over time.  
     2. It allows for the evaluation of interaction effects (group × time).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and sphericity of data, which may not hold for VAS scores.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sphericity is violated and cannot be corrected with Greenhouse-Geisser or Huynh-Feldt adjustments.  
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) or nonparametric methods like Friedman’s test.

2. **Test Name**: Post-hoc Pairwise Comparisons (Bonferroni or Tukey).  
   - **Purpose**: To identify specific group differences in pain scores at each time point.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Necessary to determine which groups differ significantly after ANOVA.  
     2. Controls for Type I error in multiple comparisons.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: May lack power if sample sizes are small.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If ANOVA assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Pairwise Wilcoxon Rank-Sum Test with Bonferroni correction.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test).  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze categorical variables like sex and arch distribution across groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Evaluates whether demographic factors are evenly distributed among groups.  
     2. Ensures no confounding due to uneven group characteristics.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires sufficient sample size in each category.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

2. **Test Name**: Multivariable Linear Regression.  
   - **Purpose**: To assess the effect of demographic factors (sex, age, arch) on pain scores.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Adjusts for potential confounders.  
     2. Identifies independent predictors of pain.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linearity and normality of residuals.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If residuals are non-normal or heteroscedastic.  
   - **Alternative**: Quantile Regression.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.  
   - **Purpose**: To evaluate the time to pain resolution across groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for censored data (e.g., patients lost to follow-up).  
     2. Provides insights into pain resolution trends over time.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires clear definition of "event" (e.g., pain score = 0).  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If pain scores do not reach 0 for most participants.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **Independent t-test**:  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: The study involves more than two groups and repeated measures.  
2. **One-Way ANOVA**:  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: Does not account for repeated measures or time effects.  
3. **Pearson Correlation**:  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: Pain scores are not continuous variables suitable for correlation analysis.
