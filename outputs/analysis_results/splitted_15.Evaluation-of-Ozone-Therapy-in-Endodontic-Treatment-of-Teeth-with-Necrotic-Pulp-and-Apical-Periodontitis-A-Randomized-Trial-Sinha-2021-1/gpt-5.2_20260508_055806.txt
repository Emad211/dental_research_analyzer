## TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION (study characteristics)

### Study identification/design
- **Design:** Blinded, parallel-group **randomized controlled clinical trial** (CONSORT).
- **Setting/time:** Department of Conservative Dentistry and Endodontics, Pacific Dental College and Hospital, Udaipur, India; **Jan–Aug 2021**.
- **Registration/ethics:** IRB approval (PDCH/19/EC-140); registered (REF/2021/03/041572).
- **Blinding:**
  - **Participants blinded** (control received saline placebo similar to “no activation” irrigation).
  - **Outcome assessor blinded** (entered VAS scores).
  - **Statistician blinded**.
  - **Operator not blinded** (procedural constraint).

### Objectives/endpoints
- **Primary objective:** Compare **postendodontic pain** after single-visit endodontics (SVE) among:
  1) aqueous ozone **no activation (NA)**  
  2) aqueous ozone **manual dynamic activation (MDA)**  
  3) aqueous ozone **passive ultrasonic activation (PUA/PSA wording varies)**  
  4) aqueous ozone **sonic activation (SA)**  
  5) **no ozone control** (saline)
- **Primary outcome:** Pain intensity by **Visual Analog Scale (VAS)** (0–10 cm).
- **Pain measurement times (repeated measures):** **baseline (pre-treatment)**; **6, 12, 24, 48 hours**; **7 days**.
- **Secondary objective:** Effect of **sex**, **age group**, and **arch** (maxilla/mandible) on pain.

### Participants
- **Eligibility:**
  - Age **18–65**, generally healthy, available for follow-up.
  - **Single-rooted teeth** with **necrotic pulp** + **apical periodontitis**, **PAI < 3**, sound periodontium, sufficient coronal structure.
- **Key exclusions:** systemic disease/immunocompromised, pregnancy, drug/irrigant allergy, sinus tract, acute abscess, calcified canal/immature apex/mobility, and specific **ozone contraindications** (recent MI, intoxication, active bleeding, uncontrolled hyperthyroidism, thrombocytopenia, G6PD deficiency).

### Randomization/allocation
- **Randomization method:** Patient picked **computer-generated random sequence** in **sealed opaque envelope** after shaping/cleaning, before ozone step.
- **Allocation ratio stated:** “**4:1**” (but actual groups were **equal n=24 each** at randomization; ratio statement appears inconsistent with the flow chart).

### Sample size
- **Pilot:** 40 patients, 5 groups, n=8 each.
- **Effect size basis:** partial eta² for **group×time interaction** = 0.128 → **F = 0.383**.
- **Planned sample size:** 110 for 90% power, 95% CI stated (more properly α=0.05).
- **Inflated for dropout:** +10% → **121** planned; “final 24 per group” (≈120).
- **Actually randomized:** **120 (24/group)**.
- **Follow-up/incomplete forms:** Loss to follow-up and incomplete diaries occurred; medication use led to exclusion. (Full analyzed n not completely visible in provided excerpt; analysis plan should predefine handling.)

### Intervention/procedure (common endodontic protocol; single visit)
- Standard anesthesia, rubber dam, working length (apex locator + radiograph), crown-down rotary NiTi, EDTA; NaOCl 5.25% (2 mL each step) with 27G side-vented needle.
- **Ozone preparation:** 4 mg/mL gaseous ozone bubbled into distilled water for 15 min.
- **Irrigation protocol for ozone groups:** **15 mL ozonated water**, **3–5 min**, tip 2 mm short of working length.
- **Activation:**
  - NA: no agitation
  - MDA: gutta-percha push-pull (~100 strokes/min)
  - PUA: ultrasonic file ISO #10, **3 cycles × 20 sec**, power 3
  - SA: Pro Agitator tip system
- **Control:** saline irrigation (placebo, similar procedure to NA).
- Obturation: AH Plus sealer + lateral condensation; occlusal reduction + temporary restoration.
- **Analgesics:** none routinely; **rescue ibuprofen** provided. **If rescue used within 7 days → excluded** (i.e., per-protocol-type exclusion).

### Data collection
- **Outcome capture:** Patient marks VAS in a pain diary at specified times; returned at 48h and 7d recalls; assessor transcribed to master chart blinded to group.

## TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN (SAP)

### General SAP elements (apply to all analyses)
- **Analysis populations (pre-specify):**
  - **Primary:** Intention-to-treat (ITT) including all randomized participants with outcome data; **do not exclude rescue-medication users**—instead incorporate as outcome-related (see sensitivity analyses).
  - **Sensitivity:** Per-protocol (PP) excluding major protocol deviations; and a “no-rescue-med” PP if authors insist, but report as sensitivity only.
- **Primary estimand:** Difference in mean pain trajectories over time among 5 groups.
- **Descriptives:**
  - Baseline: age (continuous), sex, arch, tooth type (if available), baseline VAS.
  - Report **mean±SD** (as article notes), but also **median (IQR)** for VAS if skewed.
- **Missing data handling:**
  - Document reasons (LTFU, incomplete forms).
  - Prefer **mixed models** (valid under MAR) or **multiple imputation** (MI) for covariates/sensitivity.
  - Avoid last observation carried forward as primary.
- **Multiplicity:**
  - Predefine family-wise control for pairwise group comparisons at each timepoint (Holm) or control false discovery rate (Benjamini–Hochberg) depending on objective.
- **Effect size reporting:**
  - For global group/time: partial η² (if ANOVA-based) or model-based standardized differences.
  - For pairwise: **mean difference with 95% CI** at each time or at prespecified time(s) (e.g., 24h primary timepoint if chosen).
- **Model diagnostics:** residual checks, influence, covariance structure.

## CATEGORY A – MANDATORY tests/models

### A1. Linear Mixed-Effects Model (LMM) for repeated VAS
- **Purpose:** Test whether **pain over time differs by group** (group×time interaction) while accounting for within-patient correlation.
- **Why appropriate (≥2):**
  1. Outcome is **repeated continuous** VAS over 6 timepoints → LMM directly models correlation.
  2. Handles **unbalanced data/missing timepoints** without deleting entire subjects (under MAR).
  3. Provides **time-specific and overall** contrasts with CIs.
- **Why it may not be appropriate (≥1):**
  - If VAS shows strong non-normality/ceiling effects or is better treated as ordinal; LMM may fit poorly.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If outcome is intentionally analyzed as **ordinal categories** (none/mild/moderate/severe) or as counts; or if residuals are extremely non-Gaussian and cannot be stabilized.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - **Generalized linear mixed model (GLMM)** with appropriate distribution (e.g., beta regression for bounded continuous after rescaling; ordinal mixed model), or **rank-based mixed models**.
- **Implementation details to pre-specify:**
  - Fixed effects: group (5), time (categorical), **group×time**, baseline VAS (optional; see A2), sex, age, arch (if adjusting).
  - Random effects: random intercept for subject; consider random slope for time if supported.
  - Covariance: start with unstructured or AR(1); choose using AIC/BIC.

### A2. Baseline comparability / covariate adjustment strategy
Two linked mandatory components:

**(i) Descriptive baseline balance (no hypothesis testing emphasis)**
- **Purpose:** Describe baseline characteristics by group to assess clinical comparability.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. RCT requires transparent reporting of baseline distributions.
  2. Identifies potential chance imbalances (noted by authors for age/sex).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Significance testing at baseline can mislead (randomization implies differences are by chance).
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Do **not** use p-values as “proof” of balance/imbalance; avoid using baseline p-values to decide adjustment.
- **Alternative:**
  - Use **standardized mean differences (SMDs)** and graphical summaries.

**(ii) Covariate-adjusted primary model (ANCOVA principle within LMM)**
- **Test/model:** LMM including **baseline VAS** (and prespecified covariates: age, sex, arch).
- **Purpose:** Improve precision and address noted imbalance.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Baseline pain strongly predicts follow-up pain → increases power/precision.
  2. Prespecified adjustment mitigates chance imbalances (age/sex).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If baseline VAS is conceptually “pre-treatment” but measured after anesthesia or is inconsistently collected.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If baseline measurement is missing for many subjects or not comparable.
- **Alternative if issues:**
  - Analyze **change-from-baseline** via LMM; or MI for missing baseline values.

### A3. Primary hypothesis test: global group×time interaction (from LMM)
- **Purpose:** Formal test of whether pain trajectories differ across the five interventions.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Matches the primary objective (compare pain across times and groups).
  2. Controls type I error better than many separate timepoint tests.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If clinical focus is a single prespecified timepoint (e.g., 24h), interaction may dilute power.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If only one timepoint is valid/available or if timepoints are not consistently collected.
- **Alternative:**
  - Prespecify one/few clinically primary times and model those (e.g., LMM but focus contrast at 24h), or use **area under the curve (AUC)** (see B2).

## CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED analyses/tests

### B1. Post-hoc pairwise comparisons (estimated marginal means from LMM) with multiplicity control
- **Purpose:** Identify which ozone activation methods differ from control and from each other at specific times.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Clinically interpretable (e.g., SA vs control at 24h).
  2. Uses the same model, preserving correlation structure and handling missingness.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Many comparisons inflate type I error if not adjusted and may overemphasize secondary contrasts.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If not prespecified and the study is underpowered for multiple pairwise claims.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Model-based comparisons from GLMM/robust mixed model; adjust p-values similarly (Holm).

### B2. Summary measure of pain burden: Area Under the Curve (AUC) over 0–48h (and/or 0–7d)
- **Purpose:** Compare overall postoperative pain experience rather than individual timepoints.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Reduces multiplicity and captures **total pain burden**.
  2. Robust to minor timing irregularities; clinically meaningful.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Requires sufficiently complete repeated measures; missingness can bias AUC if not handled carefully.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If many participants have missing intermediate VAS scores without reliable imputation.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Use LMM-derived **model-based AUC** (integrate predicted means) or MI before AUC calculation; compare using **linear regression**/robust regression.

### B3. Sensitivity analysis for rescue medication use (informative censoring)
- **Test/model options:**
  - (i) Include a binary time-varying indicator “rescue medication taken” in mixed model (if timing known), or
  - (ii) Treat rescue use as **worst pain** imputation after intake, or
  - (iii) Joint/mixture approach: analyze pain plus rescue as composite endpoint.
- **Purpose:** Address bias from excluding those with severe pain.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Excluding medicated patients is outcome-dependent → biases treatment effect.
  2. Sensitivity analyses quantify robustness.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Timing/dose may be unknown; composite definitions can be arbitrary.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If rescue medication data are unreliable or not collected.
- **Alternative:**
  - Report ITT and PP side-by-side; perform **tipping-point** sensitivity for missing not at random (MNAR).

### B4. Model checking and robust inference
- **Tests/tools:** residual plots; Q-Q plots; check heteroscedasticity; influence diagnostics; robust (sandwich) SEs.
- **Purpose:** Validate model assumptions; reduce risk of misleading inference.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. VAS often non-normal and heteroscedastic across time/groups.
  2. Robust SEs provide protection against mild misspecification.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - With very small sample sizes per group/time, diagnostics are less informative.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Never “not use”; but avoid over-interpreting formal normality tests.
- **Alternative:**
  - Robust mixed models; transformation; GLMM.

### B5. Secondary objective: effects of sex, age, arch (and interactions)
- **Test/model:** Extend LMM with covariates and possibly **covariate×time** interactions (pre-specify limited set).
- **Purpose:** Evaluate whether pain differs by these factors and whether they modify treatment effect.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Directly addresses stated secondary aim.
  2. Adjusts for imbalances and explores clinically plausible effect modification.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Risk of overfitting/multiplicity with many interactions.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If sample sizes within subgroups are too small (sparse cells) leading to unstable estimates.
- **Alternative:**
  - Main-effects-only adjustment; or stratified descriptive results without formal interaction tests.

## CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL analyses/tests (supplementary)

### C1. Ordinal pain categories analysis (e.g., none/mild/moderate/severe)
- **Test/model:** **Ordinal logistic mixed model** (cumulative logit) over time.
- **Purpose:** Present results in clinically intuitive categories.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Avoids assuming equal interval scaling of VAS.
  2. Handles skewed distributions and floor effects.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Requires choosing cutpoints; proportional odds assumption may fail.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If cutpoints are arbitrary and not prespecified.
- **Alternative:**
  - Partial proportional odds model; multinomial mixed model.

### C2. “Time to minimal pain” endpoint
- **Test/model:** Kaplan–Meier and Cox model (if time can be discretized/approximated), or discrete-time survival.
- **Purpose:** Compare speed of pain resolution.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Patient-centered: “How quickly do patients feel better?”
  2. Uses multiple timepoints in a different way.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Pain recorded at fixed times (interval-censoring); exact event time unknown.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If event definition is unstable or most patients already have low pain at baseline.
- **Alternative:**
  - Interval-censored survival models; or compare probability of pain-free at each time via GLMM.

### C3. Nonparametric timepoint comparisons (supplement only)
- **Test:** Kruskal–Wallis at each timepoint (with multiplicity adjustment).
- **Purpose:** Provide assumption-light cross-sectional comparisons at each time.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Works when VAS is heavily non-normal.
  2. Simple to communicate.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Ignores within-subject correlation; increases multiplicity; less efficient.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - As the primary analysis of repeated measures.
- **Alternative:**
  - Rank-based mixed models.

## TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS (should NOT be used)

1. **Multiple independent-samples t-tests** comparing groups at each timepoint without correction  
   - Inflates type I error; ignores repeated-measures correlation; not aligned with primary objective.

2. **One-way ANOVA on a single post-treatment timepoint only** (e.g., 24h) *without prespecification*  
   - Discards longitudinal information and reduces power/validity.

3. **Paired t-tests within each group across time** as main evidence  
   - Answers a different question (change within group) and does not compare interventions appropriately.

4. **Repeated-measures ANOVA (rANOVA) with complete-case listwise deletion** as the only analysis  
   - Requires sphericity; performs poorly with missing follow-up/common in diaries; can bias results.

5. **Chi-square tests on mean VAS** (misuse of categorical tests for continuous outcomes)  
   - Wrong scale/type.

6. **Baseline “significance testing” to decide whether to adjust**  
   - Data-dependent modeling increases bias; baseline tests are not a sound criterion.

7. **Excluding rescue-medication users from the primary analysis** (pure per-protocol only)  
   - Outcome-dependent exclusion likely biases treatment effects toward benefit.

## TASK 4: SUMMARY (overall analytical approach)

A longitudinal RCT with five parallel groups and repeated VAS measurements requires a **mixed-effects modeling framework** to (i) properly account for within-patient correlation, (ii) use incomplete repeated measures without biased complete-case deletion, and (iii) directly test the primary clinical question via a **group×time interaction** while providing clinically interpretable pairwise contrasts with multiplicity control. Covariate adjustment (baseline VAS; age/sex/arch) and sensitivity analyses for missing data and rescue medication strengthen internal validity given acknowledged imbalances and informative dropout risk.
