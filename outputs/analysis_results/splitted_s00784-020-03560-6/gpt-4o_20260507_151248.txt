### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Retrospective pilot study.
2. **Objective**: To evaluate the effect of sole endodontic treatment on periodontal healing in teeth diagnosed with combined endo-periodontal lesions without root damage (grade 3) in periodontally compromised patients.
3. **Population**:
   - **Sample Size**: 19 teeth from 13 periodontally compromised patients.
   - **Inclusion Criteria**: Teeth diagnosed with combined endo-periodontal lesions without root damage (grade 3).
   - **Exclusion Criteria**: Not explicitly mentioned but implied exclusion of teeth with subgingival instrumentation or root damage.
4. **Groups**: Single group (no control group).
5. **Variables**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Change in pocket probing depth (PPD).
   - **Secondary Outcomes**: Change in periodontal apical index (PAI) and change in clinical attachment loss (CAL).
   - **Other Variables**: Smoking status, antibiotic therapy, restorations, caries, and sensitivity.
6. **Measurements**:
   - **Clinical Parameters**: PPD, CAL, furcation involvement, tooth mobility.
   - **Radiographic Parameters**: PAI score (5-point scale).
   - **Root Canal Quality**: Length and density of root canal fillings (Farzaneh et al. scoring system).
7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Clinical examinations before and after treatment.
   - Radiographic evaluations using periapical radiographs.
   - Blinded assessment of PAI by a calibrated investigator.
   - Root canal quality assessed by two independent investigators with consensus in case of disagreement.
8. **Treatment Protocol**:
   - Standardized endodontic treatment with rubber dam isolation.
   - Instrumentation using hand and rotary files (Reciproc®).
   - Irrigation with sodium hypochlorite, chlorhexidine, and ethyl alcohol.
   - Calcium hydroxide dressing for at least 2 weeks.
   - Obturation with thermoplastic filling or lateral condensation techniques.
   - Teeth exempted from subgingival debridement during the study interval.
9. **Follow-Up**:
   - Clinical and radiographic reevaluation 6 to 17 months after treatment.
   - Success defined as ≥50% improvement in PPD and CAL or improvement in PAI score.
