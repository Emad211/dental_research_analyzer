### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Parallel group randomized controlled trial (RCT) with two arms.
   - Conducted from May 2019 to March 2020.

2. **Objective**: 
   - To evaluate postoperative pain after root canal treatment using a traditional endodontic motor with unconnected EAL versus a motor with connected EAL.
   - Null hypothesis: No difference in pain intensities between the two groups.

3. **Groups**:
   - **Group 1**: Instrumentation with traditional endodontic motor and unconnected EAL.
   - **Group 2**: Instrumentation with endodontic motor connected to EAL.

4. **Sample Size**:
   - Calculated using a formula for comparison of means from two independent samples.
   - Target sample size: 42 patients (21 per group), accounting for potential loss to follow-up.

5. **Participants**:
   - **Inclusion Criteria**: Healthy patients without systemic diseases, symptomatic irreversible pulpitis or apical periodontitis, first or second molars.
   - **Exclusion Criteria**: Systemic diseases, sinus tract, swelling, clenching/bruxism, severely damaged teeth, severe periodontal disease, resorption, previously treated teeth, root cracks, analgesic use within 24 hours, absence of opposing tooth, age <18 years.

6. **Randomization**:
   - Patients were randomly assigned to groups using an online randomizer (www.randomizer.org).

7. **Blinding**:
   - Patients were blinded to the treatment modality.

8. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variable**: Type of instrumentation (traditional motor with unconnected EAL vs. motor with connected EAL).
   - **Dependent Variable**: Postoperative pain intensity.
   - **Covariates**: Age, gender, tooth type, preoperative pain level, analgesic intake.

9. **Measurements**:
   - Pain intensity measured using the Heft Parker Visual Analogue Scale (HP-VAS), a 170-mm scale divided into four categories (no pain, mild, moderate, severe).
   - Pain recorded at 6, 24, 48, 72 hours, and one week post-treatment.
   - Percussion test performed at one week post-treatment.

10. **Interventions**:
    - Both groups underwent root canal treatment using Proglider and ProTaper Next (PTN) instruments.
    - Group 1: Working length determined using ProPex Pixi EAL and confirmed radiographically.
    - Group 2: Working length determined using E Connect S motor with E-Pex Pro EAL, which automatically adjusted motor speed upon reaching the apex.

11. **Data Collection**:
    - Preoperative and postoperative pain levels, analgesic intake, and demographic data were recorded.

12. **Statistical Analysis**:
    - Sample size calculation included power of 80% and significance level of 0.05.
    - No specific statistical tests were explicitly mentioned in the provided text.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test (or Mann-Whitney U Test if assumptions are violated).
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean postoperative pain scores between the two groups at each time point.
   - **Why Appropriate**: 
     1. The outcome variable (pain score) is continuous.
     2. The study design involves two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normal distribution and homogeneity of variances, which may not hold for pain scores.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are not normally distributed or variances are unequal.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test for non-parametric data.

2. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Friedman Test if assumptions are violated).
   - **Purpose**: Analyze changes in pain scores over time within and between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Pain scores are measured at multiple time points.
     2. Allows for testing both within-subject (time) and between-subject (group) effects.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes sphericity and normality of residuals.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If sphericity is violated or data are non-parametric.
   - **Alternative**: Friedman Test for non-parametric repeated measures.

3. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test).
   - **Purpose**: Compare proportions of patients requiring analgesics between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The variable (analgesic use) is categorical.
     2. Compares proportions between two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Small sample size in some categories may lead to low expected frequencies.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If expected cell counts are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
4. **Test Name**: Mixed-Effects Model.
   - **Purpose**: Account for both fixed effects (group, time) and random effects (individual variability).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Handles repeated measures data with missing values.
     2. Does not require sphericity assumption.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires advanced statistical software and expertise.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If the dataset is too small to estimate random effects reliably.
   - **Alternative**: Repeated Measures ANOVA or Friedman Test.

5. **Test Name**: Effect Size Calculation (e.g., Cohen’s d).
   - **Purpose**: Quantify the magnitude of differences between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides additional context to p-values.
     2. Useful for clinical interpretation of results.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Does not test statistical significance.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If data are highly skewed.
   - **Alternative**: Non-parametric effect size measures (e.g., rank-biserial correlation).

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
6. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (e.g., stratified by gender or tooth type).
   - **Purpose**: Explore potential effect modifiers.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Identifies whether group differences vary by demographic or clinical factors.
     2. Provides insights into heterogeneity of treatment effects.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Reduces statistical power due to smaller sample sizes in subgroups.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If subgroup sizes are too small.
   - **Alternative**: Interaction terms in regression models.
