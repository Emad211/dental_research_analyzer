### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### **Study Characteristics**
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Randomized controlled trial (RCT) with a parallel design.
   - Blinded for patients, assessor, and statistician; operator not blinded.

2. **Study Objectives**:  
   - **Primary Objective**: To evaluate and compare postendodontic pain in patients undergoing single-visit endodontics (SVE) after aqueous ozone application (no agitation, manual, sonic, and ultrasonic activation) versus no ozone treatment at baseline; 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours; and 7 days.  
   - **Secondary Objective**: To analyze the effect of variables like sex (male/female), age group, and arch (maxilla/mandible) on postendodontic pain.

3. **Study Groups**:  
   - Group 1: Ozone treatment with no activation (NA).  
   - Group 2: Ozone treatment with manual dynamic activation (MDA).  
   - Group 3: Ozone treatment with passive ultrasonic activation (PUA).  
   - Group 4: Ozone treatment with sonic activation (SA).  
   - Group 5: No ozone treatment (Control Group).

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Total: 120 patients randomized (24 per group).  
   - Final analysis: Group 1 (n=21), Group 2 (n=22), Group 3 (n=21), Group 4 (n=23), Group 5 (n=23).  
   - Power: 90%, Confidence Interval: 95%, Effect size: F = 0.383.

5. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variables**:  
     - Treatment groups (NA, MDA, PUA, SA, Control).  
     - Demographic variables: sex, age group, and arch (maxilla/mandible).  
   - **Dependent Variable**:  
     - Postendodontic pain measured using the Visual Analog Scale (VAS) at baseline; 6, 12, 24, and 48 hours; and 7 days.

6. **Measurements**:  
   - Pain intensity measured using a 10-cm Visual Analog Scale (VAS), ranging from "no pain" to "pain as bad as it could be."

7. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Pain diaries completed by patients at specified time intervals.  
   - Blinded assessor recorded VAS scores in a master chart.

8. **Randomization and Blinding**:  
   - Randomization: Computer-generated random sequence with sealed opaque envelopes.  
   - Blinding: Patients, assessor, and statistician blinded; operator not blinded.

9. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Adults aged 18–65 years with good general health.  
   - Single-rooted teeth with necrotic pulp and apical periodontitis.  
   - Periapical index score <3, sound periodontal apparatus, and sufficient coronal tooth structure.

10. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
    - Systemic diseases, immunocompromised conditions, pregnancy, allergies to drugs/irrigants, sinus tracts, acute abscess, calcified canals, immature apex, mobility, and contraindications to ozone therapy.

11. **Follow-Up**:  
    - Patients followed up at 2 days and 7 days.  
    - Exclusions: Patients taking reserve medication (ibuprofen) or with incomplete pain forms.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### **CATEGORY A – MANDATORY**
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (RM-ANOVA).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare VAS pain scores across time points (baseline, 6, 12, 24, 48 hours, and 7 days) within and between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Longitudinal data with repeated measures for the same subjects.  
     2. Multiple groups (5 groups) with continuous outcome (VAS scores).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes sphericity, which may not hold.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sphericity is violated and cannot be corrected.  
   - **Alternative**: Mixed-effects model or Greenhouse-Geisser correction.

2. **Test Name**: One-way ANOVA.  
   - **Purpose**: To compare mean VAS scores between groups at specific time points.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Continuous outcome (VAS scores) with independent groups.  
     2. Assesses differences between treatment protocols.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and homogeneity of variance.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test.

3. **Test Name**: Chi-square test.  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze categorical variables like sex, age group, and arch distribution across groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Categorical data (sex, age group, arch).  
     2. Tests for independence between variables.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires sufficient sample size in each category.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s exact test.

---

#### **CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED**
1. **Test Name**: Mixed-effects model.  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze VAS scores over time, accounting for repeated measures and random effects.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Handles missing data and dropouts.  
     2. Does not require sphericity assumption.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Computationally intensive.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If simpler models suffice.  
   - **Alternative**: RM-ANOVA with corrections.

2. **Test Name**: Post-hoc tests (e.g., Tukey’s HSD).  
   - **Purpose**: To identify specific group differences after ANOVA.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Controls for Type I error in multiple comparisons.  
     2. Provides pairwise comparisons.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality and equal variances.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If ANOVA assumptions are violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Pairwise Wilcoxon tests.

---

#### **CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL**
1. **Test Name**: Mann-Whitney U test.  
   - **Purpose**: To compare VAS scores between two groups at specific time points.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Non-parametric alternative for small sample sizes.  
     2. Does not assume normality.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Less powerful than parametric tests.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If parametric assumptions are met.  
   - **Alternative**: Independent t-test.

2. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier survival analysis.  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze time to pain resolution.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Time-to-event data (pain resolution).  
     2. Censoring (e.g., dropouts).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not designed for continuous outcomes like VAS scores.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If time-to-event is not a primary focus.  
   - **Alternative**: Mixed-effects model.
