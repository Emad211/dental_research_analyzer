### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Single-blind, randomized controlled clinical trial.

2. **Objective**: 
   - To evaluate the effect of low-level laser therapy (LLLT) on the success rate of inferior alveolar nerve block (IANB) in mandibular molars with symptomatic irreversible pulpitis (SIP).

3. **Study Groups**:
   - **IANB + LLLT group**: Patients received standard IANB followed by LLLT.
   - **IANB group**: Patients received standard IANB with a mock laser application (placebo).

4. **Sample Size**:
   - Total: 88 patients.
   - Group 1 (IANB + LLLT): 44 patients.
   - Group 2 (IANB): 44 patients.

5. **Randomization**:
   - Patients were randomly assigned to groups using a computer-generated random table with a 1:1 allocation ratio.

6. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Healthy individuals aged 18–65 years.
   - Mandibular molar teeth diagnosed with SIP.
   - ASA 1–2 patients (no systemic disease or mild systemic disease).
   - Moderate or severe preoperative pain (VAS > 44 mm).
   - Positive response to cold and electric pulp testing.
   - No periapical radiolucency (except widened periodontal ligament with intact lamina dura).

7. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Recent use of analgesics or anti-inflammatory drugs (within 12 hours).
   - Allergies to NSAIDs or articaine.
   - Pregnant or breastfeeding women.
   - Patients taking opioids or with gastrointestinal disease.
   - Teeth with periodontal disease, periapical radiolucency, or no pulpal bleeding after access cavity preparation.
   - Patients with more than one painful tooth.

8. **Variables**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Success rate of IANB (defined as VAS ≤ 44 mm during access cavity preparation and canal shaping).
   - **Secondary Outcome**: Pain scores on VAS (0–100 mm scale) at different stages of treatment.

9. **Measurements**:
   - Pain scores recorded using a 100-mm visual analog scale (VAS).
   - Pain levels categorized as:
     - No pain: 0–4 mm.
     - Mild pain: 5–44 mm.
     - Moderate pain: 45–74 mm.
     - Severe pain: 75–100 mm.

10. **Data Collection Methods**:
    - Pain scores recorded preoperatively and during treatment.
    - Success of anesthesia determined by VAS scores ≤ 44 mm.
    - LLLT applied using a 940 nm diode laser (Epic 10, Biolase Inc.) with specific parameters (0.5 W power, 30 seconds per root area).

11. **Statistical Power**:
    - Based on a pilot study with α = 0.05, β = 0.80, and effect size = 0.782, a minimum of 40 patients per group was required.

12. **Blinding**:
    - Single-blind design: The operator performing the injections was blinded to the intervention.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test)**:
   - **Purpose**: To compare the success rates of IANB between the two groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The outcome (success or failure of anesthesia) is categorical.
     2. The data are from independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If expected frequencies in any cell of the contingency table are <5.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small for valid chi-square application.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test (for small sample sizes).

2. **Independent Samples t-Test**:
   - **Purpose**: To compare mean VAS pain scores between the two groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The VAS scores are continuous data.
     2. The study design involves two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the assumption of normality is violated.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the VAS scores are not normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test (non-parametric alternative).

3. **Descriptive Statistics**:
   - **Purpose**: To summarize baseline characteristics and outcomes (e.g., mean, standard deviation, frequency, percentage).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides an overview of the data distribution.
     2. Essential for understanding the sample characteristics.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Not applicable; descriptive statistics are always appropriate.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - Not applicable.
   - **Alternative**: None.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Logistic Regression**:
   - **Purpose**: To assess the effect of LLLT on the likelihood of successful anesthesia while adjusting for potential confounders (e.g., age, gender, baseline pain score).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The outcome (success/failure) is binary.
     2. Allows adjustment for covariates.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the sample size is too small for reliable model estimation.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If multicollinearity exists among covariates.
   - **Alternative**: Stratified analysis.

2. **Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model)**:
   - **Purpose**: To analyze changes in VAS scores over time within and between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Accounts for repeated measures on the same individuals.
     2. Tests for interaction effects (group × time).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If sphericity assumption is violated.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If data are not normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Non-parametric alternatives like Friedman Test.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Effect Size Calculation (Cohen’s d)**:
   - **Purpose**: To quantify the magnitude of the difference in VAS scores between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides a standardized measure of effect size.
     2. Useful for interpreting clinical significance.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - If the data are not normally distributed.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the sample size is too small for reliable estimation.
   - **Alternative**: Rank-biserial correlation for non-parametric data.

2. **Subgroup Analysis**:
   - **Purpose**: To explore differences in outcomes based on baseline characteristics (e.g., age, gender, baseline pain score).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Identifies potential effect modifiers.
     2. Provides additional insights into treatment effects.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Risk of type I error due to multiple comparisons.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If subgroup sample sizes are too small.
   - **Alternative**: Interaction terms in regression models.
