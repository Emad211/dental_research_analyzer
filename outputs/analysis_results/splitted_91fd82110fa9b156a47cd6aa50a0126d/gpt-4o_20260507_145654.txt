### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Prospective cohort study.

2. **Study Objective**:  
   - To evaluate the influence of various predictors on the outcome of root canal retreatment in symptomatic mandibular first molars with periapical lesions after a minimum of 2 years.

3. **Study Population**:  
   - **Initial Pool**: 420 mandibular molars referred for root canal retreatment between February 2016 and December 2018.  
   - **Final Sample**: 120 symptomatic mandibular first molars with periapical lesions included.  
   - **Follow-Up Sample**: 103 molars (85.3% recall rate).  

4. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Non-contributory medical history.  
   - Previously root canal treated mature mandibular first molars with clinical signs and symptoms (e.g., preoperative pain, tenderness to percussion).  
   - At least one root with periapical lesions scored as PAI 4 or 5.  
   - Lesion size between 3–10 mm on preoperative radiographs.  
   - Diagnosed as symptomatic apical periodontitis or acute apical abscess with localized intraoral swelling.

5. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
   - Patients <18 years of age.  
   - Extra-oral swelling.  
   - Antibiotic intake within 2 weeks prior to treatment.  
   - Second and third molars.  
   - Teeth with asymptomatic apical periodontitis.  
   - Advanced periodontal disease, non-restorable teeth, vertical root fractures, resorption, or complications from primary root canal treatment.

6. **Groups**:  
   - **Exudate Group**: Teeth with active exudate drainage through the root canal.  
   - **Non-Exudate Group**: Teeth without active exudate drainage.

7. **Sample Size**:  
   - 103 teeth evaluated at follow-up.

8. **Variables**:  
   - **Predictors**:  
     - Patient demographics (age, gender).  
     - Preoperative factors: soft tissue tenderness, intraoral swelling, lesion size, PAI score, apical level, density of root canal filling.  
     - Intraoperative factors: active exudate drainage, complications (e.g., sealer extrusion, broken instruments), restoration type.  
   - **Outcome**:  
     - Healed (PAI 1–2), Healing (PAI 3–4), Non-Healing/Failure (PAI 4–5 or worse).

9. **Measurements and Data Collection**:  
   - Preoperative and follow-up radiographs using the paralleling technique.  
   - PAI scoring system (Ørstavik et al., 1986).  
   - Clinical examination for pain, tenderness, swelling, and mobility.  
   - Radiographic assessment for lesion size and healing status.  
   - Two independent calibrated endodontists scored radiographs, with disagreements resolved by consensus.

10. **Follow-Up Schedule**:  
    - Clinical and radiographic examinations at 3, 6, 9, and 12 months in the first year, every 6 months in the second year, and annually thereafter (minimum 2 years, up to 4 years).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Descriptive Statistics**:  
   - **Purpose**: Summarize baseline characteristics (e.g., age, gender, lesion size, PAI scores).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides a clear understanding of the study population.  
     2. Helps identify potential confounders.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not inferential; cannot establish relationships.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: N/A (always required).  
   - **Alternative**: None.

2. **Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Compare categorical variables (e.g., success rates between exudate and non-exudate groups).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Tests associations between categorical predictors and outcomes.  
     2. Suitable for binary or ordinal data (e.g., healed vs. non-healed).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires sufficient sample size in each category.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: Small expected frequencies (<5).  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

3. **Logistic Regression**:  
   - **Purpose**: Identify predictors of treatment success (healed/healing vs. failure).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for multiple predictors simultaneously.  
     2. Provides odds ratios for risk factors.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes linearity in log-odds.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When sample size is too small for the number of predictors.  
   - **Alternative**: Penalized regression (e.g., LASSO).

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis**:  
   - **Purpose**: Assess time to healing or failure.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for varying follow-up durations.  
     2. Handles censored data (e.g., lost to follow-up).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes non-informative censoring.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: When time-to-event data is unavailable.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox proportional hazards model.

2. **Cox Proportional Hazards Regression**:  
   - **Purpose**: Identify predictors of time to healing or failure.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Adjusts for multiple covariates.  
     2. Provides hazard ratios for predictors.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes proportional hazards.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: Violations of proportional hazards assumption.  
   - **Alternative**: Stratified Cox model.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Mixed-Effects Model)**:  
   - **Purpose**: Analyze changes in PAI scores over time.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for within-subject correlations.  
     2. Suitable for longitudinal data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality of residuals.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: Non-normal data or unequal variances.  
   - **Alternative**: Non-parametric tests (e.g., Friedman test).

2. **Subgroup Analysis**:  
   - **Purpose**: Explore differences in outcomes by subgroups (e.g., gender, lesion size).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Identifies effect modifiers.  
     2. Provides additional clinical insights.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Risk of type I error due to multiple comparisons.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: Small sample sizes in subgroups.  
   - **Alternative**: Adjusted analyses with interaction terms.
