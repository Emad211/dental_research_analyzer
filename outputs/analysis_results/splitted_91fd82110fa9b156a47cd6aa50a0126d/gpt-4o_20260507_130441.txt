### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Prospective cohort study.

2. **Objective**:  
   - To evaluate the influence of various predictors on the outcome of root canal retreatment in symptomatic mandibular first molars with periapical lesions after a minimum of 2 years.

3. **Population and Sample Size**:  
   - Initial sample: 420 mandibular molars referred for root canal retreatment.  
   - Final included sample: 120 symptomatic mandibular first molars with periapical lesions.  
   - Follow-up sample: 103 teeth (recall rate: 85.3%).

4. **Groups**:  
   - Grouping based on treatment outcomes:  
     - Healed (PAI 1 or 2).  
     - Healing (PAI 3 or 4).  
     - Nonhealing/Failure (PAI 4 or 5 with unchanged or increased lesion size).

5. **Variables**:  
   - **Predictor Variables**:  
     - Preoperative predictors: Age, gender, soft tissue tenderness, intraoral swelling, periapical lesion size (3–10 mm), PAI score (4 or 5), apical level, and density of root canal filling.  
     - Intraoperative predictors: Active exudate drainage, apical level and density of root canal filling after retreatment, complications (e.g., sealer extrusion, broken instruments, perforations, flare-ups), and restoration type (single crown/composite).  
   - **Outcome Variables**:  
     - Treatment success (healed + healing) vs. failure (nonhealing).  
     - PAI score changes over time.  
     - Clinical symptoms (pain, tenderness, swelling, sinus tract, mobility).  
     - Radiographic resolution of periapical lesions.

6. **Measurements**:  
   - **Primary Outcome**:  
     - PAI score (Ørstavik et al., 1986) assessed by two calibrated endodontists.  
   - **Secondary Outcomes**:  
     - Clinical signs and symptoms (pain, tenderness, swelling, etc.).  
     - Radiographic lesion size reduction or resolution.  

7. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Preoperative and follow-up digital radiographs using the paralleling technique.  
   - Clinical examinations at multiple follow-up intervals (3, 6, 9, and 12 months, then every 6 months for 2 years, and annually thereafter).  
   - Calibration of evaluators for PAI scoring.  
   - Standardized treatment protocol for root canal retreatment.

8. **Follow-up Duration**:  
   - Minimum of 2 years, with some cases followed up to 4 years.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test if needed).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare categorical variables (e.g., success vs. failure rates across groups).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The outcome variable (success vs. failure) is categorical.  
     2. Predictor variables like gender, presence of swelling, and restoration type are also categorical.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Small sample sizes in subgroups may violate assumptions.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected cell counts are <5 in >20% of cells.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

2. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression.  
   - **Purpose**: To assess the influence of multiple predictors on binary outcomes (e.g., success vs. failure).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Allows for adjustment of multiple confounders (e.g., age, lesion size).  
     2. Outcome variable is binary (success vs. failure).  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: If multicollinearity exists among predictors.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is insufficient for the number of predictors.  
   - **Alternative**: Penalized regression methods (e.g., LASSO).

3. **Test Name**: Paired t-test (or Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare preoperative and postoperative PAI scores.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. PAI scores are ordinal but can be treated as continuous for parametric tests.  
     2. Pre- and postoperative scores are paired within subjects.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: If PAI scores are not normally distributed.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If normality assumption is violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis.  
   - **Purpose**: To evaluate time-to-event data (e.g., time to healing or failure).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for varying follow-up durations.  
     2. Provides survival probabilities over time.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes independent censoring.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If censoring is not random.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.

2. **Test Name**: Cox Proportional Hazards Regression.  
   - **Purpose**: To identify predictors of time to healing or failure.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Adjusts for multiple predictors.  
     2. Handles censored data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes proportional hazards.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If proportional hazards assumption is violated.  
   - **Alternative**: Stratified Cox model or time-varying covariates.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Linear Mixed Model).  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze changes in PAI scores over multiple follow-up time points.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for within-subject correlation over time.  
     2. Handles repeated measures data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality of residuals.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are highly skewed or missing at random.  
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).

2. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (e.g., stratified by exudate presence).  
   - **Purpose**: To explore differences in outcomes between subgroups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides insights into effect modification.  
     2. Useful for hypothesis generation.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Risk of Type I error due to multiple comparisons.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If subgroup sizes are too small.  
   - **Alternative**: Interaction terms in regression models.

---

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **Independent t-test**:  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: The data are paired (pre- and postoperative PAI scores).  
   - **Alternative**: Paired t-test or Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test.

2. **ANOVA**:  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: Does not account for repeated measures or within-subject correlation.  
   - **Alternative**: Repeated Measures ANOVA or Linear Mixed Model.

3. **Pearson Correlation**:  
   - **Why Not Appropriate**: Does not account for categorical or time-to-event outcomes.  
   - **Alternative**: Logistic regression or Cox regression.
