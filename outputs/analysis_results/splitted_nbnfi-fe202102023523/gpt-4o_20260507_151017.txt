### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:
   - Prospective cohort study (Parogene, a subpopulation of Corogene cohort).
   - Cross-sectional and longitudinal components.
   - 10-year follow-up for mortality outcomes.

2. **Study Objectives**:
   - Investigate the prevalence of carotid artery calcification (CAC) visible on panoramic radiographs in patients undergoing coronary angiography.
   - Assess associations between CAC and:
     - Oral infections/inflammations, dysbiotic oral bacteria, and immune responses.
     - Coronary artery disease (CAD) diagnosis and severity.
     - Mortality over a 10-year follow-up.

3. **Study Population**:
   - Symptomatic Finnish patients (n=5,788) who underwent coronary angiography between June 2006 and March 2008.
   - Subsample (Parogene cohort): 508 patients invited for clinical and radiographic oral examinations.
   - CAC was technically registered in 492 patients (96.8%).

4. **Groups**:
   - Based on CAC severity: No CAC, Moderate CAC, Severe CAC.
   - Based on CAD diagnosis: No significant CAD, Chronic CAD, Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS).
   - Based on CAD severity: None/mild, 1 stenosed artery, 2 stenosed arteries, 3 stenosed arteries.

5. **Sample Size**:
   - Total: 492 patients with CAC data.
   - Subgroups: No CAC (n=390), Moderate CAC (n=81), Severe CAC (n=21).

6. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variables**:
     - CAC severity (No, Moderate, Severe).
     - Oral health parameters: periodontal pocket depth (PPD), bleeding on probing (BOP), alveolar bone loss (ABL), apical periodontitis severity, root canal fillings (RCF), subgingival bacterial levels, serum/saliva antibody levels.
     - CAD diagnosis and severity.
     - Demographics and comorbidities: age, gender, hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes, smoking.
   - **Dependent Variables**:
     - Prevalence of CAC.
     - CAD diagnosis and severity.
     - Mortality (all-cause and CVD-specific).

7. **Measurements**:
   - **Oral Health**:
     - Periodontal probing depths (PPD) and bleeding on probing (BOP) recorded as percentages.
     - Alveolar bone loss (ABL) graded (none, mild, moderate, severe/total).
     - Apical periodontitis severity scored (none, moderate, severe).
     - Root canal fillings (RCF) and untreated apical periodontitis recorded.
   - **Radiographic**:
     - CAC identified and graded on panoramic radiographs (none, moderate, severe).
   - **Microbiological and Serological**:
     - Subgingival bacterial levels (gram-positive/negative species).
     - Serum and saliva IgA/IgG antibody levels against dysbiotic species.
   - **Clinical and Demographic**:
     - Age, gender, BMI, hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes, smoking.

8. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Clinical oral examinations by calibrated periodontologists.
   - Radiographic analysis by a dental radiology specialist.
   - Subgingival bacterial sampling and checkerboard DNA-DNA hybridization assay.
   - Serum and saliva antibody analysis using ELISA.
   - Mortality data from the National Causes-of-Death Register.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Chi-Square Test**:
   - **Purpose**: Assess associations between categorical variables (e.g., CAC severity and CAD diagnosis).
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Both variables are categorical.
     - Data includes frequencies for subgroups.
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - Small cell sizes in contingency tables.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected frequencies in any cell are <5.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

2. **ANOVA (or Kruskal-Wallis Test)**:
   - **Purpose**: Compare means of continuous variables (e.g., age, PPD, antibody levels) across CAC severity groups.
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Continuous outcome variables.
     - Multiple groups (No CAC, Moderate CAC, Severe CAC).
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If data are not normally distributed or variances are unequal.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If normality or homogeneity of variance assumptions are violated.
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis Test (non-parametric).

3. **Cox Proportional Hazards Regression**:
   - **Purpose**: Analyze time-to-event data (e.g., survival over 10 years).
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Longitudinal follow-up data.
     - Adjusts for confounders (age, gender, hypertension, etc.).
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If proportional hazards assumption is violated.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If hazard ratios vary over time.
   - **Alternative**: Stratified Cox model or time-varying covariates.

4. **Logistic Regression**:
   - **Purpose**: Assess associations between CAC presence and risk factors (e.g., oral health, CAD severity).
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Binary or ordinal outcome (e.g., CAC severity).
     - Adjusts for confounders.
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If multicollinearity exists among predictors.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If outcome variable is not binary or ordinal.
   - **Alternative**: Multinomial logistic regression.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Spearman’s Rank Correlation**:
   - **Purpose**: Assess correlations between non-normally distributed variables (e.g., antibody levels and CAC severity).
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Non-parametric data.
     - Continuous or ordinal variables.
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If variables are not monotonic.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Pearson’s correlation.

2. **Multivariate Linear Regression**:
   - **Purpose**: Assess relationships between continuous outcomes (e.g., PPD, BOP) and multiple predictors.
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Continuous outcome variable.
     - Multiple predictors.
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If assumptions (normality, homoscedasticity) are violated.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If outcome is not continuous.
   - **Alternative**: Generalized linear models (GLMs).

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis**:
   - **Purpose**: Visualize survival differences across CAC severity groups.
   - **Appropriate**:
     - Time-to-event data.
     - Categorical grouping variable.
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If censoring is non-random.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If proportional hazards assumption is required.
   - **Alternative**: Cox regression.

2. **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)**:
   - **Purpose**: Reduce dimensionality of microbiological/serological data.
   - **Appropriate**:
     - High-dimensional data (e.g., bacterial species).
   - **Not Appropriate**:
     - If sample size is insufficient.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If interpretability is a priority.
   - **Alternative**: Factor analysis.
