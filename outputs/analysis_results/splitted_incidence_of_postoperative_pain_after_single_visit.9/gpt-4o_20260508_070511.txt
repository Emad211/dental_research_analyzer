### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: 
   - Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT)
   - Parallel group design

2. **Objective**:
   - Primary: Compare the prevalence of postobturation pain at 6-, 12-hour, 1-, and 2-day intervals after single-visit (SV) versus two-visit (MV) root canal therapy (RCT) on mandibular first molars.
   - Secondary: Compare tenderness to percussion after 1 week of treatment.

3. **Study Groups**:
   - Group 1: Single-visit RCT (SV group, n=35)
   - Group 2: Two-visit RCT (MV group, n=35)

4. **Sample Size**:
   - Initial estimation: 30 patients per group
   - Adjusted for dropouts: 35 patients per group
   - Total: 70 patients

5. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Patients consenting to SV or MV treatment
   - Moderately curved root canals

6. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Systemic diseases
   - Pregnancy
   - Teeth with calcified canals, unusual canal morphology, extra roots, or procedural errors
   - Teeth with periapical radiolucency >0.5 cm
   - Patients taking antibiotics/analgesics immediately after the first appointment

7. **Randomization**:
   - Random list generation to allocate patients into two groups (SV and MV).

8. **Intervention**:
   - **SV Group**: RCT completed in one visit, obturation with gutta-percha and Sealapex sealer.
   - **MV Group**: RCT completed in two visits, calcium hydroxide dressing placed as an interappointment medicament, obturation in the second visit using the same materials as SV.
   - Both groups received ibuprofen as rescue medication for severe pain.

9. **Outcome Variables**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Postoperative pain (measured using Heft-Parker Visual Analog Scale [HP VAS]) at 6-, 12-hour, 1-, and 2-day intervals.
   - **Secondary Outcome**: Tenderness to percussion after 1 week.

10. **Data Collection Methods**:
    - Pain levels recorded by patients on HP VAS forms at specified intervals.
    - Telephonic reminders to ensure compliance.
    - Tenderness to percussion evaluated clinically after 1 week.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-test
   - **Purpose**: Compare mean pain scores between SV and MV groups at each time interval (6-, 12-hour, 1-, and 2-day).
   - **Why Appropriate**: 
     - Continuous outcome variable (pain scores).
     - Two independent groups (SV vs. MV).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes normality of pain score distribution.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If pain score data are not normally distributed.
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test (nonparametric equivalent).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test
   - **Purpose**: Compare the proportion of patients experiencing tenderness to percussion between SV and MV groups after 1 week.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Categorical outcome variable (presence/absence of tenderness).
     - Two independent groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes expected cell counts ≥5.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If expected cell counts are too small.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA
   - **Purpose**: Assess changes in pain scores over time (6-, 12-hour, 1-, and 2-day) within and between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Longitudinal data (repeated measures of pain scores).
     - Allows for interaction testing (time × group).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes sphericity.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If sphericity assumption is violated.
   - **Alternative**: Linear mixed-effects model.

2. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis
   - **Purpose**: Assess time to resolution of pain (pain score = 0) between groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Time-to-event data (resolution of pain).
     - Accounts for censored data (e.g., patients lost to follow-up).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Assumes proportional hazards.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If proportional hazards assumption is violated.
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression
   - **Purpose**: Identify predictors of tenderness to percussion after 1 week.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Binary outcome variable (tenderness: yes/no).
     - Adjusts for potential confounders (e.g., age, gender).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires sufficient sample size for reliable estimates.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If sample size is too small.
   - **Alternative**: Exact Logistic Regression.

2. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis
   - **Purpose**: Explore differences in outcomes based on patient characteristics (e.g., gender, age).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Provides additional insights into effect modifiers.
     - Helps tailor clinical recommendations.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Risk of multiple comparisons leading to Type I error.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If subgroups are too small.
   - **Alternative**: Pool subgroups if sample size is insufficient.

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS

   - **Why Inappropriate**: Designed for comparing more than two groups, but this study has only two groups.

3. **Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test**:
   - **Why Inappropriate**: Used for paired data, not independent groups.
