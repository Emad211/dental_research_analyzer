### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**: Randomized clinical trial.
2. **Objective**: To evaluate whether positioning a resorbable collagen barrier at the apical third of root canals reduces the extrusion of obturating material in primary molars undergoing resorption.
3. **Groups**:
   - **Group A (Test)**: Canals with a resorbable collagen barrier placed at the apical third.
   - **Group B (Control)**: Canals without a collagen barrier.
4. **Sample Size**:
   - **Patients**: 47 children aged 7–9 years.
   - **Teeth**: 47 primary mandibular molars (94 canals; 47 canals in each group).
   - **Power Analysis**: 80%.
5. **Inclusion Criteria**:
   - Pulpally involved mandibular primary molars with at least two-thirds of root length.
   - Children aged 7–9 years with Frankel’s Class 3 or 4 behavior rating.
   - No systemic disease.
6. **Exclusion Criteria**:
   - Perforation of pulpal floor due to resorption.
   - Extreme tooth mobility.
   - Congenitally missing permanent tooth bud.
   - Radiographic evidence of internal/external resorption or radicular radiolucencies.
7. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variable**: Presence of resorbable collagen barrier.
   - **Dependent Variable**: Presence of extrusion of obturating material (binary outcome: Extruded [E] vs. No Extrusion [NE]).
8. **Measurements**:
   - Pre- and post-operative radiographs using radiovisiography (RVG) to assess extrusion.
   - Acceptable obturation defined as material 1–2 mm short of the apex; extrusion defined as material beyond the apex.
9. **Randomization**: Flip coin method performed by parents to assign canals to test or control groups.
10. **Data Collection Methods**:
    - Radiographic determination of working length using No. 15 k files.
    - Placement of collagen barrier (Coloplug) in test canals.
    - Obturation with calcium hydroxide and iodoform paste (Metapex) using syringe delivery system.
    - Radiographic assessment of extrusion by a single operator.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Chi-Square Test**:
   - **Purpose**: To compare the proportion of extrusion (E vs. NE) between Group A (test) and Group B (control).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - The dependent variable is categorical (binary: Extruded vs. No Extrusion).
     - The study involves two independent groups (test vs. control).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes independence of observations, which may be violated if canals within the same tooth are correlated.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is too small for expected frequencies in contingency tables.
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test (if sample size is small).

2. **Descriptive Statistics**:
   - **Purpose**: To summarize the frequency and percentage of extrusion in both groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Provides a clear understanding of the distribution of outcomes.
     - Essential for reporting baseline characteristics.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not inferential; cannot test hypotheses.
   - **When NOT to Use**: Not applicable (always useful).
   - **Alternative**: None.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **McNemar’s Test**:
   - **Purpose**: To account for paired data (mesial and distal canals within the same tooth) and test for differences in extrusion rates between test and control groups.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Accounts for the dependency between paired observations.
     - Suitable for binary outcomes in paired data.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires paired data; cannot be used if canals are analyzed independently.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If canals are treated as independent observations.
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE) for correlated binary data.

2. **Logistic Regression**:
   - **Purpose**: To model the probability of extrusion (binary outcome) as a function of group (test vs. control) and other covariates (e.g., canal location, patient age).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Allows adjustment for potential confounders.
     - Provides odds ratios for extrusion between groups.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Requires sufficient sample size for reliable estimates.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is too small or multicollinearity exists among covariates.
   - **Alternative**: Exact Logistic Regression (for small sample sizes).

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Cohen’s Kappa**:
   - **Purpose**: To assess inter-rater reliability if extrusion assessments are performed by multiple evaluators.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Ensures consistency in outcome classification.
     - Useful for validating radiographic assessments.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: Not relevant if a single operator assessed all radiographs.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If only one evaluator is involved.
   - **Alternative**: None.

2. **Subgroup Analysis**:
   - **Purpose**: To explore differences in extrusion rates within subgroups (e.g., mesial vs. distal canals).
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     - Provides additional insights into canal-specific effects.
     - Useful for hypothesis generation.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: May lead to overinterpretation if sample size is small.
   - **When NOT to Use**: If subgroup sizes are too small for reliable comparisons.
   - **Alternative**: Pool data across subgroups.
