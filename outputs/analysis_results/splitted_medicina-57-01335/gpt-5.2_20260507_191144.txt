# CATEGORY A – MANDATORY tests/models

## A1. Linear mixed-effects model (LMM) for pain score (continuous)
- **Purpose:** Compare mean pain between Dentapen and conventional while accounting for repeated observations within patients.
- **Why appropriate (≥2 reasons):**
  1. Handles **correlated outcomes** due to multiple procedures per patient via **random intercept (patient)**.
  2. Allows adjustment for **procedure type**, sex, age (and potentially injection site, if available) to improve precision and address case-mix imbalance.
- **Why it may not be appropriate (≥1 reason):**
  - Pain scales are often **bounded and ordinal**; residual normality/homoscedasticity may be poor, especially with floor effects (many zeros).
- **When NOT to use:**
  - When pain is clearly **ordinal with few categories** (e.g., 0–3), or distribution is extremely skewed/zero-inflated and transformation doesn’t help.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - **Ordinal mixed-effects model** (cumulative logit mixed model) if ordinal.
  - **Robust LMM** / **bootstrap CI** for group effect.
  - **Quantile mixed model** (median difference) if heavy skewness.

**Model specification (minimum)**
- Outcome: pain score per observation  
- Fixed effects: group (Dentapen vs conventional) + procedure type (+ age, sex; + bilateral indicator if relevant)  
- Random effects: patient-level random intercept  
- Report: adjusted mean difference, 95% CI, p-value; also standardized effect size if useful.

---

## A2. Generalized linear mixed model (GLMM) or ordinal mixed model (if pain is ordinal)
- **Purpose:** Compare pain distributions between groups when pain is ordinal (e.g., VAS categorized, Likert).
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Respects **ordinal nature** (doesn’t assume equal spacing).
  2. Accounts for **within-patient clustering** via random effects.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - **Proportional odds assumption** may be violated.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - When pain is truly continuous (0–100 VAS with near-continuous behavior) and LMM assumptions are acceptable/preferred.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - **Partial proportional odds mixed model** or **multinomial mixed model**.
  - Treat pain as continuous with robust SEs as sensitivity.

---

## A3. Baseline comparability / covariate balance checks (descriptive + standardized differences)
- **Test/metric:** **Standardized mean differences (SMDs)** (not p-values) for age/sex/procedure type by randomized group.
- **Purpose:** Document randomization success and characterize groups.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. SMDs are not driven by sample size like hypothesis tests.
  2. Works with clustered data description (at patient-level and observation-level).
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - If covariates are measured at the observation level with repeats, SMD calculation needs careful definition (patient-level summaries or cluster-adjusted).
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Do not use **significance testing** of baseline variables as a decision rule for modeling.
- **Alternative if issues:**
  - Present covariates by group with **cluster-aware summaries** (e.g., per-patient averages, or mixed-model-based descriptive estimates).

# CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED tests/models

## B1. GEE (Generalized Estimating Equations) for pain
- **Purpose:** Estimate **population-averaged** group difference accounting for clustering by patient.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Robust to some mis-specification of within-subject correlation (sandwich SE).
  2. Directly targets marginal/average effect, often relevant clinically.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - With **small number of clusters** GEE SEs can be biased; here 178 clusters is usually adequate.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If interest is primarily **subject-specific** effects or heterogeneity between patients (LMM/GLMM better).
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Mixed-effects models; or GEE with small-sample corrections (e.g., Mancl–DeRouen).

---

## B2. Interaction (effect modification) testing: group × procedure type
- **Purpose:** Determine whether Dentapen effect differs by **procedure category** (e.g., extraction vs filling vs endodontics vs surgery).
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Strong a priori rationale: pain differs by procedure and injection context.
  2. Prevents misleading “overall” effect if effects vary materially across strata.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Many procedure subtypes → sparse cells → unstable interaction estimates and multiplicity.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - When sample size within procedure strata is too small or categories are overly granular; then collapse categories.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Pre-specify broader procedure groups; use hierarchical/shrinkage approaches; present stratified estimates with CIs without formal interaction test.

---

## B3. Sensitivity analysis restricting to first observation per patient
- **Test/model:** Two-sample comparison on first procedure only (e.g., t-test / Mann–Whitney) + confirm with regression.
- **Purpose:** Check robustness to within-patient correlation and potential learning/fatigue effects.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Removes clustering entirely (one record per patient).
  2. Helps assess whether repeated interventions per patient drive findings.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Discards data → loss of power and representativeness.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - As primary analysis (wastes information).
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Keep all observations and use mixed models (primary).

---

## B4. Model diagnostics / assumption checks
- **Tools:** residual plots (LMM), Q–Q plots, heteroscedasticity checks; proportional odds check for ordinal models.
- **Purpose:** Validate chosen model and assumptions.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Pain outcomes often skewed; diagnostics prevent incorrect inference.
  2. Supports justification for transformations/alternative models.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Some diagnostics are less informative for small samples or discrete outcomes.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - Never “not use”; but avoid over-interpreting minor departures.
- **Alternative if issues found:**
  - Robust SEs, transformation, ordinal/GLMM, bootstrap.

# CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL tests/models (supplementary)

## C1. Nonparametric clustered comparison (cluster-level permutation / randomization test)
- **Purpose:** Provide inference aligned with randomization with minimal distributional assumptions.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Honors the **randomization** mechanism.
  2. Useful if pain distribution is highly non-normal and modeling is contentious.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - More complex with repeated measures and covariate adjustment; may be computationally heavy.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - If allocation was not strictly random at the observation level (e.g., some deterministic assignment), or if implementation details are unclear.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Bootstrap clustered CIs; robust mixed models.

- **Test/model:** Mixed model for pulse rate; correlation between pain and pulse (repeated-measures correlation).
- **Purpose:** Explore physiologic correlate of pain/anxiety and whether Dentapen influences it.
- **Why appropriate:**

  2. Can help interpret whether differences reflect anxiety vs nociception.

## C3. Multiple-comparisons adjustment (if many secondary outcomes/strata)
- **Method:** Holm or Benjamini–Hochberg (FDR) depending on objective.
- **Purpose:** Control false positives when examining multiple procedures/sites/outcomes.
- **Why appropriate:**
  1. Many dental procedure sub-analyses inflate Type I error.
  2. Improves credibility of subgroup findings.
- **Why it may not be appropriate:**
  - Can be overly conservative for a small set of clinically pre-specified comparisons.
- **When NOT to use:**
  - For a single pre-specified primary endpoint comparison.
- **Alternative if assumptions violated:**
  - Pre-specify limited hypotheses; emphasize effect sizes and CIs.

## TASK 3: Inappropriate tests (should NOT be used)

1. **Independent-samples t-test (or Mann–Whitney) on all 287 observations treating them as independent**
   - **Why inappropriate:** violates independence due to multiple observations per patient → inflated Type I error and overly narrow CIs.

2. **Multiple separate t-tests per procedure subtype without a model/adjustment**
   - **Why inappropriate:** multiplicity + sparse data in rare procedures → unstable estimates and false positives.

3. **Chi-square test comparing “pain present vs absent” without accounting for clustering**
   - **Why inappropriate:** repeated measures per patient; also dichotomizing pain loses information and power.

4. **Simple linear regression ignoring clustering**
   - **Why inappropriate:** same independence violation; SEs incorrect.

5. **Paired t-test** (as a default)
   - **Why inappropriate:** only valid for true paired/split-mouth within the same patient with both devices under comparable conditions; the study includes non-bilateral and multiple procedures, and not all patients contribute paired data.

Primary analysis should compare Dentapen vs conventional on **pain during injection** using a **mixed-effects model (or GEE)** that accounts for **within-patient clustering** from multiple anesthetic events per participant, with adjustment for major drivers of pain such as **procedure type** (and age/sex). This yields valid inference, uses all data efficiently, and supports clinically interpretable overall and procedure-specific (interaction/stratified) conclusions.
