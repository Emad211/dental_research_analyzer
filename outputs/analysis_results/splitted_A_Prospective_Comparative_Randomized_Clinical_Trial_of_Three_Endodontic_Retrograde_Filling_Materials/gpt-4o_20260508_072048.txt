### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Prospective, comparative, randomized clinical trial.

2. **Objective**:  
   - To determine the most effective retrograde filling material (EndoSequence, ProRoot MTA, or Biodentine) in promoting healing of periapical lesions following apical microsurgery.

3. **Study Groups**:  
   - Group 1: EndoSequence (n = 13 at 6 months).  
   - Group 2: ProRoot MTA (n = 14 at 6 months).  
   - Group 3: Biodentine (n = 8 at 6 months).

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Initial enrollment: 74 patients.  
   - Final analysis: 35 patients completed 6-month follow-up.

5. **Randomization**:  
   - Block randomization using a software-based algorithm (www.random.org).  
   - Allocation concealed from patients but not from surgeons due to material visibility.

6. **Blinding**:  
   - Patients and radiologists were blinded to the material used.  
   - Surgeons were not blinded due to the visible differences in materials.

7. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Adult patients (ASA classification I and II).  
   - Single chronic periapical lesion requiring apical microsurgery.  
   - Informed consent provided.

8. **Exclusion Criteria**:  
   - Drug/alcohol abuse.  
   - Adjacent periapical lesion.  
   - Periodontal disease.

9. **Variables**:  
   - **Primary Outcome**: Healing of the periapical lesion (measured by CBCT scans).  
   - **Independent Variable**: Type of retrograde filling material (EndoSequence, ProRoot MTA, Biodentine).  
   - **Covariates**: Time (2, 4, 6 months), patient demographics, lesion size.

10. **Measurements**:  
    - Radiolucent area size measured on CBCT scans (preoperative, postoperative, 2, 4, and 6 months).  
    - Subjective healing classification (radiolucent, some healing, moderate healing, good healing).

11. **Data Collection Methods**:  
    - CBCT scans acquired using standardized protocols (Accuitomo 170 machine).  
    - Measurements performed by a blinded, experienced radiologist using Invivo6 software.  
    - Intraobserver reliability assessed by repeating measurements.

12. **Statistical Tests Mentioned**:  
    - Kruskal-Wallis test for group comparisons.  
    - Mann-Whitney tests for pairwise comparisons.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY
1. **Test Name**: Kruskal-Wallis Test  
   - **Purpose**: Compare differences in healing outcomes among the three groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Non-parametric test suitable for small sample sizes.  
     2. Does not assume normal distribution of data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Sensitive to unequal group sizes (e.g., Biodentine group has fewer patients).  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are normally distributed and variances are equal.  
   - **Alternative**: One-way ANOVA if assumptions of normality and homogeneity of variance are met.

2. **Test Name**: Mann-Whitney U Test (Pairwise Comparisons)  
   - **Purpose**: Identify specific group differences after Kruskal-Wallis test.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Non-parametric test suitable for comparing two independent groups.  
     2. Handles ordinal or non-normally distributed data.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Inflated Type I error rate if multiple comparisons are conducted without correction.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If Kruskal-Wallis test is not significant.  
   - **Alternative**: Adjusted pairwise comparisons using Bonferroni correction.

3. **Test Name**: Intraobserver Reliability (e.g., Intraclass Correlation Coefficient [ICC])  
   - **Purpose**: Assess consistency of radiologist’s measurements.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Measures reliability for continuous data.  
     2. Accounts for repeated measurements by the same observer.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires at least two sets of measurements for each patient.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If only one measurement per patient is available.  
   - **Alternative**: Bland-Altman analysis for agreement.

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED
1. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis  
   - **Purpose**: Evaluate time to healing (e.g., achieving “good healing” classification).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Accounts for censored data (patients lost to follow-up).  
     2. Suitable for time-to-event outcomes.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires clear definition of “event” (e.g., good healing).  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If healing cannot be classified as an event.  
   - **Alternative**: Logistic regression for binary outcomes.

2. **Test Name**: Mixed-Effects Model  
   - **Purpose**: Analyze changes in radiolucent area size over time, accounting for repeated measures.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Handles longitudinal data with repeated measures.  
     2. Accounts for within-subject correlation.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Complex model requiring sufficient sample size.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data are not collected at consistent time points.  
   - **Alternative**: Repeated-measures ANOVA if assumptions are met.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL
1. **Test Name**: Effect Size Calculation (e.g., Cohen’s d, Eta-squared)  
   - **Purpose**: Quantify the magnitude of differences between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Provides practical significance of findings.  
     2. Complements p-values by indicating clinical relevance.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - May be misleading with small sample sizes.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If no significant differences are found.  
   - **Alternative**: Report confidence intervals for group differences.

2. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis  
   - **Purpose**: Explore differences in healing based on covariates (e.g., lesion size, age).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. Identifies potential effect modifiers.  
     2. Enhances understanding of treatment effects.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Risk of Type I error due to multiple comparisons.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is insufficient for subgroup analysis.  
   - **Alternative**: Include covariates in regression models.

---

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **One-Way ANOVA**:  
   - **Reason**: Assumes normality and equal variances, which may not hold for small, unequal group sizes.  
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test.

2. **Chi-Square Test**:  
   - **Reason**: Not suitable for continuous or ordinal data like radiolucent area size or healing classification.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test for ordinal data.

3. **Paired t-Test**:  
   - **Reason**: Designed for paired data, not comparisons between independent groups.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test for independent groups.
