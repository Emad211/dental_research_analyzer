### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Randomized controlled trial (RCT) with parallel groups.
   - Single-blinded (patients were blinded to the groups).

2. **Objective**:  
   - To compare the effect of NaOCl gel versus NaOCl solution on postoperative pain after root canal treatment.

3. **Groups**:  
   - Group 1: NaOCl solution group (control group).  
   - Group 2: NaOCl gel group (intervention group).

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Initial calculation: 18 patients per group (alpha = 0.05, power = 0.8, effect size = 0.84).  
   - Final sample size: 30 patients per group (to account for potential dropouts and increase statistical power).  
   - Total sample size: 60 patients.

5. **Participants**:  
   - Inclusion criteria:  
     - Healthy patients (ASA I classification).  
     - Molar teeth diagnosed with symptomatic apical periodontitis and symptomatic irreversible pulpitis.  
     - Preoperative pain >50 on a 10 cm visual analog scale (VAS).  
   - Exclusion criteria:  
     - Teeth with previous root canal treatment, root fracture, swelling, pathological mobility, or pocket depth >3 mm.  
     - Patients <18 or >65 years old.  
     - Patients who had taken antibiotics or analgesics within 1 month prior to the study.  
     - Teeth with radiographic lesions.

6. **Randomization**:  
   - Performed using a web-based randomization program (www.randomizer.org).

7. **Blinding**:  
   - Patients were blinded to the group allocation.

8. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variable**: Type of NaOCl used (solution vs. gel).  
   - **Dependent Variables**:  
     - Postoperative pain levels (measured at 24, 48, and 72 hours, and 1 week post-treatment using VAS).  
     - Analgesic consumption (binary outcome: yes/no).  
     - Unscheduled appointments (binary outcome: yes/no).

9. **Measurements**:  
   - Pain levels: 10 cm VAS (continuous variable).  
   - Analgesic consumption: Recorded in a questionnaire (binary variable).  
   - Unscheduled appointments: Recorded in a questionnaire (binary variable).

10. **Data Collection Methods**:  
    - Pain levels recorded by participants at 24, 48, and 72 hours, and 1 week post-treatment.  
    - Analgesic consumption and unscheduled appointments recorded in a questionnaire.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-test (or Mann-Whitney U test if assumptions are violated).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare mean postoperative pain levels (VAS scores) between the NaOCl solution and gel groups at each time point.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The study involves comparing two independent groups.  
     2. The dependent variable (VAS score) is continuous.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: If the VAS scores are not normally distributed.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If the sample size is too small to assume normality or if there are extreme outliers.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U test (non-parametric equivalent).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test if expected cell counts are <5).  
   - **Purpose**: To compare the proportion of patients who consumed analgesics or had unscheduled appointments between the two groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The dependent variables (analgesic consumption and unscheduled appointments) are categorical.  
     2. The groups are independent.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: If the sample size is too small for the chi-square test assumptions.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: When expected frequencies in any cell are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

3. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA (or Friedman Test if assumptions are violated).  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze changes in VAS scores over time (24, 48, 72 hours, and 1 week) within each group and compare the patterns between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     1. The study involves repeated measurements of the same variable (VAS) over time.  
     2. It allows for testing both within-group and between-group effects.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**: If the data violates the assumption of sphericity.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**: If the data is not normally distributed.  
   - **Alternative**: Friedman Test (non-parametric equivalent).
