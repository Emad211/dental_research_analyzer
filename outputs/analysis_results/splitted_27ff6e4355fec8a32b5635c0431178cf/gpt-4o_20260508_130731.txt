### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Randomized Controlled Double-Blinded Clinical Trial.

2. **Objective**:  
   - To evaluate and compare clinical periodontal parameters in patients with true combined endodontic-periodontal lesions (EPL) treated with gutta-percha (GP) and mineral trioxide aggregate (MTA) as obturation materials, with and without bone grafting.

3. **Groups**:  
   - **Group I**: Conventional non-surgical root canal treatment (RCT) with gutta-percha.  
   - **Group II**: Conventional non-surgical RCT with mineral trioxide aggregate.  
   - **Group III**: Conventional non-surgical RCT with gutta-percha + bone grafting.  
   - **Group IV**: Conventional non-surgical RCT with mineral trioxide aggregate + bone grafting.

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Total: 120 teeth (n = 120).  
   - Group size: 30 teeth per group (n = 30/group).

5. **Population**:  
   - Inclusion: Saudi patients aged 25–55 years with upper anterior non-vital single-rooted teeth diagnosed with true combined EPL.  
   - Exclusion: Systemic diseases affecting bone metabolism, oral cancer, long-term antibiotic use, medications modifying bone metabolism, fractures, resorptions, or associated pathological cysts.

6. **Variables**:  
   - **Independent Variables**: Treatment modality (GP vs. MTA; with vs. without bone grafting).  
   - **Dependent Variables**: Clinical parameters (probing depth, relative clinical attachment level, gingival recession, keratinized tissue width, gingival phenotype) and radiographic parameters (CBCTPAI scores).

7. **Measurements**:  
   - **Clinical**: Probing depth (PD), relative clinical attachment level (rCAL), gingival recession, keratinized tissue width (KTW), gingival phenotype (G.Ph.).  
   - **Radiographic**: CBCTPAI scores (Cone Beam Computed Tomography Periapical Index).

8. **Data Collection Methods**:  
   - Clinical measurements recorded at baseline, 3, 6, and 12 months post-treatment.  
   - Radiographic evaluation performed at baseline and 6 months using CBCT and standardized vertical periapical radiographs.

9. **Randomization**:  
   - Blocked randomization (block size = 3) using locked computer software (Minitab 1.5). Allocation concealed in closed envelopes.

10. **Blinding**:  
   - Double-blinded (blinded endodontist and periodontist).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA  
   - **Purpose**: To compare changes in clinical parameters (PD, rCAL, gingival recession, KTW, G.Ph.) across time points (baseline, 3, 6, and 12 months) and between groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Accounts for repeated measurements within subjects.  
     - Suitable for comparing multiple groups over time.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes normality and sphericity of data.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data violates sphericity or normality assumptions.  
   - **Alternative**: Linear Mixed Models (LMM) or Friedman Test (non-parametric).

2. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test  
   - **Purpose**: To compare success rates (CBCTPAI levels) between groups at 6 months and 12 months.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Suitable for categorical data (success/failure).  
     - Allows comparison between independent groups.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires sufficient sample size in each category for valid results.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If expected frequencies in any cell are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

3. **Test Name**: Independent Samples t-Test or Mann-Whitney U Test  
   - **Purpose**: To compare continuous variables (e.g., PD, rCAL) between two groups (e.g., GP vs. MTA).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - t-Test assumes normal distribution; Mann-Whitney U Test is non-parametric.  
     - Suitable for comparing two independent groups.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - t-Test is inappropriate for non-normal data or unequal variances.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data is non-normal or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative**: Mann-Whitney U Test (non-parametric).

---

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Linear Mixed Models (LMM)  
   - **Purpose**: To analyze longitudinal data accounting for repeated measures and random effects.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Handles missing data and complex covariance structures.  
     - Suitable for repeated measures with unequal intervals.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires expertise in model specification and interpretation.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If simpler methods (e.g., Repeated Measures ANOVA) suffice.  
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Estimating Equations (GEE).

2. **Test Name**: Kruskal-Wallis Test  
   - **Purpose**: To compare clinical parameters between all four groups when data is non-normal.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Non-parametric test for comparing multiple groups.  
     - Does not assume normality.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Less powerful than parametric tests.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data is normal and parametric tests are valid.  
   - **Alternative**: One-Way ANOVA.

---

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Pearson or Spearman Correlation  
   - **Purpose**: To assess relationships between clinical and radiographic parameters (e.g., PD vs. CBCTPAI scores).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Pearson for normal data; Spearman for non-normal data.  
     - Provides insight into variable associations.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Does not imply causation.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If data lacks variability or sample size is small.  
   - **Alternative**: Partial Correlation (adjusts for confounding).

2. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression  
   - **Purpose**: To predict success rates (CBCTPAI levels) based on treatment modality and baseline characteristics.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Suitable for binary outcomes (success/failure).  
     - Accounts for multiple predictors.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires large sample size for reliable estimates.  
   - **When NOT to Use**: If sample size is insufficient or predictors are highly correlated.  
   - **Alternative**: Bayesian Logistic Regression.

---

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS
1. **Paired t-Test**:  
   - **Reason**: Not suitable for comparing more than two groups or repeated measures.  
2. **One-Way ANOVA**:  
   - **Reason**: Does not account for repeated measures or longitudinal data.  
3. **Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test**:  
   - **Reason**: Designed for paired data, not for between-group comparisons.
