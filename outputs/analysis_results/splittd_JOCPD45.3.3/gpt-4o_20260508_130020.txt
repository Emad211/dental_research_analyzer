### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics:
1. **Study Design**:  
   - Randomized Clinical Trial (RCT)  
   - Double-blinded (participant and outcome assessor blinded; clinician not blinded due to material-specific manipulation requirements).

2. **Objective**:  
   - To evaluate the efficacy of Biodentine, Theracal, and Dycal as indirect pulp therapy (IPT) agents in young permanent teeth.

3. **Groups**:  
   - Group 1: Biodentine  
   - Group 2: Theracal  
   - Group 3: Dycal  

4. **Sample Size**:  
   - Initial sample size: 60 teeth (20 per group).  
   - Final sample size after follow-up loss: 54 teeth (18 per group).  

5. **Population**:  
   - Children aged 7–15 years with deep dentinal caries in permanent molars.  
   - Male-to-female ratio: 0.88:1.00.  
   - Mean age: 12.36 ± 2.29 years.  
   - Teeth distribution: 33.33% mandibular, 66.66% maxillary.

6. **Inclusion Criteria**:  
   - Permanent posterior teeth with deep dentinal caries (ICDAS 5 & 6) and open apices.  
   - Clinical symptoms: sensitivity to cold and pain on mastication.  
   - Radiographic criteria: >70% or 2/3 of dentin thickness affected without periapical pathology, external/internal root resorption, or pulp calcification.

7. **Sampling Technique**:  
   - Simple randomization using coded colored balls for material allocation.  
   - Randomization performed separately for each tooth if multiple teeth met inclusion criteria.

8. **Variables**:  
   - **Primary Outcome**: Clinical success (absence of postoperative pain, mobility, inflammation, sinus tract, or fistula).  
   - **Secondary Outcome**: Radiographic success (absence of resorption, PDL widening, periapical involvement, and evidence of root apex development).

9. **Measurements and Data Collection**:  
   - Clinical evaluations at 3 weeks, 3 months, 6 months, 12 months, 18 months, and 24 months.  
   - Radiographic evaluations using standardized paralleling technique.  
   - Inter-examiner agreement: Baseline (0.91) and final follow-up (0.94), with significant p-values (<0.05).

10. **Statistical Considerations**:  
    - Baseline agreement and follow-up agreement were statistically significant.  
    - Sample size calculation accounted for 10% dropout rate and aimed for 80% power with a 5% Type I error.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### **CATEGORY A – MANDATORY**
1. **Test Name**: Chi-Square Test or Fisher’s Exact Test  
   - **Purpose**: To compare the success rates (clinical and radiographic) across the three groups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Categorical outcome (success vs. failure).  
     - Comparing proportions between independent groups.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Small sample size in subgroups may violate assumptions of Chi-Square.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - When expected frequencies in contingency tables are <5.  
   - **Alternative**: Fisher’s Exact Test.

2. **Test Name**: Repeated Measures ANOVA or Mixed-Effects Model  
   - **Purpose**: To evaluate changes in clinical and radiographic outcomes over time (3 weeks, 3 months, 6 months, etc.).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Longitudinal data with repeated measures.  
     - Accounts for within-subject correlation.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes normality of residuals and homogeneity of variances.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If data are non-normal or variances are unequal.  
   - **Alternative**: Non-parametric alternatives like Friedman Test.

#### **CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED**
1. **Test Name**: Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis  
   - **Purpose**: To assess the time-to-failure (clinical or radiographic) for each material.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Accounts for censored data (e.g., dropout cases).  
     - Provides insights into durability of materials over time.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Assumes proportional hazards, which may not hold.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If follow-up intervals are irregular or data are sparse.  
   - **Alternative**: Cox Proportional Hazards Model.

2. **Test Name**: Inter-Rater Reliability (Kappa Statistic)  
   - **Purpose**: To validate the agreement between examiners for clinical and radiographic evaluations.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Measures consistency between raters.  
     - Accounts for chance agreement.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - If sample size is small or raters are inconsistent.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If data are ordinal rather than categorical.  
   - **Alternative**: Weighted Kappa for ordinal data.

#### **CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL**
1. **Test Name**: Logistic Regression  
   - **Purpose**: To identify predictors of success (e.g., material type, tooth location, age).  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Binary outcome (success vs. failure).  
     - Adjusts for confounding variables.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Requires large sample size for reliable estimates.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If sample size is insufficient or predictors are highly correlated.  
   - **Alternative**: Exact Logistic Regression.

2. **Test Name**: Subgroup Analysis (e.g., stratified by tooth location or age group).  
   - **Purpose**: To explore differential effects of materials in subgroups.  
   - **Why Appropriate**:  
     - Provides insights into heterogeneity of treatment effects.  
     - Useful for clinical decision-making.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:  
     - Risk of Type I error due to multiple comparisons.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:  
     - If subgroup sizes are too small.  
   - **Alternative**: Meta-regression (if applicable).
