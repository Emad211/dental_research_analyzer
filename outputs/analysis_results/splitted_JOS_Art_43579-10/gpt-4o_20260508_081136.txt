### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

**Study Design**  
- **Type:** Longitudinal study  
- **Setting:** Dental clinic at Universidad Peruana Cayetano Heredia, Lima, Perú  
- **Ethics Approval:** Approved by the Ethics Committee of Universidad Peruana Cayetano Heredia (file No. 215-10-19)

**Objective**  
- To measure and compare dental anxiety before and after endodontic microsurgery using the Visual Analogue Scale (VAS).

**Participants**  
- **Population:** Patients scheduled for endodontic microsurgery.  
- **Sample Size:** 35 participants (41 initially recruited; 6 excluded for incomplete scales).  
- **Sampling Method:** Non-probabilistic sampling.  
- **Inclusion Criteria:** Patients scheduled for endodontic microsurgery.  
- **Exclusion Criteria:** Patients with psychiatric or neurological diseases and those aged under 18.  
- **Recruitment Period:** Eight months.  

**Variables**  
- **Primary Variable:** Dental anxiety (quantitative, measured using VAS).  
- **Categorical Classification:** Anxiety levels classified using a cutoff score of 3 (based on a prior study).  

**Measurements**  
- **Tool:** Visual Analogue Scale (VAS, 0-10 cm).  
  - Scale ranges from "not anxious at all" to "worst experience ever."  
  - Anxiety score determined by measuring the distance (in cm) from "not anxious at all" to the patient’s mark.  
- **Time Points:**  
  - Pre-surgery: While waiting for the procedure in the clinic’s waiting room.  
  - Post-surgery: After the procedure, while waiting for the medical prescription.  

**Procedure**  
- Microsurgery performed by 2nd-year postgraduate endodontic students under supervision.  
- Duration of microsurgery: 2-3 hours on average.  
- The same researcher conducted all VAS assessments to ensure consistency.

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS  
1. **Paired t-test**  
   - **Purpose:** To compare mean anxiety levels before and after surgery.  
   - **Why Appropriate:**  
     1. The primary variable (VAS score) is continuous.  
     2. The study design involves repeated measures (pre- and post-surgery) on the same participants.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Assumes normal distribution of the differences in anxiety scores.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If the assumption of normality is violated.  
   - **Alternative:** Wilcoxon signed-rank test (non-parametric equivalent).  

2. **Descriptive Statistics**  
   - **Purpose:** To summarize the data (e.g., mean, standard deviation, median, range) for pre- and post-surgery anxiety scores.  
   - **Why Appropriate:**  
     1. Provides a clear overview of the data distribution.  
     2. Necessary for understanding the central tendency and variability of anxiety scores.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Not a hypothesis-testing method; does not infer statistical significance.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** N/A (always applicable).  
   - **Alternative:** None (descriptive statistics are fundamental).  

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS  
1. **Effect Size Calculation (e.g., Cohen’s d)**  
   - **Purpose:** To quantify the magnitude of the change in anxiety levels.  
   - **Why Appropriate:**  
     1. Complements the paired t-test by providing practical significance.  
     2. Useful for interpreting the clinical relevance of the findings.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Does not test statistical significance.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If the paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test is not performed.  
   - **Alternative:** Non-parametric effect size measures (e.g., rank-biserial correlation).  

2. **Shapiro-Wilk Test**  
   - **Purpose:** To test the normality of the differences in pre- and post-surgery anxiety scores.  
   - **Why Appropriate:**  
     1. Necessary to validate the assumptions of the paired t-test.  
     2. Ensures the correct choice of statistical test.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Sensitive to small sample sizes; may falsely indicate non-normality.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If the sample size is extremely small (e.g., <10).  
   - **Alternative:** Visual inspection of histograms or Q-Q plots.  

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS  
1. **Chi-Square Test (or Fisher’s Exact Test)**  
   - **Purpose:** To analyze the proportion of participants classified as anxious (score ≥3) before and after surgery.  
   - **Why Appropriate:**  
     1. Allows for categorical analysis of anxiety levels.  
     2. Provides supplementary insights into the proportion of patients experiencing anxiety.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Does not utilize the full range of the continuous VAS scores.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If the sample size is too small for reliable chi-square results.  
   - **Alternative:** Fisher’s exact test (for small sample sizes).  

2. **Repeated Measures ANOVA**  
   - **Purpose:** To analyze anxiety levels across multiple time points (if additional data points are collected).  
   - **Why Appropriate:**  
     1. Suitable for longitudinal data with repeated measures.  
     2. Can account for within-subject variability.  
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate:** Requires more than two time points; not applicable to the current dataset.  
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used:** If only pre- and post-surgery data are available.  
   - **Alternative:** Paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test.

### TASK 3: INAPPROPRIATE TESTS

1. **Independent t-test**  
   - **Why Not Appropriate:** The study involves paired data (pre- and post-surgery anxiety scores from the same participants), not independent groups.  
   - **Alternative:** Paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test.  

2. **One-Way ANOVA**  
   - **Why Not Appropriate:** ANOVA is designed for comparing means across three or more independent groups, which is not applicable here.  
   - **Alternative:** Paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test.  

3. **Pearson Correlation**  
   - **Why Not Appropriate:** Correlation measures the relationship between two variables, not the difference between pre- and post-surgery scores.  
   - **Alternative:** Paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank test.
