### TASK 1: INFORMATION EXTRACTION

#### Study Characteristics
1. **Study Design**: Experimental in vitro study.
2. **Objective**: To investigate the impact of core mass and alloy composition on the cyclic fatigue resistance of different nickel-titanium (NiTi) endodontic instruments in matching artificial canals.
3. **Groups**: Six groups of NiTi instruments based on design, taper, and alloy composition:
   - F360 (25/0.04)
   - Twisted File (25/0.04)
   - JIZAI_04 (25/0.04)
   - TruNatomy (26/0.04, variable taper)
   - Mtwo (25/0.06)
   - JIZAI_06 (25/0.06)
   - Hyflex OneFile (25/~, variable taper)
4. **Sample Size**: 20 instruments per group (total = 120 instruments).
5. **Variables**:
   - **Independent Variables**: Core mass, alloy composition, cross-sectional design, taper, and size of instruments.
   - **Dependent Variables**: Cyclic fatigue resistance (measured as number of cycles to fracture, NCF) and length of fractured fragments.
6. **Measurements**:
   - **Primary Outcome**: Number of cycles to fracture (NCF).
   - **Secondary Outcome**: Length of fractured fragments and fracture mode.
7. **Data Collection Methods**:
   - Instruments tested in matching artificial canals designed using Geomagic Freeform software.
   - Cyclic fatigue testing performed at body temperature (37 ± 1°C) using a standardized setup in an incubator.
   - Rotational speed and torque settings applied as per manufacturer specifications.
   - Fracture mode analyzed using a laser scanning microscope.
8. **Artificial Canal Design**:
   - Simulated canals matched to instrument dimensions (+0.02 mm tolerance).
   - 60° curvature, 5 mm radius, 16 mm length.
   - Manufactured using cobalt-based metal ceramic alloy via laser sintering.
9. **Statistical Power**:
   - Effect size: 0.4 (based on preliminary investigation).
   - Alpha level: 0.05.
   - Beta level: 0.20 (power = 80%).

### TASK 2: STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

#### CATEGORY A – MANDATORY TESTS
1. **Test Name**: One-Way ANOVA
   - **Purpose**: To compare the mean cyclic fatigue resistance (NCF) across the six groups of NiTi instruments.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. The study involves multiple independent groups (six instrument designs).
     2. The dependent variable (NCF) is continuous and assumed to follow a normal distribution.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Assumes homogeneity of variances, which may not hold across groups.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If the data violates the assumption of normality or homogeneity of variances.
   - **Alternative**: Kruskal-Wallis test (non-parametric equivalent).

2. **Test Name**: Post-Hoc Pairwise Comparisons (e.g., Tukey’s HSD)
   - **Purpose**: To identify specific differences between groups after a significant ANOVA result.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Necessary for understanding which groups differ significantly.
     2. Controls for Type I error in multiple comparisons.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires a significant ANOVA result as a prerequisite.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If ANOVA is not significant.
   - **Alternative**: Pairwise Mann-Whitney U tests (non-parametric).

3. **Test Name**: Descriptive Statistics
   - **Purpose**: To summarize the data (mean, standard deviation, range) for NCF and fragment lengths.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides a clear overview of the data distribution.
     2. Essential for contextualizing results.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Not inferential; does not test hypotheses.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - N/A (always useful).
   - **Alternative**: None.

#### CATEGORY B – RECOMMENDED TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Regression Analysis
   - **Purpose**: To explore the relationship between independent variables (core mass, alloy composition) and cyclic fatigue resistance.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Allows quantification of the impact of specific instrument characteristics.
     2. Useful for identifying predictors of cyclic fatigue resistance.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires linearity and independence of predictors.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If multicollinearity exists among predictors.
   - **Alternative**: Generalized Linear Models (GLMs).

2. **Test Name**: Levene’s Test for Homogeneity of Variances
   - **Purpose**: To test the assumption of equal variances across groups for ANOVA.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Ensures validity of ANOVA results.
     2. Identifies whether alternative tests are needed.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Sensitive to outliers.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If sample sizes are unequal across groups.
   - **Alternative**: Bartlett’s Test.

#### CATEGORY C – OPTIONAL TESTS
1. **Test Name**: Survival Analysis (Kaplan-Meier Curve)
   - **Purpose**: To analyze the time-to-fracture data as survival times.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Provides insight into the durability of instruments over time.
     2. Accounts for censored data (tests terminated at 600 seconds).
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires specialized software and expertise.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If all instruments fracture within the test duration.
   - **Alternative**: Log-rank test for comparing survival curves.

2. **Test Name**: Multivariate Analysis of Variance (MANOVA)
   - **Purpose**: To analyze multiple dependent variables (NCF and fragment length) simultaneously.
   - **Why Appropriate**:
     1. Accounts for potential correlations between dependent variables.
     2. Reduces Type I error compared to separate ANOVAs.
   - **Why It May Not Be Appropriate**:
     - Requires multivariate normality and homogeneity of covariance matrices.
   - **When This Test Should NOT Be Used**:
     - If assumptions are violated or sample size is insufficient.
   - **Alternative**: Separate ANOVAs.
