Outcome of Periapical Surgery in Endodontic and Oral Surgery

INTRODUCTION
Apical periodontitis (AP) or periapical periodontitis is an inflammatory lesion around the apex of a tooth root usually caused by invasion of microorganisms (Bacteria) in tooth pulp¹. Some dentists thought that periapical disease can be managed with root canal method because of its high success rate about 98%². But in cases in which root canal fails incidence of failure must be kept in mind before start of further management strategy. Causes of failure include resistant intracanal infection, coronal leakage, extra radicular infection,cyst,by residual intracanal infection and foreign body reaction³.

In the present study, we investigated the potential of using the in vitro and in vivo models to study the role of the <i>S. aureus</i> <i>agr</i> system in the pathogenesis of <i>S. aureus</i> endophthalmitis. We found that the <i>agr</i> system was highly expressed in the vitreous during the course of infection and that the <i>agr</i> mutant was significantly attenuated in its ability to cause intraocular inflammation and retinal damage. These results suggest that the <i>agr</i> system plays an important role in the pathogenesis of <i>S. aureus</i> endophthalmitis.

The <i>agr</i> system is a global regulator of virulence in <i>S. aureus</i>, controlling the expression of many virulence factors, including toxins, enzymes, and surface proteins. The <i>agr</i> system is activated by a quorum-sensing mechanism, which is triggered by the accumulation of a peptide pheromone called the autoinducing peptide (AIP). When the concentration of AIP reaches a certain threshold, it binds to the AgrC receptor, which then activates the AgrA response regulator. AgrA then activates the transcription of the <i>agr</i> operon, which leads to the production of more AIP and the expression of downstream virulence factors.

The <i>agr</i> system has been shown to be important for the pathogenesis of <i>S. aureus</i> in many different infection models, including skin and soft tissue infections, pneumonia, and endocarditis. However, its role in endophthalmitis has not been well-studied.

CONCLUSION
There is no significant difference regarding radiographic and clinical success and failure between periapical surgery in endodontic and oral surgery units. Quality of filling and filling material are two main contributing factors of periapical surgery.
