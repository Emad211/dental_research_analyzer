The LLLT and sham groups

Low-level laser therapy was administered by another
clinician (M. F.). In the LLLT group, laser irradiation
was performed using a 980-nm diode laser (Doctor
Smile; Wiser, Lambda, Italy) set at 6.89 W/cm² energy
density, 0.5 W power and a tip diameter of 10 mm for
15 s. The laser was irradiated to the soft tissues cover-
ing the mesial and distal apices of the tooth from the
buccal side with the tip of the laser handpiece held at
a distance of approximately 10 mm from the mucosa.
In the sham group, the laser handpiece was positioned
similar to that in the LLLT, group but the laser was not
activated.

Post-treatment pain

All post-treatment follow-up sessions were carried out
by the same clinician responsible for recording the pa-
tients’ demographic data (S.E.) under the supervision of
an endodontist (M. F.). After laser/sham treatment, each
patient was given a home questionnaire and a second ap-
pointment was scheduled for permanent tooth restoration
one week later. Each patient received 10 tablets of 500 mg
acetaminophen (AlborzDarouCo Production, Qazvin,
Iran) and were instructed to take them in case of pain. The
patients were instructed to record their postoperative pain
levels at 4, 8, 12 and 24 h and 2, 3 and 7 days after treat-
ment on separate VAS scales. The patients were contacted
by phone on specific hours and days to remind them to re-
cord their pain level. They were also asked to record con-
sumption of analgesic on the questionnaire (dosage and
exact time of taking the analgesic). If patients reported
taking an analgesic, they were excluded from the study
